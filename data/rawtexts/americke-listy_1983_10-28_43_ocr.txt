ŘÍJNA 1983 THE CITY OF NEW YORK OFFICE OF THE MAYOR New York, N. Y. 10007 Dear Friends: Today we celebrate the 65th anniversary of the founding of Czechoslovakia. October 28, 1918 marked the creation of an independent and democratic state under the leadership of the country’s preeminent statesman and philosopher, Thomas Garrigue Masaryk. Prior to World War II Czechoslovakia was the most democratic country in East Central Europe and one of the few countries to have a Western style constitution. Despite years of Nazi occupation and later. Communist domination, a new generation of Czechoslovak citizens continue to fight for the principles of freedom and democracy in Czechoslovakia. Hundreds of thousands of Czech and Slovak immigrants have come to the United States and settled in our great City. These immigrants built their own organizations, churches and schools as a way of maintaining their heritage in their new country. The achievements of this community in business, education and matters of cultural and civic importance have contributed significantly to the vitality of our City. Despite he freedoms granted to all citizens who live in the United States, we can not, and will not forget those who continue to live under foreign occupation and oppressive government rule.
