Dopis ČSNRA do Washingtonu August 8, 1985 On August 21st it will be seventeen years since the Soviet Union invaded Czechoslovakia, kidnapped members of her government and permanently occupied that country. What distinguished this particular aggression from other Soviet atrocities is the fact that it was directed not against an opponent, but against a communist ally only because the Czechoslovak people tried to eliminate brutality from their domestic policies and give socialism a “human face.” Why is this important? Because it is the clearest possible proof that the ruling elite of the Soviet Union does not care at all about the implementation of its professed ideals, but pursues a pure and simple policy of acquiring, enlarging and intesify- ing its absolute power over everyone — friends and foes alike. At a time when we are again engaged in an endeavor to arrive at some accommodation with this very same power elite, it is of utmost importance to remember and remind ouselves about its nature. To conduct any negotiations with the Soviet government while suffering from any illusions about its true aims is dangerous to us and usually fatal to some part of the free world. Therefore please let our adversary know that we are aware of his true character by recalling, on the floor of Congress, the unredeemed violation of Czechoslovakia on her anniversary on August 21st, and to insert your remarks in the Congressional Record. Not only the people of Czechoslovakia, but all peoples under Soviet domination will find hope and strength from your words.
