On November 17th, 1979, Sokol San Francisco celebrated its 75th anniversary with a banquet at our hall in San Mateo. The hall was filled to the capacity mainly with Sokol members, their families and friends. Saturday's banquet was a joyous affair. The guests were surprised to see the transformation from a gymnasium to a banquet hall. The entire floor area was filled with tables arranged for seating for 6 to 8 people with each table decorated with a floral arrangement, red, blue and white candles and the hall decorated perfectly for the occasion. A very fine 75th anniversary souvenier book was published for the occasion and was presented to each of the guests. It was well edited by the president, Br..George Spánek, containing the day's program, a short history of the unit and many fine ads and well wishers to Sokol San Francisco. This proves that Sokol's ideas has many supporters in the Bay Area. The delicious meal was prepared by our chef, Bro. Karel Caslava and served by his staff of youngSokol waitresses. Many thanks must be given to our sisters Sokolky and thier friends who worked so hard and generously for the success of this banquet. After the meal everyone relaxed, chatted with friends and shortly the program began. After the national anthem, Bros. President, George ápánek opened the program with the welcome of those present and introduced Bro. J. Kučera, who spoke in English. He remembered and thanked the old and present dedicated members for their determination to overcome all obstacles and challenged the members to increase the activity. Br. George Spánek in his szech address urged the members to be active, firm supporters of our unit and to take part in our programs and our Sokol ideals. Fl. Palas from Sokol Fresno. All of them wished a long and succesful future to our Sokol San Francisco. This was another mmorable occasion in the life of Sokol San Francisco. After a brief intermission, a very fine program was presented under the supervision of our very capable, Br. Eduard Ingris. The program opened with the ballet group of Mrs. Lilian Scott and they danced to the melodies of Smetana's "Vltava." The dancers were rewarded with a long lasting applause. Ed Ingris, Jr. gave us the taste of his piano masterful performance by playing several difficult compositions. Br. Eduard Ingris, Sr. had all audiences in his hands while everybody joined in a sing along with him of the famous and popular songs of Karel Hašler, whose 100th birthday and anniversary was remembered. Sokol San Francisco can surely be happy with the program and the entire celebration. It will be a source of happy memories for years to come. We too, shall remember and sincerely thank all of those who self sacrificing labors have kept the Sokol Spirit and the activity alive in the Bay Area. Memories of this event will linger on for a long time and I hope our followers will be here to repeat it again at our 100th year celebration. Seventy five years of dignified history is a record to be proud of and I am sure that many of you brothers and sisters have been a part of it. But, let's not tike a back seat and relax to that history. Ask ourselves, "What will our descendants of 25 years from now have to look back on. Will they be proud of the role that we have played in the Sokol cause?" Our most powerful weapon against dying is living - living with spirit. In this lies our fate and our destiny. . Nazdar! Joseph Málek wrote about his Sokol life story- on ,»American Sokol 'in 1954* : AUTOBIOGRAPHY — JOSEPH MALEK, SAN FRANCISCO, CALIF. Joseph Malek, Pres, of Pacific District Born March 14, 1915, in Puklice, Morava. Lived and went to school in Kněžice, Morava. Attended Sokol classes from the age of 6 to 9. At the age of 9, our family: brother, sister, father and mother, migrated to the U.S.A., Omaha, Nebraska. In Omaha I attended Comenius grammar school and joined Sokol Omaha. At Omaha Technical High School I majored in Electronics. In 1933, I was a member of Sokol Omaha team. We won second place in competition at the Century of Progress Slet in Chicago. After the Slet, Rudy Burda and I took a bicycle trip from Omaha to Winner, So. Dakota, then on through the Bad Lands and Black Hills and on to Cheyenne, Wyoming. Later, back to Omaha This was one of the outstanding vacations of my life. In 1938, I decided to make my home in California. After an extended trip through the Northwest, I arrived in colorful San Francisco. After getting situated, I looked up the address of San Francisco Sokol Hall. A visit to 739 Page St , encouraged me to come again and again. Soon I joined the men s class and became a student of Luděk Jirsák and Frank Víšek, two great Sokols. In 1939, I won first place in the mens division in the Pacific District závody during the Treasure Island Fair. In 1940, I repeated this victory at Fresno, Calif. 1 began taking Sokol work very seriously for I felt that the idea and principles were the greatest ever. Later 1 was elected náčelník, with which title also meant that 1 had to conduct classes — this I learned from Luděk Jirsák, who had great patience with me. In December 1941, being a member of the Naval Reserve, 1 reported for duty. 1 boarded the battleship U.S.S. Pennsylvania as a radar technician. During my stay of 3p2 years on this fine ship, 1 took part in the following engagements: Invasion of Attu, Kiska, Gilbert Islands, Marshall Islands, Carolines, Marianas, Philhpines and Okinawa. The naval battle of Surigao Straits in the Phillipines was the greats est thrill of my life. The Japanese fleet was annihilated. Our “Pennsy” finally got hit at Okinawa. An aerial torpedo tore a hole 30 by 60 feet in her stern on the starboard side. Twenty men were killed. My compartment was just 5 inches of steel above this place. My shoulder was peppered with schrapnel but 1 managed to get out before my room filled with sea water. Two days later, the Japs surrendered. I feel that my Naval career as radar officer of the USS Pennsylvania was the most exciting part of my life. Among several awards I received were the Meritorious Achievement and the Purple Heart medal. 1 might add that during our stay in the war zones, I had the opportunity to meet many Sokols from al) over the U.S.A., all doing their bit for our great country. After the war, I returned back to San Francisco and my wonderful wife. Back to Sokol work with even greater enthusiasm. First, náčelník of Sokol San Francisco, then náčelník of 'Pacific župa, starosta of Sokol San Francisco, starosta of Pacific župa. My favorite position is children s class instructor. Now, I am very active in making a dream come true. A new Sokol building' in a new district with all modern facilit’es This project is to be self-supporting. The building will house sixteen bowling alleys, gymnasiumauditorium, 3 clubroůms, kitchen, 4 room manager's apartment and a swimming pool. The membership is very enthused. I believe that the year of 1955 will find us in our new Sokol Home with bigger and a more enthusiastic Sokol membership! [pageend3]Page 4 Věstník Sokola San Francisco November 1979 VLADIMÍR ŠKUTINA V SAN FRANCISKU Na pozvaní Ustredi Československé Národní Rady v Americe, přijel na americky kontinent pan VLADIMÍR ŠKUTINA, televizní reportér, publicista, humorista, spisovatel, vypravěč a "Presidentův Vezeh", jehož si většina "osmašedesátníků" pamatuje hlavně ze dnů okupace z československé televize a na jeho heslo: Jsme s vámi, budte s namij Z měsíčního pobytu v USA a v Kanadě věnoval naší oblasti 4 dny, bohaté programem pro naše krajany i veřejnost. Hlavním bodem jeho návštěvy byla beseda, uspořádaná v Sokolovně v San'Mateo 13. listopadu, již se účastnilo přes sto krajanů, kteří se sjeli i ve vsedni den zblízka i z dalekého okolí, až ze Stockton, San Jose a z Marin County, aby si poslechli návštěvníkovo "Skutinování", satirickou kritiku poměrů v Československu, plnou anekdot na situace, které mohou vzniknout jen za podobného režimu, jaký existuje v Československu hlavně po okupaci republiky sovětskými tanky od srpna 1968. Úspěch byl ohromný a posluchaěstvo se nerado rozcházelo dávno po 11 hodině večer. Besedě předcházela návštěva Stanfordské University, kde Vladimír Škutina navštívil Oddělení pro východní Evropu. Druhý den následovala návštěva kanceláře Amnesty International v San Francisku. Škutina doporučil, aby Amnesty International, stejně jako i veřejnost, pokračovala v zasílání dopisů vládě Československa, vyžadujících zmírnění trestů pro odsouzené Chartisty a zlepšení v zacházení s vezni, hlavně politickými. Následoval interview v Press Club se San Francisco Chronicle a večer na další besedě interview s Peninsula Times Tribune. Oba články s fotografiemi Vladimíra Škutiny byly uveřejněny 15. listopadu. Obsahem Článků byl stručný životopis Skutinův, jeho činnost před uvězněním, léta ve vězení za politickou činnost a výzva k vytváření tlaku na vládu CSR o zmírnění pronásledování Chartistů. Další beseda s členy Amnesty International, kteří se sešli, aby se dotázali na výsledky činnosti Amnesty International v praxi. Činnost této organisace je potřebná a podle V. Škutiny, pomáhá jako největší zdroj tlaku pro zmírnění trestů, nejen v ČSR ale i jinde ve světě, kde jsou lidé vězněni z politických důvodů. Ještě další beseda s nejmladšími krajany v oblasti a cas již vypršel k odjezdu; v pátek ráno, dále do Portlandu. Mezi jednotlivými akcemi jsme Vladimírovi ukázali město a okolí podle našich možností, pobřeží Pacifiku, mČsto ve dne i v noci,s osvětlenými mosty a ostrovy, bohužel viditelnost byla v těchto dnech dost špatná. I tak doufáme, že se tu Vladimírovi líbilo a že si San Francisko zamiloval.- Snad se zas někdy přijede podívat.
