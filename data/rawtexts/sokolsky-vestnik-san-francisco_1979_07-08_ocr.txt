It has been many months since we have been able to welcome our Marin County and Alameda County members. We hope that they will make it a habit to attend future meetings. In the correspondence we received a '.’Thank You” from the trustees for the Czech Chapel in Chestnut, Pa. for our donation of #50.00. Financial Secretary, Sister Bele, reported that dues are coming in. We accepted with regret the resignation of Loy Kolar. Sad to say, the May dance was a real disaster. The net profit was just $100.00. Singer Jaroslav Hutka presented a program on June 1. Since there was not time to really publicize the event, we were satisfied with the attendance. Total income was $377.75, profit I18I4..25. Those attending really enjoyed it. Brother Kryska reported on the Los Angeles Commemorative event. All were pleased at the participation from the San Francisco Boys. The money given for expenses was well-spent. The subject of a unit flag was brought up. Since we do not have one, various ideas were presented— possibly a contest to design one. Further details will be in this bulletin. Sokol Sydney has sent us a beautiful ribbon. It was decided to have the Sokol picnic at Coyote Point on August 26. We can reserve space here. Admission will be $2.00 per car. Each group will bring its own food and beverage. Those who come by eleven will have the better parking spaces as the place does become crowded with cars. The 75th anniversary of San Francisco Sokol and of the Pacific District will be observed on November 15. Br. Kryska will have a small gymnastic exhibition. Br. Kučera suggests a banquet for members and their friends. Some will come from Los Angeles and this can be combined with a Zupa meeting. Br. Denis Spánek presented his case the use of the passage through the back yard. He read the contract whith was in accordance with the request of the Board of Directors from their March 29 meeting. Br. Kučera spoke on maintaining a reasonable attitude toward the matter. Sister štulir asked to have the issue clarified. There were many opinions. Br. Rozehnal feels strongly that the entire membership should be polled. Br. Kryska moved that the contract be accepted seconded by Sister Bele. The motion carried 16 for, 1 against. Br. Duffey has secured three estimates on refinishing the Hall floor. Prices vary from $1614.9 to $1075* Br. Duffey explained that the floor is in urgent need of attention. All bids are from reliable firms. Br. Duffey was thanked for his good work. A committee was appointed to work on the 75th Anniversary. Meeting adjourned at 10:30 P.M. El. Jozifek [pageend2]July-August 1979 Věstník Sokola San Francisco Page 3 Sokol Fresno Even though Fresno Sokol does not have an active gymnastic group, they have some great social times. The annual picnic of Sokol Fresno was held June 10 at the home of Valerie and Joe McCown. This was a special picnic because the McCown home is set amidst tall pines at Meadow Lakes. They generously let us bring all our picnic paraphernalia, so we sprawled all over their patio porch, house, and the whole yard. Andy Yakovetic drove up with a humongous barbecue pit, vih ich he made himself, just in time for this affair. The ladies brought l . salads, desserts, dips, chips, etc. There were plenty of drinks, too. The men had the honor of barbecuing our meats and otherwise managing the picnic. This tras tremendously enjoyed by the ladles-- as all they did was to throw on the paper plates and the plastic sterling. Everyone ate heartily vři lie beautiful Czech music played from a nearby speaker. We had special guests, too. Chuck and Florence Palas and their granddaughter came all the way from Los Angeles, gas shortage or not-- they made it. Many of us had not seen them for a number of years. It was a great reunion. The men played horse shoes and some of the children swam in the lake. We all had a very good time and want to thank Vai and Joe for their lovely hospitality, and also Andy for the huge barbecue/ There were enough coals left glowing to nave barbecued a whole steer, but everyone was too full to go look for one. Oh well, there’s always next year. Bude tomu v srpnu již 22 let když jsem přijel do San Francisca ze Sydney, Australia, Tam jsem přirozeně byl členem sydneyského Sokola a a samozřejmě mé první otázky se týkaly informaci o Sokole v mém novém bydlišti. - Do San Francisca jsem přijel v době prázdnin a tak jsem čekal až bude zahájeno cvičeni, - Dnes již zesnulý Tony Vacek, který bydlel v sousedním apartementu nás všechny naložil do svého vozu a odvezl do Sokola na Page Street, kde nás již jako nová členy představoval.
