GORMAN: WHERE DO WE STAND TODAY? The other day David Sarnoff, President of Radio Corporation of America addressing an American Legion Convention in Philadelphia, voiced the most realistic evaluation of current world trends heard in years. Ko fancy phrases . . no hollow wishful hoping . . no diplomatic hog-wash trying to cover up a foul smelling situation. Sarnoff called a spade a spade and voiced no false illusions about Russia’s change of aims or policies . . Quoting names, citing statistics, drawing hard, irrefutable conclusions, Mr. Sarnoff pointed out the danger that confronts the free world and called for a quick reassesment of our attitudes and a continued "alert" of all our forces. "Russia’s aims have not changed", was the gist of hr. Sarnoff's remarks. "Only her methods and some of the front line personnel are changed. The former nine men Politburo has been replaced by the Presidium of eleven men . . . yet seven of these are the same men who have for years guided the Politburo . . while the other two, Stalin and Beria have been liquidated and replaced by other fanatics.
