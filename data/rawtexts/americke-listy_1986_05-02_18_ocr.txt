For a few days, the world was shocked but all too quickly switched its attention to other problems, of which there was an abundance that year. The author, a Czech-American, was deeply troubled by this unprovoked act of aggression and some years later decided to record his own recollections, starting with the first invasion of his native country in March 1939 when other tanks, flying the nazi swastika that time, stormed into Prague and put an abrupt end to national life as he knew it — a 19-year old student and musician. Less than a decade later, having returned to Czechoslovakia with the U. S.
