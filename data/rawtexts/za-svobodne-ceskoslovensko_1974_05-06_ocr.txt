Dear Mr. President, Before your visit to the Soviet Union, Americans of Czechoslovak descent, in California, wish to bring to your attention the following facts: Almost six years have passed since Czechoslovakia was brutally Invaded by the Soviet army. Many defenceless people were killed and enormous losses caused In the economy. Cultural life was entirely paralyzed, scientists and artists are not allowed to work In their professions, authors are not permitted to Dubllsh their works. The prisons were filled, fear ruled the country. There are still sixty thousand Soviet soldiers in Czechoslovakia. This Illegal, criminal occupation, represents the most ruthless ignorance of the International Laws, the United Nations Charter and of the human rights. We appeal to you to bring up these facts during your official visit. We appeal to you further to include the case of Czechoslovakia In all your future negotiations with the Soviet Union to prove that the United States cannot and will not endure the interference In the sovereignity of other nations. Only by such a strong stand and firm policy can the United States give the enslaved nations the badly needed faith in the alliance of all democratic oriented people and countries. We appeal to you also to clear up the situation regarding tourism and cultural exchange, and to request the full reciprocity In both. While tourists from communist controlled countries are enjoying the hospitality In this country, the American tourists are limited In their travel; they are subjects to a degrading search; forced to exchange disproportionate amounts of their currency; their books and publications are confiscated. After many years of experience with communism and with the situation In the United States,we firmly believe that only by an open and firm demand to respect the International Laws,Unlred Nations Charter,and the Declaration of Human Rights, can you again,Mr.President, show the American people where this democracy standB and what human policy It follows. We want to assure you, Mr. President, of our support in your defence of the Democracy and the American freedom. // Miroslav Konvička, !/ Secretary. Vaclav jS'alak, President.
