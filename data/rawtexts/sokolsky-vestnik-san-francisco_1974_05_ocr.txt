EVERYBODY LOVES A PARADE The week-end immediately following our Exhibition we entered Sokol in the "Burlingame Days" Parade. We had a rather busy time as our entry was a last minutes decision since we weren’t sure we’d have time after the Exhibition to pull things together. Yarn, old shirts, pipe cleaners, plastic flowers, spools, wire, rice various macaronis, lentils, beans, split peas, small juice cans, corks, colored feathers, material scraps, egg cartons, shirt cardboards, stocking boxes (lids), old fashioned clothespins (without wires), toothpicks, straws, empty paper rolls. However, we managed by decorating a truck with Czechoslovak flags, red and white streamers, and signs reading "Sokol Gymnasts". About 12 children participated in the parade doing their tumbling at the side or in back of the truck as we slowly moved along. F, Sokol Věstník June, 197h Page 5 PACIFIC DISTRICT GYMNASTIC EXHIBITION 1974 When the delegatee of our Sokol Unit accepted the decision at last year’s Annual District Meeting, that the Pacific District Exhibition be held in San Francisco, we all r ealized that it would be a great challenge, a big responsibility, lots of worries, and surely a lot of hard work. We also realized that this was a great opportunity for our Unit and that sacrifices must be made in order to rejuvinate the activities and the life of our Sokol. After many years, we would be able to show not only the Czechoslovak community, but also the American public what is behind the Sokol idea and how we are working in the field of physical fitness, what is the idea forcing generation after generation to keep this national organization to not only survive, but under difficult situations to grow event bigger. Today we are all assured that we did not misjudge our expectations. The Sokol organization needs to be active, it must bring new ideas, sound programs, and right direction to our younger generation not only for their benefit, but for the good of the whole nation. When the news of the exhibition spread, the increased activities and preparations started early in the gym classes. Sister and Brother Meier, our Nacelnik and Nacelnice, had their hands full with the increased number of children in our classes, probably the biggest in San Francisco Sokol history. Very soon other committees got under full steam and we were pleased with the reports of progress made on the Souvenir Program for San Francisco Sokol’s 70th Anniversary by Br. Starosta George Spánek, Sir. Finally, May 25, 197b - the day of the Pacific District Exhibition was here and with it all the excitement and expectations. The first bus load from Los Angeles arrived early in the morning and immediately Br. Louzensky, our Quartermaster and sister, taking care of so many hungry stomachs, got busy. In a very short time, everyone was happy and soon all were in the Burlingame High School Gymnasium conveniently located only a short distance from our Sokol Hall. Competition started at 11 am. Over 60 Los Angeles and San Francisco children and juniors were competing. All judges and participants were doing their jobs and Competition under the direction of Zupni Nacelnice, Sister Meier was very well organized and prepared. Those of us who know how much work it takes to put together this kind of competition can appreciate what was done. We were extremely happy to see so many children in Sokol uniforms, but more than that, they were the children of Sokol San Francisco and they are our future? they are our hopel The progress of our children and juniors was OUR pleasant surprise. In such a short time to be ready for competition and to win the top prizes is truly a great achievement. But enough about this, as we are sure that Sister and Brother Meier will have another article in this Věstník where they will present the results. One more big THANK YOU to our instructors, Jan Clark and Ernie Crueger for the tremendous jobs they have done with our children. After the competition was over the traditional Volley Ball games between San Francisco and Los Angeles men and women teams was played in the girls gymnasium. Sokol San Francisco’s Women Team was winner 3:0 after a very exciting, closely matched game. In the men’s division, LoS Angeles won 3:0 beating San Francisco rather easily, Los Angeles has a very good team and if they continue to play together, they will be hard to beat in many years to come. prom the gymnas lum, we returned to Sokol Hall where our Sisters were already very busy, The beginning of our Banquet had just started with over 100 dinners to be served before the program was t o start. Sister Marta Hermanova put her kitchen team to work and everybody was running just like in the morning competition, and I mean really running. [pageend5]S. F. All we can do is thank you girls - oops - ladies again. Very well doneIII Shortly after 9 pm, Br. Louzensky introduced our distinguished guests; Br. Stanley Barcal, President of ASO, Br. Vaclav Salak, President of Pacific District Sokol, Dr. V Pechacek, Director of Radio Free Europe in Munich. President George Spánek welcomed all guests and in his speech outlined the history of Sokol San Francisco - 70 years of its founding. The artistic program under the direction of famous composer Eduard Ingris followed the opening ceremonies. Mrs. Nina Ingris sang some of the songs from national folk lore and some of the classical arias. Young and talented Edousek Ingris showed us his piano skills and Br. Jan Stibor and his students presented a two flutes medley from famous Czechoslovak operas. Young Suzie Bouman and her brother had a very effective acrobatic nunber. A very nice program, indeedj Next day, Sunday May 26, 1974 found us once again in the Burlingame High School Gym. The program opened with the presentation of colors while all participating gymnasts were marching in to the hall. Br. Louzensky, our Master of Ceremonies, introduced, once again some of our distinguished guests: Br. Stanley Barcal, Br, V. Salak brought greetings from ASO Chicago and the Pacific District. Mr. Wm. J. Crosby, Mayor of Burlingame, in his short speech expressed his happiness in having such an organization like Sokol in his neighborhood and many fine members of Sokol in his city. In the program following, we could truly observe the wide base of the Sokol activities, The Oyd Guard, Stars Garda, from Los Angeles showed the same young spirit and enthusiasm in their calisthenics as the juniors in their tunbling. Czechoslovak national costumes were well represented by Los Angeles Folk Dancers and by the San Francisco Beseda group. Sister Dagmar Hintnaus, member of the U. S. Olympic Team put on a dazzling show of gymnastic exhibitions on the balance beam, uneven parallel bars and floor exercises, and Los Angeles and San Francisco youngsters performed their special numbers. The Victory Dance held Sunday evening was attended by many gymnasts patiently waiting for the results of competition and hopeful for a trophy. We wish that everyone could be a winner, but it is not possible. Maybe a few young warriors were disappointed, but they did not show it openly. There were certainly more happy ones and all of us could hear their friends loud approval and support. The older generation enjoyed the happiness of our young gymnasts. During the presentation of awards, Br. and Sister Meier were presented with certificates for outstanding achievement from ASO by Br. Barcal, and our youngest gymnast, Marla Maier, was presented with a Special Award for her participation in competition. What else is there to say about these two days of Sokols gathering together? We are sure that the children had a wnderful time; that new friendships which were started on this occasion will last for many years to come - just as ours started many years ago. Another asset of this event is the fact that new and able, good workers, mainly parents of Sokol children helped us make this week-end so successful. This is the right way to go, and we should keep working in this direction. It would be a long list of names were we to thank everyone individually who helped from the beginning to the end of this happy event. Everyone who works for the good of Sokol should be rewarded for his effort, and the reward is the satisfaction felt in his heart not in his pocket. If anyone expects more, he will be disappointed. Thanks to all of you wonderful brothers and sisters who helped in any of the many functions. Thanks to all the children and juniors who conducted themselves like true Sokols. We are proud of you!JI Nazdar! Jan Kucera [pageend6]S* F. Sokol Věstník June, 1974 Page 7 Pokračováni : Pohled do minulosti Sokola San Francisco.
