The December number of 'Sklizeň’ completes the fifth year of our production. The number contains four poems from two authors who still live in Czechoslovakia and have managed to smuggle their verses out. Contacts with the homeland are very difficult but they have never been quite broken. It is easily imaginable that there are many works written behind the Iron Curtain which have no hope of ever being published there because they offend against the prevailing system. The real writer cannot suppress his urge to write and quite naturally, too,he wants a reading public: and so we receive poems and other scripts brought out by visitors or slipped between the pages of letters. Poems such as we publish in this number put us m a better position to judge the effect of Communist development on the soul of the peoples who live under its yoke. The names we have given are, of course, pseudonyms and one of them, A.D.Martin, is already known to our readers,Vladimír Hajek is a new appearance. Věra Stárková has written us a searching analysis of„the soul of the people as seen in a drama of Paul Claudel. There follows the a picture from the homeland — St. Nicholas Church in Prague in winter and, on page 6, there are other photographs of the celebration of our Independence Day on October 28 in the Czechoslovak Exile School. Dr.Div.J.Jokr describes for us the ancient history of Palestine on the basis of the Gospels.B.Stefan-Mastný (USA) writes on page 8 a critique of philosophical works by Lewis Mumford and Swami Virajananda. Dr.Jaroslav Jira,(France) in reviewing a new book of Vladimír Zelený also touches on the histoiy of the Czechoslovak Colony in Paris. On page 11 Jindřich Strada. (Austria) gives the views of a historian on old Bohemian stat; law m discussing a work by Jan M.Kolár. On page 15 there is a^short description by P.Růžička, (Ireland) of a new book by Rev.Přemysl Fitter in which he publishes his religious talks on" RFE and the BBC. There are then news items from the Cultural Council in Exile and the exile school. On the following pages there are more pictures of the Independence Day Celebrations, and also in picture form, we show' some shots of the Czechoslovak display at the Royal Adelaide Exhibition in Australia this year: and lastly the machinery with which your editor is constantly trying to produce betier pictures and improve the whole publication. On the last page there is a summary of the contents of the 1957 issues.
