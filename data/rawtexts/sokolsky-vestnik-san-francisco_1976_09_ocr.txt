A RESPONSE TO THE S. 0. S. OF THE SAN FRAiCISCO SOKOL The signal S. 0. S. of Sokol San Francisco in the June bulletin was strong enough to reach Brazil. This call of distress is also coining from other units. That is why it is so important. We have some Sokol Units which are not sending this S. 0. S. signal. Why? Because the officers and all leaders understand that in the Sokol Program we need to return to Tyrs’ ideal of complete involvement of body and mind— mental, moral, and physical development. The Sokol Gymnastic Program should be accessible to everyone, not just to prospective Olympic Champions. Sometimes the handicapped gymnasts feel very sad because they cannot accomplish the difficult exercises. They become observers instead of participants, weakening rather than strengthening themselves. They do not have the pleasure or satisfaction in accomplishment according to ability that a capable instructor could give. Each of us is handicapped by the mechanical aids which surround us. We drive or ride in car, polluting the air and depriving our bodies of exercise. We ride the elevator or escalator instead of walking up the stairs. You should take advantage of your beautiful California sunshine— swim and walk in nature. Give the youth what they deserve! Give them opportunity! The spirit of brotherhood should prevail in the Sokol Gymnastic activities. Older leaders should encourage ar.d advise younger members to become the leaders of tomorrow. Prospective leaders should refer to the technical books in the library. The spirit and whole atmosphere in your Sokol Hall should be saturated with the Sokol friendship and Sokol ideals. Orly in these ways can you attract young people. Don't stop exercising when you leave the class. Continue to exercise to an advanced age. This is your best life insurance. Take advantage of the opportunities in your beautiful Sokol Hall, where you have enough gym equipment and which is near to you. This is my response to your S. 0. S. I am writing these lines on the eighth of August, on the ninetieth anniversary of the tragic death of Dr. Miroslsv Tyrs^ who taught us that only in a healthy body can we have a sound mind in our long life. Nazdar Yours, Marta Herman [pageend1]Page 2. Věstník Sokola San Francisco September 1^76 - ZURICH SLET - I heard the mighty accords of Suf’s march "V Novy Zivot" at Letzigrund Stadion in Zurich announcing the opening of IV Sokol Slet sponsored by the Czechoslovaks Sokols.Abroad. Most of the people are not able to recognize the importance of the day we are just going thru. We need the time to evaluate whether the time just passed was important or if it was just one more ordinary day. Now, after so many weeks of looking back on the Zurich Slet, I realize what a wonderful event in the history of Sokol I have witnessed. This was the fourth Slet conducted by the Czechoslovak Sokols Abroad. The first one was in Vienna, Commemorating the 100th anniversary of Sokol, in 1962. The second Slet was in Canada in I967 when the World Fair was held there. The third was again in Vienna..in 1972. Many older Sokols, remembering the Slets before the Second World War know that these Slets cannot be compared with Slets in Prague. So why are we conducting these slets outside of Czechslovakia? Of course we cannot match the numbers of Prague’s Slets, but the purpose is the same. One of the aims of Tyrs and Fugner was to unify the nation, to bring all people together so they would join what is close to everybody - interest in one’s nation and its freedom and democracy. Slets are bringing people together from all parts of the world. ’When we meet Sokols from Australia, Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Francs, Britain, Germany or South Africa we realize that we are thesame kind of people, that we understand each other, that we belong together to the same organization v/hich is spread around the world. This is something that we need in exile more than anything else. ’We are going thru the difficult time when our units are getting less and less of the new blood and the old devoted Sokols are not replaced. The Zurich Slet was an outstanding achievement. The main importance was in the fact that thousands of Czechoslovak people who escaped after 1968 had the opportunity to participate in the Slet or to watch from the grandstand for the first time all of the wonderful force that idea is bringing to all of these people from all sites of the earth together. The Sokol movement, especially in Europe after 1968, gained great momentum and certainly the Zurich Slet has generated a lot of energy for it. Today there are eight active units in Switzerland and their slet showed us that they can be proud of the effort and hard work effectively done. The Slet in Zurich was conducted very precisely, in great style. Shortly, I would say it had "Class.” I can imagine how many days, how many nights, how many headaches the Zurich organizing committees have spent to put together something like that. Being very close to Bro. Mirek Svoboda, starosta of the Sokol Zurich, and chairman of the committee, I had the first hand opportunity to see the enormous load of hurdles they had to overcome. You should be proud and this should be warming your heart that you did something very big and important for the Sokol movement, something that will be recorded in the pages of Sokol Organization History. I an truly grateful and I believe all of the Sokols attending the Slet are also for having the opportunity to be a part of this historic event. My congratulations to Czechoslovak Sokols Abroad, but even more my respect and appreciation to the Sokol units in Zurich on a job well done.
