Duben-April 1982. Číslo-Number 4. Strana-Page 1. "Thomas Garrigue MASARYK day" in San Francisco. The planned public commemoration of Thomas Garrigue J MASARYK'S 132na birthday in San Francisco's Golgen Gate Park X. 1- on the 14 of March had to be cancelled because of rainy weather s t and postponed to the 21 of March. The weather was better but cold, especially for the musicians who were exposed to light but cold wind and hidden from the sun rays in the pavilion. On the overleaf you will find the program. Here I would like to express our deep appreciation and thanks to our own violin soloist Jan Novák for his rendition of Humoresque, and who, after resounding applause, played Masaryk's favorite sing “Teče voda teče" which was very moving. Our profound thanks go to Mr. Robert M. Hansen, the director of the Golden Gate Park Band, and all his players who really showed us that their playing is truly a labor of love. We really appreciated what you did for us. Thank you! Yes, it was a pleasure for all present to listen to those old beautiful scores by those giants like Bedrich Smetana and Antonin Dvořák. Truly memorable afternoon. V.F.B. [pageend1]Strana-Page 2. Věstník Sokola San Francisco. Duben-April 1982. PROGRAM GOLDEN GATE PARK BAND Ro be r t M. Of f ic e o f t h e May o r SAN FRANCISCO Dian n e Fein s t ein Proclamation WHEREAS, The principal figure in the creation of the Czechoslovak Republic after World War I was THOMAS GARRIGUE MASARYK.; and WHEREAS, It is fitting that we remember March 7th as the anniversary of the birth of this founder and first President of Czechoslovakia, who labored for the return of freedom to his beloved country without recrimination or bitterness, and whose dedication reminds us that the flame of freedom is eternal; and WHEREAS, THOMAS GARRIGUE MASARYK was, and still remains, the spokesman for free Czechoslovakia and for all people vho believe in an intensely human and moral philosophy, in the insistence of strictest integrity, and in championing self-government and democracy based on respect for the dignity of each human being without regard to their beliefs, their origins or their station in life; NOW, THEREFORE, I, DIANNE FEINSTEIN, Mayor of the City and County of San Francisco, do hereby proclaim March 14, 1982 as 'THOMAS GARRIGUE MASARYK DAY" in San Francisco in recognition of this great statesman and true believer in democracy. IN WITNESS WHEREOF, I have hereunto set my hand and caused the Seal of the City and County of San Francisco to be affixed this twelfth day of March, nineteen hundred and eightyAtwo. MEMBERSHIP DRIVE This Slet will commemorate the 100th Anniversary of the first ever Sokol Slet held in Prague in 1882 as well as the 150th Anniversary of birth of the founder of Sokol Dr. Mir. Tyrš. Next year’s Slet will be attended by Sokols from many countries of the Free World. In addition, members of several cultural, fraternal, sports and political organizations will also convene some of their meetings and other events in Vienna at that time. Thus the Slet in Vienna will bring together Sokols and Czechs and Slovaks from around the world.
