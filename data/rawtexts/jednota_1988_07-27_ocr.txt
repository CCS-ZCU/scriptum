We meet today for the semiannual meeting of (he Board of Directors of the First Catholic Slovak Union, which now is just a few days away from the start of our 43rd National Convention. All the reports have been written, all the committees have been appointed, and the direction in which we want our Society to move has been firmly established. As we make the final preparations for our convention, perhaps we, as a Board of Directors, should look at our accomplishments since our last convention, for I am pertain that some of u$ even today do not fully comprehend the magnitude of the changes in our Society over the past three years. !f you will take a moment to refreshen your memories, you will recall that in the early 1980s, when this board assumed their elected offices, our theme to the membership was the necessity for "change” in our Society, and how difficult it was to make changes. Change was necessary, for we had just changed administrations, the last administration having been one very deeply divided in philosophy and action, in fact, a review of the minutes of the administration of 1976-1979 will indicate that those negative forces on the Board in those days are still causing unnecessary costs to our Society today, Ju the early 1980$ we concentrated on updating our buildings in Middletown, and bringing new products into our Society's insurance portfolio. Many of us remember the painting of the pavilion, the editor’s home, the apartments, the former priest’s home, and the orphanage building. Many will remember tearing down the old barn, bringing water and new electricals lo the pavilion, and filling in the old swimming pool. Many will remember the creation of new soccer fields and the planting of new fields of grass for the beautification of the Jednota Estates. How well this Board of Directors remembers the middle 1980s, purchasing almost one hundred acres of new property at our Jednota Estates, bringing our holdings to approximately 315 acres from the roughly 200 acres to which cur holdings had shrunk over the past fifteen years. Previous administrations had rhe benefit of enjoying adding the profits from land sales to tbeir annual statements, but this administration had no such good fortune. This administration was destined to build for the future, and establishing a solid base on which their new growth could be accomplished. During these middle 1980s this Board of Directors took the responsibility for relocating our Slovak Museum & Archives from the Jednota Home, to updated quarters in the printery building. This Board instituted the Jednota Family Day Picnics, which are now an established program for our entire membership. In 1985 this board, in its wisdom, saw the necessity of creating a chair of Slovak studies at the University of Pittsburgh, and pledged Society funding for this program over the i next ten years. The program is now in ils fourth year, with approximately $200,000 of the program funded and highly visible and successful. For the first time in our history outside funds have been obtained.for the expansion of the Slovak program at the university, and we are now in the position of creating college student exchange programs with the universities in Slovakia and the University of Pittsburgh. There are no other fraternals with this unique funding and programs. But the Slovak community has done it, and the First Catholic Slovak • Union was at the forefront of this undertaking. This Board of Directors also took (he bold step of buying a new three story building in Independence, Ohio, for approximately two and a half milion dollars, instead of repairing and refurbishing the old home office headquarters in a deteriorating section of Cleveland, Ohio, as fought for and recommended by delegates from the State of Ohio. Had we taken this action, we would have truly relegated our Society io second rate status. In 1986 this Board of Directors again began to broaden IMPORTANT NOTICE CONCERNING FAMILY DAY its horizons. How many of our board remember the introduction, of the "Guaranteed Issue" insurance program, followed immediately by the new "1980 CSO program,” both of which are still in progress with today. These complaints were filed in violations of our Society’s Bylaws, which-mandate this misunderstanding between members and the Society be resolved within the forums of the First Catholic Slovak Union before complaining before outside entities. How well this Board can remember the problems in the Executive Secretary’s office, which forced this board to remove the Executive Secretary from his elected office in September 1987. But all of these problems and all of this strife is now history. This board has weathered all the storms of the past few years, and has won every legal battle. This Board of Directors can take full credit without any hesitation, that it has taken the right direction in assuring our Society’s survival and growth for the next one hundred years of our existence. All of us realize that our Society will be one hundred years old in less than sixteen months, and it is prudent for all of us to step back an see where our Society stands today. We are the only Slovak Fraternal Society with a Holding Company, and three subsidiary corporations.
