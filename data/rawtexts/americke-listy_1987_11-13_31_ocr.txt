Ředitel světové zdravotnické organizace doktor Halfdan Mahler zdůraznil, že syndrom získané ztráty imunity znamená ohrožení společenského a hospodářského rozvoje světové kultury, lidí v tvořivé fázi života, života rodin, matek a dětí. Doktor Jonathan Mann, který vede oddělení pro boj proti epidemii při Světové zdravotnické organizaci však připomněl, že AIDS je kontrolovatelný a lze mu zabránit. Přenáší se sexuálním stykem, transfuzí infikované krve nebo používáním znečištěných injekčních stříkaček. Nepřenáší se potravou ani vodou, nerozšiřují jej komáři ani jiný hmyz, nakazit se jím nelze na toaletě, na plovárně nebo v telefonní budce, podáním ruky, kašláním ani kýcháním. Rozšiřovaní imunodefektu lze tedy omezit svědomitou životosprávou. Předběžně se však AIDS šíří geometrickou řadou a v nejbližších letech bude postiženo několik milionů osob. Doktor Mann zdůraznil, že perzekuce nakažených, nemocných by mohla ohrozit boj proti nákaze. Infikované osoby neznamenají v normálním styku žádné nebezpečí. Vyloučení nemocných ze společnosti a zatajování problému by znemožnilo boj proti šíření nákazy. Postoj společnosti vůči nemocným bude nejen zkouškou základních hodnot, ale rozhodne i o úspěchu snah o omezení infekce. Americký úřad pro kontrolu potravin a léků povolil prvpí pokusy na lidech s vakcínou proti syndromu získané ztráty imunity - AIDS. CONGRESSMAN BERNARD DWYER REPORTS FIRST ANNIVERSARY OF ANTI-DRUG ABUSE LEGISLATION October 27 marked the first anniversary of the Omnibus Anti-Drug Abuse Act. This landmark legislation was an important step in focusing our efforts - national state and local - on combating drug abuse. As a supporter and cosponsor of this legislation, I am pleased to recognize this important anniversary as a means of calling attention to these vital efforts. . This new law put thousands of additional law enforcement officials irtthe field and toughened sentences for drug violations. During the past year, 37 percent of those sentenced to Federal prison were arrested for drug offenses. That figure was 25 percent in 1980 and 1 I percent in 1970. The bill also funds new prison construction to keep criminals behind bars. . Despite attempts by this Administration to curtai. anti- drug funding for state and local assistance, the Congress has acted to provide reasonable funding levels in fiscal 1988. Additionally, attempts by the Administration to reduce by one-half funding for drug abuse education programs in our schools, met with vigorous opposition in the House which rejected these reductions. Education is an important factor in convincing our youth that health considerations far outweight peer pressures to use drugs. The Anti-Drug Abuse bill also recognized the need to stop the flow of drugs at the border, and provided increased numbers of U.S. Customs Agents to ensure heightened vigilance. Searches and seizures conducted by Customs agents account for a significant portion of all illegal substances confiscated by this country. This year, I had the oportunity to chair an Appropriations committee hearing witn the U.S. Drug Enforcement Agency (DEA). It is clear to me from this hearing that our efforts must be intensified not only in seizing drugs, but in educating our youth to the dangers of drug abuse. The DEA has made dramatic gains in arrests of major violators and seizures of assets. Last year, over 18,700 drug- related arrests were made. Convictions in major cases increased by 22 percent. Assets seized by the DEA totaled more than $ 386 million, which represents an increase of 51 percent. In fact, in 1986 the value of seized assets exceeded the agency's appropriations. Drugs removed int the U.S. by the DEA also have increased substantially. Cocaine seizures increased by 49 percent, other drug seizures - including stimulants, depressants and hallucinogens - increased by 76 percent. In addition, the siezure of clandestine laboratories producing illicit drugs also increased dramatically. Over 430 laboratories were seized, an increase of over 28 percent. Despite record seizures, there are alarming facts to consider: more than 20 million Americans regularly use marijuana, a dangerous and relatively inexpensive drug called ’crack" is making it's way onto our streets, and experts estimate that more than half of all crime in drug-related.
