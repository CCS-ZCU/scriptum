KRAJSA, KSGG SLOVAKIA’S ‘ROBIN HOOD Illusions and Realities THE SECOND PHILLIPPINE MIRACLE By Michael Novak JANOŠÍK AND HIS VALAŠKA The talc of Janosik. the Robin Hood of Slovakia, woven in part from historical fact and in part from the rich imagery of the Slovak people. is part of the history of Slovakia. Sung in song, told In innumerable versions. pictured in poetry, painted in every graphic medium. Juro Janosik has become the symbol of the Slovak people in their fight for Justice, human rights, and freedom. There is no doubt that the exploits credited to him and the rich personality which he has assumed in wayside tales all over Slovakia have given him the character of glamour and might. Each nation and its people have legends and folklore and while there is no suggestion that Janosik should be elevated to the status of a national hero. It is a fact that he did exist and that he did capture the imagination of the ordinary people. In putting his legend into proper perspective. the evidence Is indelible — he. like England's Robin Hood, did take from the rich and give to the poor and he did Indeed break the law of the land. About the purpose of his deeds against oppression, he said. "The day will come and must come when truth will sit at the table of my people." Juro Janošík was hanged on March 13, 1713 at Liptovsky Svaty Mikulaš. And as he hung from the gallows by a hook under his ribs, the King's messenger galloped to the execution site with a written pardon. It was too late. Death came slowly to Janosik. For three days the hook, lodged under his ribs, dragged the life from his body. And when the King’s pardon was read, a pain-wracked Janosik gathered his remaining strength and with distain in his breaking voice replied. "You have already roasted me. so now you qan eat me as well.” then he died. ' Janosik and his valaška are synonymous because he developed it into a skillful weapon. The txtlaska or mountaineer's axe has a history that is as colorful and as lasting as that of the Slovak nation. Its origin can be traced as far back as the year 545. the period generally acknowledged by scholars as the start of recorded Slovak history. , Like the antique pistols of the Old West, the ualaska is now regarded as an historic ' symbol — a nostalgic bit of the past — momen- tos to decorate the walls of a den or study to remind today's generation of what once used to be. The valaška was basically a weapon used in self defense and over the years its size and .5 shape varied with modifications and improve- - meats. But its balance and physical dimensions of today go back to the 1700s when Juro Janošík and his band of followers roamed the Tatra Mountains and the Slovak countryside. Legends have a tendency to grow out of proportion and succeeding generations embellish the story so that fact and fiction seem to blend into a Gospel-like truth. The most outstanding portrait painting and the creation of an original work of 'Janošík' that is most striking was done by the noted artist. John Cabore. who was born In Forty-Fort. Pa. Vivid biographical accounts of Janošík can be found in many books, but the artist was inspired by Peter P. Yurchak's book. The Slovaks: Their History and Traditions. Through the centuries, many poets wrote ballads on the exploits of Janosik. but the most noted and outstanding was Jan Botto (1829-18811. a narrative poet with a taste for ballads. He made himself known for his epic song "Smrt Janošíkova" (The Death of Janošík) written in 1858. It is a thrilling story of the Slovak Robin Hood, hanged as a rebel by order of the nobility. With this poetic imagination Botto presents Janošík as a fighter for social and national Justice against Hungarian feudalism He succeeded in writing a poem of perfect rhythmic structure, resounding with noble eloquence Many of its verses of extraordinary beauty have become the patrimony of subsequent generations. Today, the valaška and Janosik remain sj^mbols of hope for the Slovak people. The valaška today is used as a walking stick It is probably the biggest souvenir item purchased by tourists visiting Slovakia. Washington.
