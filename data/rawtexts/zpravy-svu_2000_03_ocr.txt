This is our 20th Anniversary Congress. The Congress will také plače on the campus of American University, which has gone out of its way to make it a success. All the logistics and local arrangements are completed. This will be a truly monumental Congress. The program will offer a large number of choices, from generál cultural themes to the arts, humanities, sociál and behavioral Sciences, science and technology and various sessions regarding Czechs and Slovaks abroad. The eventful 5-6 days will commence already on Tuesday afternoon with a reception in honor of the Slovák and Czech American community leaders, by the Slovák Ambassador and Ms. Butora in their residence. The official opening of the Congress will be in the morning of August 9. In the afternoon there will be a speciál plenary session on "Czechs and Slovaks who Made a Difference in the Second Millennium" which will include presentations on such world figures as Hus, Komenský, Dvorak, Mendel and Freud. In the evening there will be a festive reception- hosted by the Czech Embassy, jointly with the SVU Washington, DC Chapter. Individual sessions will be on Thursday thru Saturday, August 10- 12th. The academie program in the arts will include two musicology sessions, theater, and history of art and architecture. The humanities will comprise of literatuře, literatuře in translation, two poetry sessions, linguistics, modem history and military history. In addition, there will be three sessions relating to Bohemian Reformation and a symposium on US response to the Slovák National Uprising. svu [pageend1]The sociál and behavioral Sciences will cover contemporary Czech and Slovák Republics, human rights in the two republics, creation of Civil Society through NGOs, inter-ethnic relations, volunteerism, politics and govemment, law and justice, media and joumalism, economics, business and trade, foreign investments, women's issues and the Holocaust, Science and technology will feature a two part "Twentieth Century Science Symposium: Success Stories and Experiences of Czech- American Scientists," and sessions on medicine and clinical research, health care, biological Sciences, physical Sciences, information technology and future of planet Earth. The generál cultural subjects will include a symposium on education reform in Central Europe, a symposium on Sokol, its history and its perspectives for the future, a session on sports with the participation of Aja Vrzanova, genealogy and family history, philately, and a symposium on Czech beer. The academie program on the Czech and Slovák presence abroad will feature sessions on emigration and immigration, early settlements and communities, cultural contributions of Czechs and Slovaks abroad, Czech and Slovák repositories in America, future of Czech and Slovák studies abroad, Czech and Slovák entrepreneurship abroad, and the role and personál experiences of Honoráry Consuls of Czech and Slovák Republics in the US. Part of the program has been prepared by our younger generation. Bruče Berglund organized four panels under the title "New Perspectives in Czech and Slovák Studies." In addition, there will be a discussion panel organized by Anna Vysoka on "The Next 100 Years: The Younger Generation Speaks Out". Apart from the academie program there will be a number of other exciting and entertaining activities. The highlight will be a banquet cruise on the Potomac and an excursion to Monticello, the home of President Thomas Jefferson. The participants will háve an opportunity to also attend the U.S. premiere of a poetic drama by one of the most outstanding Czech playwrights living today. The play "Hour of Love," (translated into English by Věra Bořkovec) is the work of Josef Topol, recipient of many prizes and most recently of the prestigious Karel Čapek Literary Award. She spoke on “Heart Disease: From the Czech and Slovák Republics to the United States - What You Should Know to Protéct Your- self”. This lecture demonstrated to our members how to focus on the control of risk factors and how to diet and exercise. In September the local chapter of SVU in cooperation with Americké Listy hosted the prominent philosopher and sociologist Professor Tomas Halik of Charles University. His perceptive analysis stimulated a lively and extensive discussion skillfully moderated by Petr Bisek, publisher of Americké Listy and the North American Representative of Impuls 99. On the occasion of our generál membership meeting and the elec- tion of the Chapter’s officers for the new term, the transfer of Národní Budova to the Czech Republic was presented for discussion and approval. In January 2000, Dr. Josef Machac, member of the Chapter’s committee, presented an outstand- ing lecture on “The Life and Legacy of Norbert Čapek”, foun- der of the Czech Unitarians. It was accompanied by extensive pictorial materiál and traced Capek’s con- 9 [pageend9]nection with Tomas and Charlotte Masaryk, his emigration to the United States and his return to the newly established Republic of Czechs and Slovaks, where he founded a religious society based on humanistic principles. ZUZANA JUSTMAN’s film ”Voices of the Children”, which was broadcast on PBS in April 1998, received a number of awards including the 1999 Emmy Award for Outstanding Historical Documentary. The film tells the story of three people who were imprisoned as children in Terezin and it explores the effect of the Holocaust on their lives and the lives of their families. The daughter of one survivor confronts her father for the first time, saying: “You cannot pretend it did not affect us.” In their efforts to use Terezin for propaganda purposes, the Nazis permitted inmates to stage a children’s opera called “Brundibár”. The survivors attend a performance of Brundibár in Prague and recall the speciál significance it had fifty years ago. Today the opera symbol i zes the lost world of the Terezin children. Despite his pian to eventually move back to Czechoslovakia, Munk decided to make Oregon his permanent home. After retiring from Reed College in 1965 he went on to teach political science at Portland State University for another 18 years. He was a passionate political scientist who dedicated his life to teach- ing world affairs. He helped establish the World Affairs Council of Oregon, commented on globál issues for KOIN-TV in the 1960s and wrote about politics for The Oregonian and The Oregon Journal. Frank Munk was a prodigious writer. Among other, he was the author of The New Economy (1929), Advertising for Export (1929), Con- temporary Distribution (1930), Cartels and Trusts (1931), Distribution Costs (1935), The Economic Eorce (1940), Legacy of Nazism (1943), and Atlantic Dilemma (1964). Josef Topol je snad nejvýznačnější český dramatik dnešní doby, který byl nedávno vyznamenán Cenou Karla Čapka za své celoživotní dílo. V Americe je celkem málo známý, avšak díky režisérce Gail Humphries (profesorce divadelního umění na Americké univerzitě) budou účastníci sjezdu mít možnost shlédnout tuto básnickou hru na scéně univerzitního Experimental Theatre. Představení v pátek večer 11. srpna je vyhrazeno pro účastníky sjezdu, kteří si mohou lístky objednat při registraci u Dr. Blanky Kudějové. * American premiere of Josef Topol´s play HOUR OF LOVE in Washington, DC During the Twentieth Congress of the Czechoslovak Society of Arts and Sciences (SVU) which will také plače in Washington, DC on August 9-13, 2000, the participants will háve an opportunity to attend the U.S. premiere of a poetic drama by one of the most outstanding Czech playwrights living today. The play Hour of Love (translated into English by Věra Borkovec) is the work of Josef Topol, recipient of many prizes and most recently of the prestigious Karel Čapek literary award. Josef Topol is little known in this country, but American University’s Professor Gail Humphries of the Performing Arts Department was fascinated by a reading of the play and decided to direct it and produce it for the Congress at the University’s Experimental Theatre. The opening night, Friday evening, August 11, is reserved for Congress participants. Tickets for the performance can be ordered at the time of registration from Congress Treasurer, Dr. Blanka Kudej.
