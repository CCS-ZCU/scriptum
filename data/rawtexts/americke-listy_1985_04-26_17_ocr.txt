According to the report, at least fifty percent of the pay gap remains unexplained. The Subcommittee on Compensation and Employee Benefits of tile House Post Office met on March 28 to review the recently released report. In the last Congress, a bill to study the federal civil service pay system was introduced by my colleague. Congresswoman Mary Rose Oakar. By a vote of ■113 to d. the “Federal Pay Equity and Management Improvement Act of 1984,” overwhelmingly passed the House. The legislation failed to gain approval in the Senate and died following the adjournment of the 98th Congress. However, under an agreement worked out between several key legislators, the Government Accounting Office was requested to evaluate different methods of studying the federal civil service pay system. Generally speaking, the GAO report discusses ways to determine why female federal employees earn less than male federal employees. Two general approaches were discussed, economic analysis and job content. Economic analysis attempts to measure and explain existing wage differentials between men and women, using characteristics of individuals, occupations and the workplace. On the otherhand, the job content approach focuses on an individual's particular characteristics, such as seniority and education. GAO believes that each approach has merit. Through the passage of the Equal Pay Act of 1963 and the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Congress sought to deal with the problem of sex-based wage discrimination.
