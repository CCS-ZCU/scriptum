Napište si o ni: [pageend3]LETTER TO NOBEL PRIZE LAUREATE Dr. Owen Chamberlain Californians for a Bilateral Nuclear Weapons Freeze 7250 Franklin Ave..Suite 101 Los Angeles, California 900^6 July b, 1982 Dear Dr. Chamberlain: I received your letter in which you are appealing for a bilateral nuclear weapons freeze. I cannot believe that a Nobel Prize Laureate wrote this letter. The tone of the letter is too demagogical and the presented "facts" are too vague, very untypical for a scientist. The rhetoric of the letter is more typical of political campaign brainwashers. I would rather like to believe that your name was misused by people who would like to see the Free World militarily weaker, and therefore, submissive to Soviet demands. Why do your actions start now, when the United States wants to eliminate the strategic and tactical disadvantage of NATO in Europe? Why did not these actions start years ago before the Soviet Union demonstrated the fastest armament buildup in history? West Germany Chancellor Helmut Schmidt, addressing a convention of the Social. Democratic Party, warned proponents of a nuclear freeze that its enactment in Europe would be the equivalent of accepting the "unbelievable Soviet armada that is aimed at us." Schmidt said the West must not let the Soviet adventage become institutionalized. Do you really believe the Soviet propaganda that they need their weapons for their defence? Tell me please, who in the West really wants to destroy and to occupy the Soviet Union, one sixth of the world? NATO countries? They do not even have in most of them the military draft! Communist countries have it! I was bom in Czechoslovakia and lived under the communist regime for 20 years. I got my education in communist schools and I was taught that "peace is a time for gathering our forces (Lenin)" and that "the purpose of the Soviet state is the complete and final victory of communism on a world scale." Therefore, I was not supprised that the Soviet Union was engaged in direct aggression in Hungary (1956), Czechoslovakia (1968), Afghanistan (1980), and more or less indirectly in many other countries such as South Vietnam, Ethiopia, and recently in Poland. These countries are now enslaved, because they were not under the protection of the western nuclear umbrella. Only the fear of an unwinnable nuclear war has restrained the Soviet Union from aggression against Western Europe so far. However, I myself experienced the fact that Soviet soldiers and some part of the Soviet public are mentally ready for an attack against the West. On August 21, 1968, when I met the first Soviet tanks on the streets of my native city, and when I tried to convince the occupants of their misdoing in the Russian language, they told me with suprise, "What, aren't we going to go fight West Germany?" [pageend4]-5- Therefore, I am convinced that ONLY WESTERN SUPERIORITY IN NUCLEAR ARMS IS THE BEST GUARANTY OF PEACE between NATO and the Warsaw Pact. The United States had nuclear- superiority for three decades and this superiority did not lead to a nuclear war. Your statement, that "a small group of military and political strategists ... are moving us closer and closer to nuclear war" is truthful in the Soviet Union hut not in the United States. The U.S.A. has not even started to build a civil defense. In the Soviet Union, civil defense has a 30 year history with a system of shelters in every city block. Dr.Chamberlain, your people have to start with anti-war and anti-nuclear activities in Moscaw, not in the U.S. Good luck: Our friends with similar initiative in communist countries have ended up in prisons. Ido not trust any agreement with the Soviet Union. You are asking us to do hard things with our minds. You are expecting us to believe that the Soviet Union can betray an ally (Hungary, Czechoslovakia, Afghanistan) and be faithful to us, their sworn enemy. You write, that, "a freeze on nuclear missiles and aircraft can be verified by existing means." It is theoretically possible only to a limited degree. As a physicist you should know how complicated and expensive it is to detect the rocket silos, luider- ground airports or mobile launchers that can be hidden in buildings from satelites. It would require regular picturetaking of every acre of Soviet soil and to have these many pictures evaluated by an army of specialists. The number of nuclear warheads is not practically detectable at all. According to ex-Defense Secretary M.Laird, the Russians cheated during SALT I. Why trust them now? They are still teaching their citizens that cheating capitalist countries is a "revolutionary tactic." A freeze would not save the 20 billions dollars that you predicted in your letter. A freeze would start a new race in conventional arms where the NATO army is at a large disadvantage. In tanks alone NATO is outnumbered by Warsaw Pact ^ to 1, in combat aircraft 2 to 1, and in artillery pieces 3 to 1. Twenty billions dollars is less than 3$ of our federal budget. For Russians this expense would be much more painful. We can afford it for the protection of the Free World, but a Soviet additional arms buildup would deepen the Soviet bloc economic disaster. We have rather help to speed up this disaster by the additional economic embargo. Hunger is the best revolutionary as we saw in Poland. A revolution in the Soviet Union would mean the end of the Soviet state, freedom for occupied nations, freedom for democrats in the GULAG - soviet concentration camp system, and finally, a total nuclear disarmament all over the world. This is a way to the true peace. 'It must be our goal. Your initiative, Dr.Chamberlain, can only lead to the similar destruction which helped to create another "peacemaker" with the same surname in 1938.
