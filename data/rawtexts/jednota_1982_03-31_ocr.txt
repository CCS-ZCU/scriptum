A GUEST EDITORIAL ESSAY - A Bit of Nostalgia Many of the Slovak oldtimers regret the untimely disappearance of the Slovak Dramatic and Cultural Societies which were in existence during the early 20's and 30's. These societies played a vital part In the^ preservation of the Slovak heritage and Identity. At the dawn of the century when Slovak immigration brought along a rich and colorful heritage of folklore, songs and dances, dramas, plays and skits of all sorts traditional to the locale from whence they emigrated. These Slovak Immigrants represented the most predominant Slovakian counties of Sarlä, Gemer, Orava, Splä, TrenCín, Zemplín, Bratislava and many otheiSt^Éach group was seem- ingíý: tjj^sed with a dialect of their own,* á'-language vehicle , '•communicative, yet understandable. often enforced by a com-, mon dialect coined as "po naáe- mu.” Once the Slovak immigrants settled in America, they sought a haven of refuge in an area that provided them with some real economic security. With economic security realized and a Slovak ethnic neighborhood stabilized, the Slovak immigrants focused their attention to the preservation of their religion, language and cultural heritage. Endowed by God with a simple but deep faith and armored with the "Sväté Písmo" and the "Nábožné Výlevy" the Slovak immigrants set out with apostolic zeal and unmatched sacrifice to build a fitting House of God- Surely, the truism, "that Slovaks pray well and pay well, ” can be truly applicable to the Slovaks of the early period of Slovak American history down to the present time with unques tionable validity. The motto, “Za Boha a národ" has always been a living reality for the Slovak Immigrants. Their priorities were basic and sound - God-Country-Home-Famlly. The -Catholic churches the Slovak immigrants built in the U.S. stand today as monuments of their living faith and sacrifice as their ultimate contribution tó American Catholicism. KOVALOVSKY ON COMMUNICATION - "Yes, we all need to practice more and better communication With each other to assure progress and to survive as fra lern allste It seems to me that some of us have adopted a negative attitude towards fraternal activities, just because we are not asked to participate. But within our hearts, there is a wann flame glowing and waiting to be discovered and put into action. "Our present financial independence and self-sustaining status are not giving us the needed satisfaction we are looking for. Most of us have been raised in a close-knit family circle — not in an aloof environment, but rather one with an honest, friendly, religious and fraternal atmosphere. "Through higher education, economic and financial gain and prosperity, we have drifted away from our own people. "On various occasions which we have attended, you and 1 have noticed this happening. If we just let our hair down and gqt back to our original heritage, we would quickly discover that we like each other very much, that we have a great deal in common, and together we can and will perform ‘miracles'. "Each group of people, whether they are organized or not, need a leader, someone who cares enough to get involved, not for selfish reasons but for the good of his fellow men. A self appointed leader doesn't last very long and usually does more harm than good- To find out who is available and capable to organize and lead, we have to communicate with each other and be prepared to accept leadership, when and if it is asked for or entrusted to us. "Thera are many members of fratemals with leadership qualities because they were raised-in Slovak discipline, honesty, hard work and mutual admiration. Many present officers of long standing are very good examples of this leadership, which they have carried on for many, many years. Now the reins must be turned over to our younger, energetic and capable members under the guidance of our elders. So. let's improve our communications with each other thereby reflecting improvement in ell phases of our fraternal organizations." — Tibor T. Kovalevsky.
