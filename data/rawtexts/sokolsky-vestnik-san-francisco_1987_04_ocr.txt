Children's classes started the 18th of September, and we meet every Friday from 7pm to 8pm. The average age of our class is ten, but I would like to begin new classes for tots and juniors. We also have an adult class starting at 8pm. If there are enough people interested in aerobics, we have a young teacher who would be more than willing to teach that class. What we do: Children learn gymnastic skills, worth with apparatus, play ball games, learn about Sokol history and Czech songs. After gym class the children have an educational class where they draw pictures, learn how to work with different kinds of material and make pretty things. We will soon start to learn calisthenics for the Slet in Canada in 1988. It is always a lot of fun when people get together. If you are interested, come to see us on Friday night. Pass the word along to your friends. It doesn't matter what origin or color you are. What counts is to make new friends, have fun and learn something new. Don't let our Sokol Unit die!! NAZDAR! Ave., Burlingame, CA 94010 Name Address___________________________ Amount Enclosed [pageend4]SOKOL SAN FRANCISCO VĚSTNÍK JULY, AUGUST, SEPTEMBER, OCTOBER PAGE -5- Bass Lake was the site of the Pacific Zupa Annual Camping trip. Approximately 200 Sokols spent their Labor Day Weekend recreating around and in this lake situated in the Sierra National Forest near Yosemite Park. The weather was pleasant throughout the weekend: warm during the day and comfortable (with the help of a thick sweater and a cup of mulled wine) during the evening.. Considering the proximity of the Yosemite wildfires, very little smoke was noticeable . Nevertheless, many people stayed away from the forest that weekend. The fair weather', beautiful scenery, and scarcity of people provided everyone the opportunity to participate in the activity of his choices. And the choices one had!!! Activities included hiking, swimming, volleyball, tennis, windsurfing, waterskiing, and even spectating. Once night fell most Sokols sat by campfires and sang Narodni Pisne until the following morning. The highlight of the weekend was the volleyball competition between the San Francisco and Los Angeles Sokols. "Warm-up" matches took place on Saturday. These practice rounds allowed each team to acout the opposition and discover any exploitable weaknesses. After Saturday 's practic matches concluded, both teams returned to camp and began plotting their respective strategies. Sunday morning the games began in earnest and lasted until Monday afternoon (after the last competitors were forcibly removed from the court by their wives). The outcome of these matches is really not relevant since either side will surely claim superiority over the other. The important point to note is that the competition was intense but friendly. Everyone behaved ina sportsmanlike manner; not a harsh word was spoken (in English at least) during this competition. What made this weekend so enjoyable was not the weather, scenery, or activities, but rather the pleasant company of the participating Sokols. We, of Sokol San Francisco, thank those who attended this year’s Sokol camping trip, and look forward to seeing everyone again next year!
