Seventy-five years is the blessed milestone, it is three quarters of a century of dedicated work for the public. Many people reaching this age cannot be too proud looking back and searching for their achievements. How different is Brother ^panek's fruitful life and his past. He can look back at his work for the National Orgánizaions and espcially for Sokol with satisfaction and happiness in his heart and more so, he should proudly look upon the job he has accomplished. It is not easy now and it was not easy in the past to work publicly for national organizaions. There are no materialistic rewards and very seldom the appreciation for this kind of activity. There were many disappointments, crucial and unpleasant moments, espcially during the second World War in the occupied part of France by the Nazi's. Several times George's life was on the line but as soon as the danger was over, he was back again working for the same ideals- the freedom and democracy of his homeland. - .. i Sometimes we wonder what is the power behind the man like Br. George Špahek and what keeps him going in his pace. It is the moral and physical strength, the dedication to patriotic and Sokol ideals brought up and cultivated in the early childhood in the Sokol gymnasium. These ideals were adopted by Br. Spánek and he understood their meanings. He fulfilled all the demands and tasks with which he was confronted. Today then, as Br. George Spánek celebrates his 75th birthday, we wish to pay our sincere thanks and appreciation for the hard work he is doing for the unit Sokol San Francisco. We believe it will long remain in existence and active due to the energy and iniciativě of the men like Br. George S'panek. We all wish him in the future a good health, long life and more happiness . We believe that with our help he will strive toward the fulfillment of the ideals he has set forth for his life- free men in the country and Sokol brotherhood. Enriched by the experience of his life, he will guide us toward the bright and prosperous future of our unit. All the Sokols of San Francisco wish you "Mnogaja Ljeta............"
