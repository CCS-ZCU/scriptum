Although Brezhnev continues to mouth the myth that the USSR is a classless society, he personally owns two .yachts and maintains a fleet of fancy foreign cars, including a Rolls-Royce Silver Cloud, a Citroen-Maserati. a Mercedes 450-SCL and two gifts from Richard Nixon: a Cadillac and a Lincoln Continental. Brezhnev s Moscow apartment and his country house are tended by servants and furnished with the latest Western gadgets as well as his famous collection of antique clocks. His favorite gadget helps him to fight his tobacco habit. It’s a locked cigarette case with a timer that forces Brezhnev to wait for his next smoke. However, unlike the rest of the people in the USSR, who have no choice but to wait for goods, Leonid Brezhnev sets his own timer. Capricious Summer is based on a book by and is a tribute to the Czech writer Vladislav Vančura, whose contributions in developing the early Czech film industry, in originating an economical, lyrical literary style and in purifying the Czech language had particular significance for the succeeding generation. Set in a small resort town at the turn of the century, the drama revolves around three middle-aged men whose inept vacation is interrupted by the arrival of an acrobat-magician, played by Menzel himself. The focus of the three men shifts from swimming in the river to seducing the magician’s very pretty assistant while the magician one-ups them by having an affair with the wife of one of the three men.
