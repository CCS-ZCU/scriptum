Březen - March 1976 Číslo-No 3 The future of Sokol San Francisco There are very few ethnic groups in USA which have their own building for their activities. Sokol San Francisco is fortunate to pride Itself in owning their own Sokol Hall since 1912. After a long and almost unanimous vote /95^/ majority,it was determined to move from Page street S.F. to San Mateo. The San Mateo building serves us well for all the functions,but it would be even more comfortable and in better repair if our members would take more Interest and pride in it. Maintenance of a building is very demanding and renting it out becomes at times quite discouraging to say the least. Only the small profit from various entertainments and renting permits us to break even. Planning and follow up those activities is hard work,sweat and dedication. There are but a few active members who participate and volunteer their time for the good of all as oposed to an army of critics. Our compatriots want for little money a lot of music and an overabundance of food. The Indolence and pesslmismus of some members and some compatriots assuming that Sokol in the USA is nearing its end,hence justifying their indifference to the cause of Sokol to support and help in any way to keep Sokol alive. And those very people are the greatest barrier in the work of a few Sokols who believe in it and devote their life and energy to the ideals and philosophy of Tyrš and Fugner. It is a pity that in a time of rapid social changes on a' global scale and those who should know from history that what we are trying to preserve is the very essence of basic universal human values. True to the human condition,we shut our eyes to the possible faults of a friend we cherish, and so prevent ourselves from seeing his virtues. Would you risk not seeing those rays from a star which would reach us only after the star itself has ceased to shine ? People are vulnerable and become easily phlegmatic and sceptical in the arbitrary chaos ruling the world. The strugle and strengt lies in challenging the misplaced values and stir up the bad conscience. Critlzism is not enough. Act upon the negative situation. Resist the penetration of the omnipresent cultures. Be a participant in things of passionate interests. Stop being an idle observer, listener or critic. The rumor of the doom of Sokol has been circulating in the USA the past half of century and still we were celebrating 111 years of Sokol in USA. It is true that there have been changes in the USA Sokol as well as other organizations. That is to be expected. In the past three decades the emergence of mass media had a tremendous impact on the socio-cultural aspect of humanity, affecting the global village with the onslaught of rapid change. [pageend1]Page 2. Věstník Sokola San Francisco Match 1976 Then there is the problem of the two languages. It is only right that we should all speak the tongue of our adopted country, but that does not mean that we have to neglect or forget our native tongue. We are in dire need of communion and understanding. Then there is the nagging problem of not having a náčelnice and načelnik. Sokol S.F. is becoming a social club only. How long this crisis will last, depends on us all. We received complaints of not having adequate gymnastic equipment. This too depends on the goodwill of us all for betterment. I call on all those who wish to be convinced of the difficulty of organizing any activity with food service, they are welcome . to attend members meetings every second Monday of the month. We are always looking for volunteers for various functions: i.e. selling tickets,at the bar, kitchen,clean up etc. There is always the question what will be served,who will cook,who will make the best Czech dumplings etc. If we would have to payfor all the services which constitute a social gathe«- ring with food service, the dinner would cost $ 7.00 at least and not the present $ 3.00. It is regretful that all the volunteer work is done all the time by the same volunteers. And there are always the same critics who never participate in work and ask for more knedlíky a vepřovi for the nominal price. Some complain of heat or lack of it. Some feel it would be beneat their dig— nity to lend a hand at clearing their table with overfowing bottles,glasses and dishes. 0 Please,come to the meetings, perhaps you have a better idea and better know how than the present small circle of volunteers, you wont have any difficulty to be elected, even for a leading position in the Sokol. Na zdar! Czechoslovak Sokol Abroad To Our American Sokol Brothers and Sisters: As most of you are aware our organization will hold its IV. Sokol Slet in the City of Zurich, Switzerland in 1976. We hope that it will be possible for you to join us there. The tradition of co-operation and mutual assistance between American and Czechoslovak Sokol is well known. It is best and visibly expressed at Slets of our organizations. This mutually beneficial practice dates back to 1882 when American Sokols attended the first Sokol Slet held in Prague, directed by the founder of Sokol, Dr. Miroslav Tyrš. We trust that this traditional Sokol brotherhood between American and Czechoslovak Sokol will be realized again at our Slet, and we extend to you our most sincere invitation to join us in Zurich for a demonstration of the strength and vitality of the Sokol movement in the free world. Hope to meet you in Zurich! Nazdar! Jan Waldauf, Director of Men Vlasta Waldaufová, Director of Women A brief outline of our organization would be of interest to you: Sokol in Czechoslovakia - COS - associated 52 districts; 50 in Czechoslovakia, two outside - the “Vienna district” and the “Sokol Abroad district” associating Czechoslovak units outside of Czechoslovakia. Following the dissolution of Sokol in Czechoslovakia by the Communists, Sokol units belonging formerly to the Sokol Abroad district, together with Sokol units newly formed after 1948, established in 1952 our present organization - Czechoslovak Sokol Abroad. In 1955, following the departure of Soviet occupation forces from Austria the Vienna district joined as well. Czechoslovak Sokol Abroad is, therefore, a continuation of ČOS, having developed out of two of its original districts. Today, Czechoslovak Sokol Abroad has 30 units in Argentina, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, Holland, Germany, England, France, South Africa, Sweden, Switzerland. Its membership totals 2,102 men and women. The information contained in this communication is a brief outline of our Slet events and activities. Ferjenčík: Sokol Organization The first Sokol unit in the U.S.A, was founded on February 14, 1865, at St. Louis, Missouri. The National headquarters are located in Berwyn, Illinois. Sokol “Slets” or Festivals are held by units and districts each year; by the national organization every four years. Besides the American Sokol Organization there are the following Sokol organizations: Slovak, Polish, Yugoslav and others patterned after the original Sokol organization. The combined membership in all these organizations is well over 250,000, young and old. EASTERN DISTRICT, New York, N.Y.
