[pageend1]Page 2 Věstník Sokola San Francisco January 1^79 ANNUAL REPORT OF ,S OKOL SAN FRANCISCO FOR 1978 The year 1978, is history now and we are standing on the threshold of 1979. This is a good time for accounting and evaluating of the past year and also planning and setting our goals for the upcoming year. Last year, was the year of sad anniversaries as we remembered 1938, The Munich Agreement, 19^8 - the end of democracy in Czechoslovakia and finally 1968, the invasion of Warsaw Pact forces into Czechoslovakia and its lasting occupation. Looking back on the activity of our unit, we cannot be satisified with 1978. While we were trying to keep social events as frequent as possible (and we achieved that program) we are not happy with the gymnastic activities. Classes were conducted on a limited basis for the first part of the year but after school vacations, we were not able to sustain any regular activities in the gym classes. This year (1979) Sokol Los Angeles is celebrating the 70th anniversary of its founding and planning the district exhibition or possibly even a bigger slet. They are depending on the participation of Sokol San Francisco as we are the only two active units in the Pacific District. It will be a difficult task to build up some type of attendance in the gym classes and enough enthusiasm to send decent delegation to L.A. The above situation is probably the reflection of the whole membership of our unit. Our membership is still representing a targe number of 170 members. The problem is though, how many of these people are interested in participating and witting to keep us alive and at feast to worry about our future? About 10 to 12 members are doing a11 the work, week after week, month after month and year after year. They are not only doing it for their pleasure or financial gain but are doing it because they feel that there is nobody else and it is their responsibility, belonging to this national organization, to keep the unit active. This is our duty above al 1. A very disturbing fact is the average age of the members of Sokol San Francisco. I cannot pin point the exact age number but it is certainly around 55 years. This is becoming a very serious situation for the unit in the near future. This is also clearly proving that we are not gaining any younger members particu1ary!y from the younger immigration wave of 1978. The situation in our working membership group, i* getting to the point that the people who do do all the work are becoming over- smrked and dissappointed as here in this letter, there are constant calls for help and unfortunatly there is no response. Every time one of the people of that precious groqp departs or is unable to help anymore, there is nobody to take his place and that precious group of members gets thinner every year. Besides the difficulties which I have stressed above, we as a unit have achieved quite an iapressive record of social and educational events during the year of 1978. The following is a chronological recapitulation of a!1 our unit's activities: January 15th: We opened the year of 1978 with an annua! meeting. At that time, we decided to have an annual meeting on Sunday in the anticipation of acquiring a larger attendance. The decision was apparently a correct one, as we had about 30 members attending the lunch and then the meeting. Febuary 11th: St. Valentine's dance was also the occassion to celebrate the 75th Birthday of our Soko! unit President, Brother George Spánek. During the short program, in his behalf we expressed the thanks and appreciation for his hard and endless work for Sokol. The soko! members presented him wi th a digital dock and radio. March 8th: We enjoyed an evening with Br. Matef Najman, a long time president of Soko! Washington, D.C. and well-known worker in A.O.S. and Soko! in Exile. Unfortunatly, this was his last trip as he died (2) weeks later of heart attack in Singapoore. March 11th: Traditional celebration of 128th birthday of T.G. Masaryk. After a short program everyone joined in a dance. [pageend2]January 1^79 Věstník Sokola san Francisco May 6th: the May dance was very successful as some of Zupa delegates from Los Angeles attended that evening. July 22nd: We called on all voluntary helpers to paint the Sokol Hall. We were able to finish the job the same day because some 17 members (old and young) answered the call. Also, the part of the same group installed and put into concrete the volley ball posts in Sokol's backyard. August 19th: Soko! with the cooperation of the National Council of Free Czechoslovakia, commemerated the 10th anniversary of the invasion of Czechoslovakia by the Soviet and Warsaw pact armies. The film, "Seven Days in August" was presented after the artistic program provided by Ed Ingris' family. It was a very nice program but the attendance, especially with the people involved in that historical event, was disappointing. August 27th: Br. and Sis. Stroleny invited al! Soko! members and friends to the picnic in Richmond, the recreation center of Standard Oil Company refinary. The attendance was about 60 people. Sept. 30th: St. Venceslaus dance was very well attended and financial results were good. Oct. 28th: Commemoration cf Czechoslovak Independance Day. Attendance on this evmt was suprisingly large. There were about 150 people. Br. Ingris and his family again took charge of providing the artistic program. Sept. 2-4th: Pacific District Sokol camping at Huntington Lake. This traditional outing is always enjoyed by many members and this time is no exception. We counted 43 members and non-members from the Bay Area. All enjoyed the campfire and volley ball. Our members attended the Zupa meeting held Sept. 3rd. Dec. 2nd: St. Nicolas Dance was reverse of the two previous dances as attendance was very low. Well, we cannot win them all! Dec. 31st; Our New Years Eve party was a real winner. This farewell party with the year 1978, was well extended into the 1979 and everybody welcomed the new year with new hopes and new expectations. Let us hope that this year will bring us also new hopes and expectations. Hopes and expectations though will not bring about a!1 the positive results..We have to work on it and work hard before it is too late. We have to try to get a new and young blood <n our veins and rejuvenite the unit. On the other hand, though, we have to stick desperatly to that strength that is keeping our Sokol unit going. For that reason, we are pleading to all of you for help and to those who are helping, we express a great amount of thanks and gratitude for the job that was achieved only with your understanding and loyalty. Our special thanks must be paid to our President George Spánek, He is doing the giant job which should be certainly more appreciated by our •embers. Thanks to his effort, our SOKOL VESTNIK is issued regularly, not only to the members but to many Soko! units in the U.S. and everybody who is interested in the activity of our unit. This job of writing, typing, editing, assembling ard mailing of several hundred printed materials, is difficult to imagine. There are also invitations , several hundred of them almost every month, which are singlehanded by Br. Spánek. He needs a1ot of help and this is the time we should realize that ard make his job a little easier. There are a few things we all should be thankful. First of all, we are thankful for the support we are enjoying from the Czechoslovak ethnic group around the Bay area. Without that support, we could not maintain our financial responsibilities. Despite the difficulties facing us daily, we are thankful for sti!1 being able to be here after so many years and we are alive helping and providing the Czechoslovak people with the service and entertainment they enjoy. We are thankful to a!1 of you who are here today. It is the proof that you are the members interested in Soko! and its ideas. We urge you to stay and help us and to appeal to your friends to join us and get the new blood, new ideas and the new life into the unit. This would be the only weapon on how to fight the worst enemy of our Soko! organization - and this enemy is genera! apathy of Czechoslovak people in this area. May the year, 1979, bring you all hopes and expectations and to Sokol San Francis© it should be a year of improved activities and fu1 fi 1ment* of long lasting wishes to bring the unit to the brighter times.
