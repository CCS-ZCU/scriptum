The instructors are having classes twice a week. This means that the hard work of our brothers and sisters is beginning to pay off. Once can feel the Sokol Spirit among our young. To top it all off, the first group of boys and girls who came to Sokol Hall on October 28 took the places of our tired Sisters and patiently served and cleaned the hall. Their contributions mean a lot to all of us. It does not matter how or what they dido What really counts is that they came to help. They came because they want to follow in the footsteps of their fathers and grandfathers who started Sokol here in the Bay Area. Everytime one of our members submits his resignation, we all worry, but after last October 28, we need not worry again. Our new generation has proved, as many have predicted, they stand ready to take our places. Charles Samal Educational Director Bulletin of Gymnastic Association Sokol San Francisco [pageend1]ANNUAL REPORT OF THE PRESIDENT OF SOKOL SAN FRANCISCO FOR 1974 At this Annual Meeting we will examine the work accomplished in 1974 and set up our goals for 1975. We will elect officers, devoted men and women, to their various roles of leadership, and assure them our full cooperation. I wish to express my sincere appreciation to all officers and members who helped me to organize our events. The year 1974 has been a special one. We celebrated the 70th Anniversary of the founding of Sokol San Francisco, and we successfully organized a District Gymnastic Exhibition. The results of our action have been divided in different ways. Not only do we have the responsibility to keep up the maintenance of the Hall, but we must strive to improve it. Also, it is essential that we balance the budget. T. G. Masaryk once said? "The best idea is always the question of financial support". We rent the hall. We organize our dances and celebrations not only for money, but we want to give our membership and friends some Sokol and artistic image to capture their interest. We must never forget our duty is to support the fight for the freedom of Czechoslovakia. Our gymnastic activities must be our great concern. Without them our Sokol Unit will only be a social club. This year our Sokol children under the direction of Sis. & Br. Meier, made great progress in gymnastics. We deplore the fact that our adult Sokol members are not at all interested in gymnastics. We cannot be satisfied with the lack of an entertainment committee. During the year it was virtually impossible to form a team of volunteers to set up our program. Only a few members and always the same are involved in the hard work of setting President’s Report Continued from Column 1 up the tables, preparing and servJ ing the food, decorating, etc.MorJ membership participation is essen-l tial to our success. Since our Sokol Units are using thf English language more and more in 1 the meetings, new Sokol members fr<f the old country are handicapped. I They are sometimes afraid to speak 1 in English because they feel unsure of their English. Do not forget that we can speak Czech or English in our meetings. If you feel more comfortable, use the Czech language We will translate for those who do not understand. The Sokol newspaper has been the heart of our Sokol movement since Tyrš and Fugner organized the Sokol. In relationship to our membership, our Sokol Bulletin has a most significant function. Our articles are appreciated outside of our unit and reprinted in the A.S.O. Bulletin in Chicago and in other unit’s bulletins. Our Věstnik is self-supporting. However, we could do better if all members would take a little more interest. I mentioned earlier that this has been a special year. In addition to our anniversary celebration and the gymnastic exhibition, we had our traditional celebrations. We had a successful show with Jara Kohout. Eduard Ingris entertained us with an unusual movie. Each of these events was a financial and moral success. We must constantly search for more artists in our area sc that they may participate in our programs. This will add to our success.
