The difference between a good organization and a poor one, an effective committee and an inefficient one, is often reflected in the cooperation, or lack of it, among the people who work for it. We do things better and we live better by helping each other. The fellow who lends a helping hand, benefits himself at the same time as he helps others. When people help each other freely and voluntarily, there is a spirit of teamwork that makes a unit or organization really go and a pleasure to be associated with. When there is no cooperation or spirit of helping hand, what might have been pleasant jobs become grudging bores. There is very little difference in people but that little difference makes a big difference. The little difference is attitude. The big difference is whether it's positive or negative. People can be divided into 3 groups: those who make things happen, those who watch things happen and those who wonder what happened. Do not expect all people should be perfect. If you do, your whole life will be a series of disappointments and complaints. If, on the other hand you set up your expectations low, you will be frequently surprised by having them perform better than you had hoped. It is not the incompetent and inefficient who destroy the organization because he never gets in a position to destroy it. It is the one who achieved something and wants to rest on the glory of achievements and is forever messing things up. Sooner or later, we will learn than............ . . . 2 [pageend1]Fage 2 Věstník Sokola San Francisco March 1977 - It doesn't pay to be sensitive; that we should let some things of no importance go over our heads. - It doesn't matter so much who gets the credit as long as the organization prospers and things are done. - It doesn't do any harm to smile and say "well done" even if it is not to your expectations, or say "good morning" even if it is raining. - One who loses his temper is usually the loser. - Temper is what gets most of us into trouble and our pride is what keeps us there. - The most valuable gift we can give to others is a good example. - Be kind; remember, everyone you meet is fighting a hard battle, too. - Nothing is easier than finding faults with somebody or something; there are no talents, no brains and no character required to set up the grumbling business. - The trouble with people who talk too fast is that they often say something they hadn't thought of yet. - So be sure that the brain is engaged before putting mouth in gear. George Spánek, Sr. "OUR ROOTS" My young grandson came to me with a serious question, namely, if I had watched the Television serial entitled "Roots" by Alex Haley and what was my opinion about the brutality of some of the American pioneers against the black people 200 years ago. I did not want to deny this historical fact of shame in American history by some of the pioneers but I explained to him that, at the same time in Europe, many of our ancestors lived in slavery. That 200 years ago in Europe, there were two classes: the masters and the servants who served the masters. heard We, the first born/from our parents about their grandparents living in slavery. The only difference was that the slaves were not imported but domestic. The poor laborer, with all his family was working for the master from sunrise to sunset without pay. The master only gave him food and lodging. He was not free to change his place of work, he was not free to send his children to school and, if he should try to escape, the soldiers would bring him back to the master. We can read in History books what the social life of poor people in Europe was like. If a poor, hungry.man stole a piece of bread, his hand would be cut off. Justice was only for the rich. Add to this physical slavery the moral pressures for religion and it was not an easy life. Our religious sect, the Moravian Brothers, with little belongings, crossed the border to exile. We can remember the persecution against the Jews, their ghettos, pogroms and concentration camps. When social progress came to Europe, the white masters could not continue their horrible profession. Instead, they emigrated to [pageend2]March I977 Věstník Sokola San Francisco Page 3 America where they began to oppress the black people, imported from Africa since, at this time, they did not have to fear law or justice in America. We do not need to go back that far (200 years) in history. My old aunt in Texas told me that, just a hundred years ago, her parents came to America where life was not all that easy for white people from Europe. Her parents bought a piece of land, cultivated the fields, built a farm, raised livestock - all the hard labors associated with farming. It wasn't too long before the Indians or "bandidos" came along, burned the farm and stole the animals. They started the farm up again. By this time, the black people were relatively more secure under the protection of their white masters. As in Europe, the social conditions in America changed with progress and it was the white progressist, Abraham Lincoln who gave, by law, freedom to the black people. The second part of Roots deals with the segment where the author, Alex Haley found his roots in a little village in Africa. I know many of my friends who are researching their own roots. They spend a lot of time with aging relatives; they have also been to Czechoslovakia to research their roots. They searched for ancestors in different archives, however, they are stuck on either the 3rd or 4th generation. They return to America with a child's story about their great-great-grandfather serving in the army of Jan Zizka. However, the question of our roots remain. The point is.......... why or what does it matter? Why isn't it sufficient to be just what we are? Why pay so much attention to our ancestors the older we get when we should learn to live and appreciate our own limitations. I wonder how Alex Haley found his roots in Africa as far back as 200 years I explained all this to my grandson and asked him to go to his teacher and ask what she knew about our roots.
