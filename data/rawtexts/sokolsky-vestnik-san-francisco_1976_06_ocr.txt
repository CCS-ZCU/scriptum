MORE "GOOD NEIGHBOR" RECIPES With more and more people taking to home gardening, we thought you might make use of these recipes. Cucumbers always grow well and very rapidly. Here’s two good ways to prepare them. We thank Sokol Havlicek-Tyrs for these recipes which appeard in an issue of their Newsletter, Cucumbers for Freezing 5 or 6 cucumbers, peeled and sliced 1 or 2 onions sliced 1 1/4 cups vinegar 2 cups water 1/4 cup salt 1 1/2 cups sugar Boil sugar, salt, vinegar, and water. Let cool. Arrange slices of onion and cucumbers in large bowl. Pour cooled mixture over all and let stand overnight. Put into containers next morning and freeze, CRISP SUMMER PICKLES 1 1/2 cups each, sugar, cold water, and cider vinegar 1 1/2 Tsp. onion flakes 3/4 Tsp. each salt and celery seed 1/2 Tsp. each garlic, celery, and onion salt 4 medium cucumbers, unpared and sliced a bout 1/4" thick. Place all ingredients except cukes in quart jar and shake well until sugar is dissolved. Pack cukes in gallon jar, cover with the vinegar mixture and store in refrigerator for a few days, (if they last that longl) Don’t forget our "Good Neighbor" Cookbooks are still on sale. OUR SOKOL BULLETIN Since Dr Miroslav Tyrš founded the Sokol organization in 1862 the Sokol Bulletin was the educational, informational and organizational liaison between the Sokol membership. American Sokol Organization prints the monthly bulletin "American Sokol" and many Sokol units have their own bulletin which is not only the bulletin for membership of the units but also the exchange of ideas between uni ts. Printing the Sokol Bulletin is not very easy. First it takes courage to be an editor and also needs the financial support. The units with the large membership can iprint the bulletin with very professional printing, but the value of the bulletin is not only the question of the editor but, the Sokol duty of all members to support it morally and financially. Now, we have a problem in what language should the; be 1 i etin be printed? In Czech or in English? We should take in consideration several reasons for and contra; We are living in America and, we are । all speaking and reading less or more English. But, we are also basically an ethnic organization and we have the Sokol duty to keep alive our heritage. The number of members reading Czech or English is a local matter. We are interested about the situation in Sokol San Francisco. 75% of our readers are speaking and reading very well the Czech language and only 25% read only English. The Czech readers are an absolute majority, but we do not want to discriminate and our general information is printed in English. What way our bulletin should be printed depends on our readers. They should collaborate and express their point of view. Before you start to crit- ize, let's think about our different p oblems - our readers have the floor.
