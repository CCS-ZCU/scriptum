New Conservation Corps to provide jobs for youth The House of Representatives has moved early in this new session of Congress to provide much-needed jobs for youth through passage of the American Conservation Corps Act of 1983, H. R. 999- Nationwide, the rate of youth unemployment is 24.5 percent; the rate for minority youth is more than 50 percent. H. R. 999, which I co-sponsored, would provide jobs for more than 100,000 young people through a youth conservation work program, modeled after the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) of the 1930’s. The purpose of the legislation is two-fold: to provide jobs and training for young people who need them and to accomplish needed conservation work in a cost-effective, efficient way. Conservation projects include conservation of forest, fish, wildlife, rangelands and soils; revitalization of urban areas and preservation of historic sites; maintenance of recreational areas, energy conservation and production of renewable resources. H. R. 999 consolidates and improves previous youth conservation programs sheduled for termination by the administration — the Youth Conservation Corps (YCC), a summer program, and the Young Adult Conservation Corps (YACC), a year-round program. Both of these programs had proven to be cost-effective in providing needed conservation work as well as meaningful training and jobs for unemployed youth. The YCC returned §1.04 in work for each dollar expended; the YACC returned $1.20. Funding for the new American Conservation Corps (ACC) would be derived from Federal oil and gas leasing revenues, timber sales and similar activities. Of the total annual appropriations, 35 percent would be distributed to states, 25% each to the Departments of Interior and Agriculture, 5 percent to participating Indian tribes and 10 percent to other federal agencies and for special projects. The new ACC wotdd have year-round and summer employment components. The year-round program would hire unemployed youths between the ages of 16 and 25; the summer program would hire unemployed youths between the ages of 15 and 21. Special consideration would be given to disadvantaged youth in areas of high unemployment, however, the legislation specifies that ACC projects must not displace currently employed individuals or individuals who have been laid off from the same or equivalent jobs. With unemployment among youth at record high levels, this bill would be one cost-effective step we can take to offer them meaningful jobs that will not only aid them during these difficult times, but that will help provide them with a future as well. Like the old Civilian Conservation Corps, it would help restore physical and land-based resources, Irom which future industries and jobs can be derived. Tento obdivovaný kámen totiž s velkou pravděpodobností pochází z Marsu. Byl nalezen před dvěma roky v Antarktidě a byl klasifikován jako meteorit. Už při prvním ohledání tušili geologové z Johnson Space center v Houstonu sensaci. Jako skládačku sestavovali všechna fakta, analysovali je, nechali počítat a kombinovat. Jak vyplývá ze zprávy, před 180 miliony let byl Mars zasažen obrovským meteoritem, který dopadl v šikmé dráze na jeho povrch. Při jeho dopadu vznikl v blízkosti vulkánu Ceranius-Tholus, který byl ještě před 1,3 miliardou let aktivní, podlouhlý kráter, douhý 30 a široký 14 a půl kilometrů. Úžasná síla dopadu zvířila mnoho tun horniny a mezi ní možná i kámen, nesoucí nyní ozna- čení 79 001, 8 kilogramů těžký, hnědošedý basalt. Laurence Nyquist, vedoucí oddělení výzkumu planet NASA v Houstonu líčí, jak bylo možné, že kámen z Marsu se dostal na zem.
