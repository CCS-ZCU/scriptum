From the Stet opening to the closing ceremony very few details were overlooked. The official Stet opening took place Friday evening, June 23 at the Pollock Concert Hall on the McGill University Campus. Br. Karel Finke, President of Sokol Canada welcomed the audience numbering over five hundred people. After Br. Pinke 's address, the audience was treated to a cultural program which featured local Czechoslovak artists performing the works of Dvorak, Smetana, and Martinu - among others. Guest performers included Vit Fiala (violin cello), Vera Brun (mezzosuprano), Dr. Jan Rubes (Baritone), and Prof. Oskar Morawetz (piano). The evening ended with standing ovations and audience request for more music. Gymnastic and Volleyball competition took place on the University of Montreal and McGill University campuses. After the contests ended, the competitors were honored at the Slet Victory Dance and Banquet at the McGill University Student Centre. Over 600 Slet participants and spectators attended. Furiant! - Sokol San Francisco Czechoslovak Folk Ensemble - entertained the guests and received very favorable reviews. Once the athletic competition was over, only the closing ceremony and calisthenic drills remained. The closing ceremony took place Sunday morning on the University of Montreal athletic field. All Slet participants marched onto the field and were reviewed by Sokol officials and acknowledged by the Slet spectators. Next, Mass Calisthenics follwed. Each Slet participant (Child, junior, or senior) performed with his peers in a program specifically choreographed for his class. Finally, after nearly two years of drilling, the participants were finally performing before a large audience. The mass calisthenics were, by far, the most exciting part of the Slet program. The conclusion of the VIII Montreal Slet was exceptionally dramatic - mostly because it was not planned by any committee. After the official Slet conclusion, spectators, participants, strangers, and new friends were exchanging farewells, tearfully embracing, and some, even excitedly planning reunions for the next Sokol Slet. This scene on the field, I am sure, will be remembered by many Sokols well after they return to their homes in Canada, the United States, and Europe. I, on behalf of those San Francisco Sokol Slet participants, would like to acknowledge Sokol Canada and Sokol Masaryk-Montreal for successfully preserving Sokol traditions and promoting Sokol Ideals during the VIII Montreal Slet. Folk Ensemble — Sokol San Francisco cordially invite you to attend an open workshop given by a leading authority on Czech and Slovak dance, Frantisek Bonus. FrantiSek BonuS, professor of dance pedagogy in Prague, Czechoslovakia, has had a lifetime career in dance and music. He is Europe's best known exponent of Czech and Slovak dancing, and has given workshops in France, Belgium, Denmark, Switzerland, Italy, Finland, as well as in his own country. Professor Bonus has served as choreographer, folklore advisor and technical consultant^to the z Československý Statni Soubor Písni a Tanců, the Czechoslovak national dance ensemble. He is also the founder and longtime director of one of Czechoslovakia's finestz amateur groups, the Josef Vyčpalek Folk Dance Ensemble. Professor BonuŠ's research in dance has led to the publication of ten books, and he is presently completing a study of the historical origins of the "polská" and the polka. Professor Bonus will be conducting folk dance workshops at Sokol Hall during the week of August 1st through August 10, 1988. The workshop is open to anyone who is interested in Czechoslovak folk dancing — spectators and participants alike are welcome! For further information, please call Jana Kurkova at 415/332-8741. Ho - hum! by Victor Graff Millbrae Chapter No. 2049, American Association of Retired Persons, recently nominated three of the six winners in the Senior Forum of San Mateo County 29th Annual Senior Citizens of the Year competition: Rudy Dauber, 1st Place (Men); Meta Berry, 2nd Place (Women); and Elizabeth Bele, 3rd Place (Women). The competition is designed to recognize those older men and women who have made an outstanding contribution to their community during the past year. The selections were made by three prominent judges on the basis of the candidates’ voluntary contributions to community betterment, concern for people, and service to their neighbors. The awards were the highlight of the Senior Forum annual picnic held this year at the County Fairgrounds on June 17. Elizabeth Bele also gave almost 1,000 hours to community service as a Millbrae Parks and Recreation volunteer in many activities, including: nutrition program, surplus food distribution, member Senior Advisory Board, Senior Talent Show, Halloween Spooktacular, and Ye Oide Christmas Faire.
