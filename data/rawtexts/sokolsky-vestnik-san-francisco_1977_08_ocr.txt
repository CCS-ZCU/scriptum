American Sokol and politic The Alv Slet of the American Sokol Organization has been successful and now we should raise the question concerning our future Sokol task, aim, and goal. The basic idea of Miroslav Tyrs was to train the Sokol members through gymnastics so that through the development of healthy and energetic individuals the whole nation might become strong and be one firm pillar of democracy. But, Miroslav ly.rs first of all wanted to steep the Sokols in the idea of the struggle for existence and the independence of the nation. In.other words, he wanted the Sokol members to be interested in their national politics. When they hear this word, politics, our present Sokol leaders and many good Sokol members run away. They argue that politics should be kept out of the Sokol organization. In the past Sokol members in Czechoslovakia, accepted Tyrs idea and were always ready to support the fight against dictatorship. They contributed to create the Czechoslovak Legion Abroad during World War 1. They stood up for the election of President T. G. Masaryk and they helped to support the fight against the Nazis during the World War II. A great service was rendered to the Czechoslovak people by the valuable support of the American Sokol and the Czech National Council of America each time that the independence of Czechoslovakia was in distress. Czechoslovak independence has again disappeared, but at the present ■ time the American Sokol believes in "splendid isolation*’ and doos not support the struggle for the liberation of Czechoslovakia. Vře can see an example in our official bulletin- ®!3RICAN SOKOL. In the past we could read in Czech or English language articles commemorating the historical events and supporting the fight of the Czechoslovak people for freedom. Many local bulletins of American Sokol units follow this example. Our Sokol members should think about this situation. Many times we have said that our American democracy has the same importance as Czechoslovak democracy to us.
