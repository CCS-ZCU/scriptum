With the exception of the first one, the year 1918, they are the reminders of the sad milestones in the history of our nation. This year we will celebrate 60 years when the new Czechoslovak Democratic State was born. After 300 years of endless fighting and effort, the dream of Czechs and Slovaks came through on October 28, 1918. A new and free nation was born in the middle of Europe. During the next 20 years, this new state proved its ability to govern itself under the Democratic Regime designed by his founder, T.G. Masaryk. But very soon, the dark clouds coming from Hitler's Germany entered the bright sky of this unfortunate nation. Because of unpreparedness and unwillingness of Western Democracies to face the Hitler's war machine, the vital parts of the new republic were ripped off in the Munich agreement signed in 1938, under the foolish presumption to save the peace in the world. Terrible war conflict was just born and Hitler armies started marching. Febuary, 1948, reminded us that 30 years just passed by since another terrible force, this time coming from the East, knocked down again the rising hopes of the people in the newly established democracy, after the 6 dreadful years of the 2nd world war. But this nation and its people didn't resign. The new hopes and expectations were rised in the so called "Prag Spring 1968" only to be dwarfed again with armies of Soviet Satelites marching into Czechoslovakia. Over 100,000 Czechoslovaks left the country for unknown destinations, just like in 1948 in the hope that they would find peace and a better system womewhere else. In the comparatively short span of this young nation, the nunfber "8" was playing the sad role in its history records. These anivers- aries force us to think what, where and who went wrong. But whatever arguments we will present, none of them will return the democracy in Czechoslovakia -more- the arguments will divide us and that is exactly what we hardly need at this time. We cannot look back but at the same time we cannot reject the history either. We have to learn our mistakes from it and look forward. Our future and the future of our nation is still ahead and we have to be unified to be able to face the tasks and hurdles which will face us.
