Helen Murphy : OCTOBER 28, Those of you who were born in Czechoslovakia and are now living in America, have a closer attachment to the old country than those of us who represent the generation born outside of Czechoslovakia, Nevertheless, we too have strong ties. We are proud to be born of Czechoslovak parents. We learned from them about their struggle for the independence of the Czechoslovak Republic. They taught us about their first president, Thomas G. Masaryk and informed us about the Nazi occupation of they country. We know about the Communist push in 19^8 and we know about the Soviet occupation of Czechoslovakia. As Americans, we are attached to our democracy but we are not indifferent to the country which is in trouble. Until the last small nation is delivered from slavery and any kind of dictatorship, our duty is to help. Only in this way will our American democracy become stronger. What can we, Americans, do to help liberate Czechoslovakia from Communist oppression ? One way is to assist Czechoslovak organizations in furthering their cause for freedom by constantly reminding the American people that they share in the responsibility for the fate that befell the Czekoslovak nation. We must strive to persuade American poli - ticians and the leaders in Washington that they hgve a duty to help the Czechoslovak people. As Americans, this is what we must stand for. Tonight, at this commemoration, I appeal to all of you. Join our Sokol Unit or the Czechoslovak National Council of America“ and help us work together for a better future ! NAZDAR ! PROCLAMATION of the Czechoslovak National Council of America At a time when the United States are being maligned abroad and our free institutions are being undermined at home, the Board of the Czechoslovak Na tional Council of America, speaking on behalf of numerous American citizens of Czech and Slovak descent, solemnly proclaims: We believe that America represents the best hope for freedom, justice and democracy throughout the world. We declare our intention to do our utmost to strengthen the healthy forces in our society and to help America in bringing peace, justice and freedom to all people deprived today of these values, which alone make life worth living.
