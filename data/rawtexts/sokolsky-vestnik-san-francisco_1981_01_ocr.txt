Klaudia, narozená v Kursku v Sovětském Svazu, byla za války nasazena Němci na nucené práce v Říši. Konec války ji zastihl na pracovišti v Rakousku, kde byla zachycena Rudou armádou a dopravena do sběrného tábora v židenicích, odkud měla být spolu s ostatními podobnými nešťastníky dopravena do SSSR s konečným cílem : Sibiř. Nedlouho po svatbě obavy o budoucnost je z.a hnaíy do exilu, aby nako— nec zakotvili v San Francisku. Klaudia našla v Československu vlast, ke které zaujala po zbytek jejího života velmi vřelý poměr, který nakonec vyjádřila přáním, aby její popel byl uložen tam, kde se k ní poprvé začali lidé chovat jako lidé a kde jí byly poskytnuty podmínky k lepšímu životu - na Moravském Slováčku. Thanksgiving service for fallen heroes. after the fall of France in 1940, members of the Czechoslovak armed forces in exile arrived to England, and the first units were formed in Cheshire to continue fighting with the Allies until final victory. Many of those soldiers and airmen have kept vivid memories of the camp at Cholmondeley Castle and the warm welcome they were given by the people of Cheshire. It was therefore appropriate that on the fifty-ninth anniversary of the Royal British Legion they have invited members of the Czechoslovak Association of Legionaries to join in a Service of Thanksgiving, to recall the struggle they shared to resist Nazi tyranny in Europe, and to dedicate a Memorial in the Cathedral of Chester to those who sacrificed their lives in that war. The simple Memorial stone has been set in the wall of the South Transept near R.A.F. Memorial and the inscription reads: "To the memory of Czechoslovak soldiers and airmen who fought with the Allies and sacrificed their lives during the 1939-45 war." The stone was unveiled during the course of the service by Sir William Barker, PhD., K.C., M.G., O.B.E., formerly H.M. British Ambassador in Prague. [pageend5]Strana-pa^e 6. Věstník Sokola San Francisco Leden-January 1981 Report on the bokol San Francisco Annual Meeting 198o. There is no easy way to honest success. It takes a lot of planning, thinking and hard work of our few true Sokol members, nonmembers and friends who helped to make our social events successful. Usually reports were done by the educational director, but as soon as I took office, trouble started. First we received the resignations of sister Sieber as secretary and “náčelni ce" and brother Sieber as "náčelník" . We immediately elected sister Helen Murphy as secretary. Later brother Kryska resigned as educational director and brother Spánek Sr. as^editor of Věstník. Brother V.F.B. was elected as the new editor of Věstník. It certainly was not a smooth beginning for the new administration but with the help of Sokol members we worked it out and Sokol San Francisco is still here and growing. At last count we had eleven new members, ^ere were no physical activities, but we hope that in 1981 we will once again offer children and adult classes.
