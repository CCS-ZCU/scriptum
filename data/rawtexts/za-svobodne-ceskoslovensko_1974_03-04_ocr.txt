First on agenda was a plan to keep in contact with the membership, to inform them of the duties and activities of CNCA in Los Angeles. As we began our work without any funds, we were forced to turn to our members and friends for support.The results were very gratifying. You will find the list of doners in the Czech section. We sincerely thank all for their donations, support, and understanding. Members wrote us many encouraging letters, expressing their gratitude for reactivating the organization. Requests also came to print an English section in the bulletin for our members and friends, who are proad of their Czechoslovak heritage, but do not speak or read--Czech. We are honoring their wishes by not translating the Czech articles into English, but by a separate English column informing reader* of our goals, activities, events, etc. On March 3,1974 we held a Memorial for 124th Birthday of first president of Czechoslovak Republic, T.G. Masaryk. Altho the weather was not ideal, and gas shortage existed, we had an attendance of about 100 people, who by their presence proved their williag&es to defend democratic and humanitarian ideals of T.G.Masaryk.
