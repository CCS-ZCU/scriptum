This is what I wish to share with you. We'll start out early in the day when the lady or man of the house goes out to shop for breakfast makings:fresh rye bread or rohliky, butter, a few envelopes of coffee and perhaps somethings for dinner. The main meat of the day is at noon,so the menu for the day would be planeed when the shopper sees what the meat store has to offer. Shoppings is done daily because the mini-refrigerators of the household are only about 2^ to cubóc feet and hold just about a day,s supply' of food . Amazingly, many food stores are already open at 6 a.m. close at 8 a.m. and then reopen later in the afternoon. People can nad must do their shopping before leaving for woek. I,d heard about queues and thought that wasn,t very strange, for I stand in line at a chck-out at Uncle Bi1l"s or Heinen,s, but it is unusual to have stand in line just to get into a store. Although there are a few mini-supermarket-type stores, the majority specialize in one or two products; meat, fruits and vegetables, milk etc. There you must stand in line until you are waited upon, then you place yaur order from what is available. There may have been bananas when you get into line 15 minutes ago, but chances of finding them a when it's your turn, are slim. Your purchasses of unwashed carrots, onions, califlower, a loaf of ryeJ bread are put as you get them, into your taska (string bag, cloth or plastic tpte bag) which a!1 sensible citizens carry at all time. Paper bags do not exist, although shops do have paper cut into various sizes, much as we have our graduated sizes of paper bags. Articles such as clothings, glassware, books , records and other such good are wrapped in this paper, but you,11 be clutching, fumblings and dropping little packages a! 1 the way home. While we are shopping. I'll mention something strange... someting one becomes aware of only after a while,- the shop and their names! With only a few exeptions, the only name that appears is the product they sell. You see shops called " Books " "Records " Chtkdrens games" 'Sweet shop" etc. It's quite a challenge to try to tell someone where you bought a book. •Oh ... I bought it at a store called " Books ", on the corner of Sunchandsuch street ( if pau can remember the street name) .. ( otherwise ), next to a Sweet Shop and across from a "Fruits and vegetables" store. The abolishment of private owner-ship has wiped out individuality, and the sameness is sad. One more word about queues. I had heard it said that people will line up for anything; that when they see a tine, they will get into line first and then find out out item the store is selling. I can say that this must be true, for one day we came to a gift shop and found it closed. The store hours were posted and the shop should have been open by that time. We stood for a moment or so, debating whether to stay a tittle while longer in hope that the store would be opened... and 1o and behold, six people lined up behind us. A coincidence ! Now we'11 go for a little snack... to my favorite shop , the Cukra'rna (sweet shop ). These tittles shops quite often have a " zmrzlina " ( ice-cream) Inside one can buy zákusky open -faced sandwiches, omelety and coffee. The little cakes (dorty) are wonderful for they taste as good as they Idok. ( So often the same cakes here at home are too sweet and taste nothings but suger). These Czech confections are not wery sweet, but have individual flavors that are delicate. They each are a picture, with layers, fillings, toppings and garnishes. The coffe is the usual " kafe s logrem". This is coffee which has been ground into a fine powder- boiling water is poured over as we do for our instant coffee - and the resultant brew is stirred and allowed to settle. Although^ many people I talked to here at home didn't care for this coffee, I grew to like it. The trick ,was to left the ground settle. And remember not to drink it to the last drop. This was very nice for both of us for had a chance to talk to many people..... not exchanging names but exchanging a lot of information. It was asually agreed that the talk would stay away from heavy politics. Uiich it did most of the time. Perhaps the answer 1s the next subject: -Walking. Oh my, how.' people do walk in Czechoslovakia. There are cars, to be sure, but with the high cost of gasoline and the poor quality of roads, the best way to get anywhere is either by public transportation or walking. There is no driving down to the corner for milk or a loaf of bread «... you* walk there. It was not unusual to be far out in the country and see a great number of people walking... to the bus for work, to the store, walking everywhere. Here at home we park the car as close to the store as we can get, or even double park. There, when we did get a rids somewhere, our driver parked the car anywhere he saw an empty spot,.... no matter that it might be 10 block away from where wanted to go. CO JE Č S N R A ? ČSNRA Je organizací Američanů československého původu. Vznikla po druhé světové válce a v tomto roce se dožívá 60 let. Vznikla na základě sjednoce— ných československých organizací v Americe, které pomáhaly organizovat čs. zahraniční odboj a našim politikům v úsilí o obnovení samostatného čs, státu v době první světové války. Této tradici zůstala ČSNRA věrna. V jejích stanovách se praví: "Je věrna tradičním americkým zásadám spravedlnosti a demokratického řízení státu z vůle lidu a pro lid. Je věrna zásadám Jednoty Cechů a Slováků v Jednotném samostatném státu československém. Usiluje o spolupráci a účast všech složek krajanského lidu ve Spojených státech amerických k povzbuzení zájmu všech A- meričanů o náš československý původ, k šíření československé kultury na půdě americké a k zachování vlastenecké tradice a dobrého Jména amerických krajanů. " Ve stanovách je také odstavec, který říká, že ČSNRA bude bránit demokratický řád a principy proti komunismu a všem totalitním silám a hnutím. Pro tento odstavec je považována CSNRA za politickou organizaci v širokém významu tohoto slova. Tento odstavec má tu výhodu, že nám určuje důležitý směr v naší činnosti a odpuzuje ty Jednotlivce, kterým jsou^totalitní režimy sympatické a nebo kterým se chtějí zavděčit. ČSNRA není však přívržencem žádné politické strany, má vsak v současné době vynikající a vlivné zástupce Jak ve vedení demokratické strany, tak i strany republikánské.
