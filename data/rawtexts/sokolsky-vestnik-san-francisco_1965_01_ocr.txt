As you can see, the formát is different, the contents enlarged, the print better. With so many periodicals around already, why do we con- tinue editing the Bulletin? What function do we hope to fulfil? The answer is simple: if we were content to assimilate into the American society as speedily as possible, for- get our background, traditions, our own brand of life- philosophy, if we indeed hoped that our children would be no different from the kids neit door, then we'd be wasting our and your time. Assimilate, to a certain extent, we will and must. But is it necessary to lose one ' s ethnic, cultural and phi- losophical background in the processVDon 't we háve any- thing of value to preserve, nourish.to hand over to the next generation? To the wider society of which we háve become a part? We are a distinct group, sharing certain common ideals, characteristics, behavioristic patterns, cultural heri- tage . In today's world of growing uniformity, we háve a function to perform. If any group is to survive, retain its identity,it must háve some means of inter-communication: a fórum, so to speak. Without some means of keeping in touch with each other, without discussions, exchange of opinions, argu- ments, critique, without even the possibility of agree- ment on the important issues, we are merely a handful of guys of foreign extraction and background, living in Virtual isolation in the Bay Area ( although it might just as well be the Congo or Siberia ), waiting for the inevitable oblivion. Our contribution to the socio-cul- tural mainstream is exactly nil ! This Bulletin will become our common meeting ground, we hope. Our means of reaching each other, a chance to be heard, an outlet for all who háve something of interest to say; a plače where we can say aloud what we like and don't like, where we find out what we háve in common. Ankles get occasionally twisted, backs strainedjthe muscles hurt, the joints ache the next day. No records are set, no trophies won, and yet in some strange way it all makes sense, in the end. The players may not make a sporting history, but they do not finish the losers, either. ( Proverb) [pageend8]9 WHAT LIES IN THE FUTURE ? In every issue of the Bulletin we plan to include at least one article dealing with the more fundamental issues we are facing today. Your comments, critique or contributions are welcome. Today, a thoughtful article by our " nacelnik ", br. J.Kucera about the future of SOKOL in U.S.A. The gym attendance figures are up for all groups. The rise was quite remarkable last fall, in fact. It's a great feeling when the gymnasium is full, every piece of apparatus in use, every corner occupied. That's what keeps the unit alive, that's what we are working for. Who minds the extra work ? Yet, those of us brought up in the old country know that this isn't the Sokol we used to know. Sure,many people come,but for what reason? Some just for fun,others to meet friends, or even just to keep their weight down.... they know little or nothing about the Sokol movement, its history and ideals. Partly, this is our own fault: we seldom conduct any educational classes or give lectures. How can we then expect those people to learn or understand the fundamentals of Sokol ideology? The indoctrination of young members started at an early age back home, and continued through the junior years.lt was aided by the tremendous prestige our movement enjoyed: we were supported by popular opinion, by the newspapers and radio. Many prominent people were our members,In most places Sokol was the center of physical, sporting and cultural activity. The young people were proud to belong. Of course, the life was very different then. We did not feel the disruptive impact of the modern way of life quite as severely as we do now.Television and cars were not luring young people away from the gym; everybody had more time for meaningful activities. Here,living scattered over a large area, to attend the gym classes often means driving many miles. It takes a real determination and enthusiasm to come regularly. Most youngsters,insufficiently motivated as they are, come only occasionally; nor are the parents willing to bring the very young ones regularly, for the same reason... that is why we have comparatively few children in the hall,and why very few juniors stay with the movement long enough to attain a full membership. This spells a serious problem later on [pageend9]10 As for the senior members and gymnasts,most of them first joined Sokol here. They do not have the background the old-timers had some fifty years ago. And how could they? They grew up in very adverse times. 26 years passed since the Germans marched into Czechoslovakia and the planned destruction of Sokol began. The movement was persecuted during the war, and subverted by the "peoples republic". The short period after the war,when Sokol was trying to rise up from the ruins, was too short to leave a lasting mark. Let us bear that in mind before criticizing someone for lack of loyalty or deeper feelings for Sokol Oddly,even while living in a friendly country and pleasant atmosphere, we have hidden enemies in our midst which we have to seek out and fight: shallowness, indifference and apathy. The Czech and Slovak immigrants of last century brought with them a profound national feeling, rich traditions, love for their language and songs, a deep respect for the Sokol ideals. They guarded those like a treasure. The new immigrants do not as a rule possess this enthusiasm and loyalty...we do not feel like a national group; we are cosmopolitan, eager to adapt,ready to forget the language, the songs, the ideology. It is quite true that Sokol is not the same it used to be back home, but this does not necessarily mean it is bad. We live in fast-changing times, the conditions are different. This calls for flexibility of thinking. We cannot bluntly reject all that is new and modern. Rather, we have to adapt, develop, adjust,, After all, Dr. M. Tyrs, the founder of Sokol, lived a century ago; his views would certainly be different today. What about the future? Should we give up because the movement isn't quite the same it used to be? Should we dispair because there is lack of interest or even indifference amongst certain Czechs and Slovaks? Of course not,those are challenges we have to overcome) We must wake up the apathetic and bring them back into our fold. We must meet the problem of changing conditions by keeping our unit physically and spiritually active. We will definitely have to become more flexible in the future about the membership of non-Slavonic people interested in Sokol. In fact we'll have to find new ways to make Sokol more popular amongst the American public. Highly placed government officials and o- ther influential people are well aware of our achievements and the role we could play in the quest for physical fitness of the American nation.... the issue of Sokol Centennial Stamp proved it. This goodwill must not be wasted. Those are the problems we are facing,but despite them our next century should be even more fruitful than was the first one. I believe that Sokol will rise up to the new challenges, which, after all, are no bigger than some in the past. Sure, it will take work, thought and enthusiasm, but Sokol did it before and will do it again. You'll always find something of interest there I Some of the most beautiful tropical fish are on display. Cissy, one of the two brown harbor seals in the outdoor tank will make you smile at her cute antics. And then there are the new arrivals --- like, for instance, the sweetwater dolphin " Whiskers " whose real home is the Amazon and Iquitos in Peru. "Whiskers" is the most gentle,trusting and lovable dolphin you ever want to meet.Just look how happily he reposes in Bob Dempster's arms.... It was Bob who went to bring "Whiskers" to San Francisco from his temporary home in Florida. The journey started by plane, with our "Whiskers" bedded on some sponges in a tub. There was very little water in the tub, but even so, Bob had to hold "Whiskers" head up all the way,not to get the dolphins blowhole submerged in water.This did not work very well.. "Whiskers" was getting uncomfortable; so much so Bob thought he might lose him. The journey had to be interrupted at Chicago's aquarium, where "Whiskers" revived overnight in a large tank. It was decided that he should make the last leg of the trip in a tub filled with water. Luckily, a pilot was found who must have been the ace of the Tiger Line. Not a single drop of water was spilled while taking off or [pageend14]15 landing in San Francisco. Bob and "Whiskers" received a royal welcome at the San Francisco International Airport, and the short ride to the Steinhart Aquarium was just a breeze. "Whiskers" has adjusted admirably in his new home., already he has grown 2 inches and gained 20 pounds. He is being weighed and measured every month,to see what progress he's making. He lives in Tank # 43, right by the alligator gars, from whom he is separated by a nylon mesh. Being a curious and neighborly creature, he tries all the time to make their acquaintance, pushing his slender bewhiskered nose as far as it would go against the net. " Whiskers " is one of the most charming and adorable creatures at Steinhart.... everybody just loves him and goes out of his way to take at least a look at him when ever possible. See for yourself! I am sure " Whiskers " will charm you too.
