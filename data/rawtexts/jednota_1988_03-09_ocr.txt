Illusions and Realities WIN ONE FOR THE USA By Michael Novak The most striking point about the Winter Olympics is that, once again, the Germans won. The Soviet Union won 29 medals. But the East Germans won 25 and the West Germans won eight. Reunite the two Germanies, and the Germans beat the world. The second point Is that you have to give communism credit. Communism isn't very good at growing food, at producing goods, or at breaking down class structures between the nomenklatura and ordinary people. But communism does do four tilings superbly: raise huge armies: establish an immense secret police: fund and train athletes for international competitions, and specialize in propaganda as its most important weapon. Communists take ideas and images and their own reputation very seriously. And their sports programs are only one aspect of their general strategy. But hand it to them. They do these four things very well. As for the United States, the first thing the U.S. Olympic Committee must do is fire the psychotherapists, therapists, and counsellors to U.S. athletes. More and more. U S. athletes are talking psychobabble. They are explaining their defeats in terms of their feelings. They have become athletes of the psyche. They have also begun to psychologize their own performances. One hears things like: "My main goal this year was to enjoy skiing (skating, luge. etc.I," or "My personal goal this year was to move up to sixth — and I did it." Others said: "I did my best; I can't do more than that." And: "Even if I didn't win. I'm happy with my performance, and that's what counts." Hey. look! Pressures from outside arc inhumanly intense, and each athlete must look within. But if the purpose of these Olympics is only to soothe psyches, why should the public be asked to foot the bill? I did not hear a single performer (there must have been some) say that they were trying to win one for "old glory": or that nothing but the gold is good enough for the United States of America. The most often expressed motives were therapeutic, not self-transcendent; self, not country. No doubt. If any athlete Had expressed a nationalistic motive, many "enlightened" citizens would have chided them as old-fashioned, sentimental. not universalistic. against the spirit of the Games - we know those words too well. All of them false. When there are causes greater than we are. we all give efforts greater than those of which we are capable. What every athlete needs is to be lifted out of self, to play for a moment or two among the gods, not among the mortals. On March 5. 1925. a special meeting was held in Philadelphia. Pa., to discuss the possibility of Joining together the various Slovak Catholic fraternal societies. Present at the meeting were; the First Catholic Slovak Union. First Catholic Slovak Ladies Union, the Pennsylvania Slovak Catholic Union, the Ladies Pennsylvania Slovak Catholic Union, and the Catholic Slovak Gymnastic Union Sokol. A resolution was passed by which all the societies agreed to work together on the national, cultural, and religious fields. Jozef Husek. Editor of JEDNOTA, had long advocated the joining of the societies since before World War I. A few weeks after the Philadelphia meeting in 1925. Husek wrote this about the union of the Slovak Catholic societies: "One thing is certain. If we Join together we will prolong our life for more than a century. If we remain divided up as we are now. in one quarter of a century all our Slovak institutions, religious, national, cultural and benefit societies will be buried. "By saying this, we do not mean that in 25 years there will not be any Slovak organizations, but these organizations will not be Slovak organizations. There will not be Slovak churches, schools, newspapers or societies. This is not a prophecy, this is purely a mathematical calculation. "And this is not everything. Although we will continue to exist for these 25 years, we will not produce much, and after that even less. Finally, brother will rise against brother, sister against sister, organization against organization. We will destroy each other because everyone will want to hold on to his own longer at the expense of others. And with this, we will quickly end our existence. "The only defense of this destruction is the union of the organizations. If we merge, we will be stronger financially and morally The Joining of the organizations would not impose any further burden on the membership. It is more probably that things will be easier. Administration will be not only simpler, but more effective. "The organization numbering over 200.000 (the total number of the union at that time) would be such a force that it would be respected everywhere. It will be more effective when it defends Slovak national interests and when It works for (he nation's moral and material progress." — Jozef Husek, JEDNOTA.
