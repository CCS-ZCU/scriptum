Pa. this past August) Annuities .Annuities represent the newest line of FCSU. There was no annuity bMjnndfln force Ln-the FCSU at the time of the last Convention. At December 31. 198?. annuity reserves amounted to $1.700.000 or 3% of total assets of $51',600,CCD. The annuity reserves may be-expecled to grow dramatically- By the next Convention, annuity reserves could exceed 50% of all assets This asset accumulation will be most helpful Ln the maximization of profits in the subsidiary companies and hold the potential for very attractive profits to the FCSU. It is Important that you be Informed as to the advantages and the risks which are involved. Annuities represent the accumulation at interest on a tax deferred basis until the retirement date selected by the annuitant. During the buildup period, the member places depos its with the FCSU on a single pay, regular pay. or Irregular payment basis. After retirement, the FCSU guarantees payments for the rest of the member's lifetime according to an option elected by the annuitant. True, an individual member can create his own annuity and retirement fund, but subject to certain drawbacks: The average individual is not as skilled at making in vestments as the FCSU; small investment cannot be made as advantageously as large investment; the individual loses the safety arising from a spread of investment risks: the accumulating interest Is taxable; the individual must have great discipline to make regular deposits; and when the individual retires. there is no guarantee that he will not outlive his assets. The FCSU. therefore. 1s rendering a real service to Its members by offering the annuity product. A new Investment philosophy Is required for annuities. Traditionally, life Insurance proceeds were invested In long-term contracts at the highest possible yield In recognition of the long-term nature of the contract, namely, until death or maturity. The few surrenders which arose could be satisfied through investment Income. Annuities, however^have a shorter contract duration and an earlier demand for money. This demand for money is controllable by the annuitant and frequently reflects changing interest conditions. Accordingly. the annuity proceeds must be Invested on a basis such that cash will be available to meet the demand. This means investing In shorter maturities with a uniform amount maturing each year. Further, the annuity assets must be segregated sc that management will know exactly how much is being earned and. therefore, how much can be credited to the members. These needs have given rise to a new era of regulation under the general heading of "cash flow analysis.” This cash flow analysis Is not the same as that which appears on the Annual Statement. The Annual Statement • merely reports the cash in and out during the latest calendar year. By contrast, cash flow analysis studies cash income and outgo for the foreseeable future according to a wide variety of possible interest scenarios. The purpose of these cash flow studies is to answer the question as to whether there will be cash on hand to meet the demand for cash as it arises. As• you actuaries, we have always made in your behalf projections as to the future profitability for each plan. These arc tested annually to determine If the anticipated profits are being reasonably realized. These studies are not the same as cash flow analysis, as It is quite possible to have a plan which will project strong profits over a 10- or 20-year period but may create a negative cash flow in between- Slnce the last convention, new state requirements have been promulgated on cash flow analysis. At the moment, ihe studies are required only on the annuity business. However, the regulators have "promised” that these requirementswill be expanded to other lines ofbusiness and will soon be required by many, if not all, states. The appropriate studies were made in behalf of the FCSU at Decembers], 1987- Nineteen different Interest scenarios were devel oped as Interest projections for the next ten yearn. They summarize the results of those studies in the form of showing discounted v alue otiuture profits. The value at December 31.1987 of the future profits on the business then in force ranges from a low of 38.575 to a high oF$549.O16, The range depends upon the Interest path which actually occurs. Six other societies, some of which have been writing annuity business much longer than the FCSU. have therefore accumulated much larger assets and hence profits. The results are quite favorable and show that an enlightened Investment philosophy will create, asset growth and future profits. Insurance profits are declining In the industry due to the competition of Universal Life and rising costs The FCSU must follow suit. The formation of the holding company and its subsidiaries was Ln full accord with actuarial principles. The following observations should he made; a) The holding company and the subsidiaries are completely owned by die FCSU. There are no outsiders. The Subsidiary Boards report to the FCSU Board of Directors, who report to the delegates, who in turn report to the members. For all practical purposes, the FCSU Bqard of Directors has complete control of the entire operation. b) Money transferred from the FCSU to a subsidiary does not reduce assets or surplus of the FCSU. Such transfers result in a change In the form of investment as from cash or bonds to common stocks Profits are affected only to the extent that Investment income on the common stocks may be less temporarily than on the previous investment. This Is a.i very small price to pay for the opportunity to earn a very large appreciation on the investment in subsidiaries. It was my privilege to hear a great debate by your Directors as to the proper method of developing subsidiary property. In the performance of their duties, strong views were presented. The choices in the development of the property were; 1) Sell the property for an immediate profit: 2) Undertake a Joint venture with a developer who would use his money and therefore receive the lion's share of profits and appreciation; 3) Development by FCSU but borrowing money from the outside for development purposes; and 4) Development by the FCSU with the subsidiarviröTTOwTrtgs, from the parent. / It was not an easy decision to choose between an immediate and sure profit which might amount to $3.000,000 or more, or the long, arduous route of self-development with all of the attending risks but a possible potential profit of $25.000.000- The Directors In their collective wisdom elected the fourth choice, primarily on investment grounds. The subsidiary borrows money from tile FCSU at 1% less than from the outside. However, that rate represents a 1% higher return than the FCSU could get through normal sources, thereby permitting the FCSU to project a very attractive rate of return to the annuity depositors. Cyrila a Metoda. Mnohí z nás si ešte dobre pamätáme vzrušenie a slávnostné ovzdušie, ktoré sme prežívali na tom istom mieste ako jenes. Vtedy však nová budova -stála uprostred voľného poľa. Dnes zelená záhrada a mnohé stromy naznačujú, že aj Ústav, ktorý vznikol zo skromných začiatkov a z drobnej obetavosti našich krajanov po celom svete, dnes prináša kvety a zrelé plody. Tak ako košaté pínie okolo Ústavu sú príbytkom pre nebeské vtáctvo, aj Ústav sa stal útočišťom, strediskom a ohniskom pre Slovákov z domoviny i z celého sveta, ohniskom viery, nádeje a lásky, "ohniskom náboženského života Slovákov", ako to prorocky predpovedal a predurčil Pavol VI. práve pred 25 rokmi. Dnes sme sa tu znovu zišli z rôznych strán sve'a, nie tak obdivovať stavbu a okolie Ústavu, ale pre tri hlavné ciele: ř— ďakovať Bohu za všetko, čo sa prostredníctvom a s pomocou Ústavu pre Slovákov vykonalo a zhodnotiť význam a vplyv Ústavu na duchovný život celého slovenského národa; — obnoviť si ideály a ducha, z ktorých sa Ústav zrodil a pre ktoré žije; — upřesnit’ si plány a predsavzatia do budúcnosti. A to všetko v radostnom duchu dnešnej liturgie, ktorá prekypuje povd’ačnosťou, vierou a nádejou. Chválospev, ktorý vytryskol z čistej pritom však plodnej duse mladej Matky Márie, nech je symfóniou, ktorá bude potichu zaznievať cez celú našu slávnosť, lebo naozaj, "velebí moja duša Pána — a môj duch jasá v Bohu, mojom Spasiteľovi— lebo veľké veci mi urobil ten, ktorý je mocný, — a sväté je jeho meno — a jeho milosrdenstvo z pokolenia na pokolenie s tými, čo sa ho boja". 1. V týchto slovách, je vlastne zbj^nitý.celý-program vu, ale aj program našich osláv. Ponajprv je to nadviazanie /na minulosť, na naše korene, z ktorých Ústav vyrástol a žije.
