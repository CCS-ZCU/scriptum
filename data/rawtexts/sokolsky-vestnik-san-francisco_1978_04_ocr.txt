Dnes bohužel naši mladí i starší krajané ztrácejí zájem o tělovýchovu, zatím co Američané začínají uvažovat, že sportem a tělocvikem si udržují zdraví. Dnes vidíme Američany provozovat tělocvik různým způsobem. Běhají dlouhé tratě, hrají tenis, plavou, pěstují zápasy i japonskou sebeobranu. V Americe sport a tělovýchova dostupuje záliby až možno říci vášně. Znalci zdravotnictví doporučují tělocvik a sport, který udržuje ~lepší zdraví i do budoucích dru. Američané hledají, physical fitness aby odolali nemocem a zvláště srdeční vadě, která tolik řádí v americkém životě. Americká mládež se zájmem sleduje champlony gymnastiky a snaží se je nápodobit. — Podle posledního Gallup Poll 1^.7 % Američanů provozuje nějaký sport nebo tělocvik a dvakráte tolik co bylo zaznamenáno v roce 1961. V minulosti americký student jakmile opustil vyšší školu zapomněl na tělocvik a sport, Stal se jen sportovním fanouškem s láhví piva v ruce. Dnes Američané začínají chápat Tyršovo heslo : „Ve zdravém těle zdraví duch ", Provozovat sport a tělocvik není však v Americe tak snadné.- Chcete hrát tenis, lyžovat, plavat, cvičit na nářadí, to všechno stojí dost peněz a není to na dosah ruky. Tělovýchovné americké organizace nemají dostatek tělocvičen a lékaři upozorňují, že jednotvárný sport, jako běhání na dlouhé tratě není tolik prospěšné tělu, ale naopak. Jiří Spánek [pageend1]Page 2 Věstník Sokola San Francisco April 1978 In Memoriom BROTHER JOSEPH POKORNY Our Sokol family in Lo b Angeles has again been saddened,this time by the passing of our beloved member, brother Joseph Pokorny. One by one our faithful members are departing from our midst, leaving us a legacy, the fruit of their many years of labor in our organization,—legacy of traditions. As we take over their duties, we are constantly mindful of their efforts and we are grateful. One of faithful members was brother Pokorny, or Joe as we called him. He dedicated his life to Sokol. In 1950 he came with his Wife Margaret to Los Angeles from Baltimore, where he was active in Sokol from his childhood. He immediately joined our unit. Joe was our instructor for 24 years, teaching calisthenics to all classes.He was Pacific District physical director and traveling instructor for several years. He also held the office of president and vicepresident of our unit for 2 years—and took active part in all committees as long as his health permitted. As a Sokol Member he fulfilled his oath in everyway. In 1969 an honorary membership was bestowed upon him. Joe lived a full life—87 years. Up to his 85th year he lived his life full of action,untiringly teaching drills, taking part in all our endeavors. He will be fondly remembered by all who knrw him. His memory will always be treasured among Sokol membership. With his passing, another era in our Sokol unit has ended. Brother Pokorny passed away April 4th in St.Joseph’s Hospital in Burbank.The funeral was held in Glendale with a large Sokol and CSa membership in attendance. We all wish him a peaceful rest. Věstník Sokola San Francisco Apríl 1978 Martina Navrátilova In sports and gymnastics, through the Sokol philosophy, we have been raised to follow the ideals of Tyrš. I know we are all Americans, however, we should not forget our Czech heritage and lose respect for our fatherland. Beyond sports and gymnastics, our heritage introduces us to other cultures, such as, music, as in the teaching and productivity of Smetana and Dvorak. When the foreign sportsman or artist comes to America, they receive a very warm welcome. I recently read with pleasure that, in Chicago and Cleveland, the Czech hockey team received a gracious reception and were accepted with great enthusiasm. They did not discuss the politics of their country, nor did we expect them to. With our friendly reception, we, in turn, expressed our open- mindedness in the democratic society in which we live. On Sunday, April 2, our family had the opportunity to visit the Oakland Coliseum to see the Virginia Slims Tennis Tournament and to personally see Martina Navratilova. Interest in our visit was two-fold; to enjoy a good match but 'also to see a tennis star of Czech origin who has been doing so well in her field. Among the large crowd, it came to me that there were many Czech Americans who had also come for the same reasons. Unfortunately, however, Martina's arrogant manner against our Czech Americans diminished our enthusiasm for her. After the match had ended, some fans and our children asked for her autograph in Czech to which she not only refused but with bitter remarks of annoyance. Perhaps her rise to fame and the American dollars have blinded her. In Sokol we are taught that a winner is not measured by his or her performance alone but he or she is judged more by their good qualities of conduct with regard to character and national pride. I recall when after one of the Olympic Games, an American athlete received a medal and during the National Anthem, he seemed very disinterested as well as disrespectful to the attention being given his country. I realize that in today's society, be it Czech or American, certain demands on stars, such as Martina Navratilova can be great. Constant harassment for autographs may be too much for her to handle, however, when she is asked in her own native tongue (namely Czech), she should at least share the pride of her fans or refuse with some graciousness, rather than an arrogant manner. It left our family with a bitter taste towards her and I feel it will be a long time before we make another trip anywhere to see her play. It's unfortunate we were all saddened by this experience; yet, our children today at least learned the difference what a real winner is all about. NAZDAR!
