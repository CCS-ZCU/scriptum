LENHART Realtor and Business Opportunity Broker 533-1404 843^4942 4142 Mar Arthur Blvd. Oakland, 9, California [pageend2]3 EDITORIAL We are glad bring you a summer edition of our Bulletin. In keeping with the mood of the seasons,you'11 find much -of its contents light-hearted,with stress on our summer activities,picnics. trips and such like. However, one does not stop thinking altogether in the summertime. A careful reader may notice a certain contradiction in this and the last issue. Two months ago you have read an article by our Director of Men regarding the future' of the Sokol movement in America. The most interesting and challenging of his opinions was the belief that the movement must integrate into the American io- ciety and try to attract members of American nationality. In todays article by our Educational Director you will find an expression of the opposing point of view.This is significant;not necessarily of some deep schism in our ranks,but rather of the importance of the issue. In a way, the future of Sokol in this country may depend on its solution. It is not difficult to see the strength of the arguments of both sides. From the end of the last century there was a steady flow of immigrants to the U.S.A, from the old country. As was pointed out, those people had strong ties with the old country and a very strong nationalistic feeling.Sokol for them was a connecting link with the past, a piece of home-away-from-home. This generation developed the movement to its present strength,worked hard toac- quire its assets, build its halls,etc. From those traditionalistic ranks will come most of the opposition against any "americani- . zation" of Sokol. The new generation (not necessarily in terms of age) is aware of its obligation to develop further, to strengthen what it is gradually taking over. However, since the fifties the flow of new immigrants dwindled to a trickle. Hardly anyone leaves the old country any more. Apart from the few people who come here from other foreign countries,the movement cannot look forward to a new influx of members from Czechoslovakia in the foreseeable future. Under the standing rules, the new members can only be the children of today's members and perhaps their families.providing their interest in Sokol can be maintained in the meantime. Any growth and strengthening of Sokol can hardly be visualized along the prospect of dwindling membership,leaving of the old guard, gradual americanization of the children. There are other dangers inherent in the present policy. To mention one, we must admit that there is a gradual falling off of the quality of technical leadership, which is getting out of date by being out of touch with the developments and methods worked out in other gymnastic centers of this country. The system needs to be re-vitalized, it needs new [pageend3]4 instructors of high calibre, new ideas, new blood. It can hardly hope to attract those by remaining a strictly nationalistic organization, Czech speaking and thinking, opposing jealously any in_ trusion of the world around it. Those are, very briefly, the two sides of the problem. And yet there does not seem to be any inherent contradiction between the basic ideas of the Sokol movement and the desire to modernize and integrate it into the wider framework of the life around us. Tyrš had himself said many times: " he who remains stationary has already fallen behind. " Development, change and progress are the very essence of the Sokol philosophy. It is then mainly the question of the nationalistic content which causes most of the dissent. Should Sokol remain predominantly Czech (or perhaps Slavonic),should it remain that home-away-from- home,even if it should mean a gradual atrophy of its strength because of the changed political situation? Is the nationalistic undertone, so important at the time Sokol was founded,equally important in the United States of America in the last decades of the 20th century? If the Sokol idea and system is so effective and beneficial for its members,are we not selfish in making it difficult for other nationals to attain membership instead of attracting them into our midst? Those are but few of the questions which could be asked. It would be interesting to hear from our readers how they look on this problem. It is extremely unlikely that any drastic change will come from the Congress in Chicago later on this month. And yet, this is a question with which we will have to come to grips seriously in the near future. OUR YOUNG "SOKOLS" [pageend4]SOKOLSKÝ SLET Sokolský Slet — jaká to kouzelná slova v sokolské historii! Ve staré vlasti slety bývaly nejen přehlídkou síly sokolské or- ganisace, ale i ukázkou vyspělosti tělovýchovy v národě. Také v Americe jsou sokolské slety přehlídkou naší organisace. Na našem XI. sletu v Chicagu uvidíme jaká je naše síla. Pro Tyrše idea sletů nebylo jen ukázat cvičení několika set cvičenců pro několik tisíc diváků. Tyrš chtěl, aby si na cvičišti cvičenci s diváky vyměnili místo, aby celý národ, až na nemluvňata a starce, prováděl tělesná cvičení. Od této myšlenky Miroslava Tyrše jsme zvláště v Americe ještě velmi daleko.Vidíme jak na několik desítek hráčů se přímo fanaticky dívá většina národa, čemuž říkají sport. Americká Obec Sokolská se snaží ukázati Američanům jak alespoň poněkud se můžeme přiblížiti snaze o zvýšení "Physical Fitness" celého národa. Ze San Francisca nas na slet do Chicaga pojede opravdu velmi málo. V prvé řadě je příliš veliká vzdálenost,v mnoha případech, opět chybí nadšení pro sokolskou věc. Americká Obec Sokolská bude jen tehdy silnou organisací, když každá malá jednota bude činná, a členové budou míti větší nadšení pro naši "sokolskou myšlenku". Před sletem v Chicagu bude se konati Valný Sjezd, na kterém delegáti všech jednot vyslechnou výsledky naší společné sokolské práce a vytýčí nové cesty do příštího období. Americké sokolstvo žilo 100 let a není důvodu, aby nemohlo jiti dále svou svéráznou cestou, aniž by bylo nuceno "zamerikanisovati" svou organisaci. Byl o to již několikráte učiněn pokus, a jistě bude učiněn i na tomto sjezdu. Naši delegáti ze Sokolské Zupy Pacifické jistě budou na stráži proti každému rozvrácení svéráznosti Sokolstva a útěk od Tyršovy myšlenky. Jiří Špánek ZDRAVÍME XI..
