The Job Training Partnership Act passed by both houses of Congress and signed into law last October, is designed to provide job training and related assistance to economically disadvantaged individuals, dislocated workers and others who face significant employment barriers. The ultimate goal of the Act is to move trainees into permanent self-sustaining employment. A program of enhanced and long-term job training services in this period of record- high unemployment is generally recognized as one of the most critical elements in our efforts to meet the employment challenges of the 1980’s and beyond. As a member of the House appropriations subcommittee responsible for funding these vital job training programs, I have been working to ensure that they are funded at a level which will provide meaningful employment opportunities to the many Americans who need them. Another important title of the new job training law is the dislocated worker program which will provide training and other assistance to workers who have been or expect to he laid off due to the permanent closing of a plant or industry. This title is also aimed toward the long-term unemployed who have little prospect for re-employment. The law also includes a summer youth employment and training program which is expected to provide 718,000 summer jobs for youth nationwide in 1984 alone. This law, in particular, has been structured to capitalize or. the experience and resources of the private sector where the jobs are, and it is hoped that the Job Training Partnership Act will become a model for future public/private partnerships in an area which is vital to our future growth and prosperity as a nation.
