Jindřich FÚGNER—TYRŠ’S COLLABORATOR J. Fú g n e r was born in 1822. At first he was engaged in business. He became the managing director of an insurance company and eventually a bank director in Prague. He was a genuine and enlightened democrat who hated despotism, and he infused the Sokol with his own sterling qualities of character. He advocated the principle of equality of rights and succeeded in making the Sokol an organisation of brothers who treated each other alike, regardless of his position in life. The statutes, the manifestos, the speeches and all the other activities of the Sokol were planned and arranged by Fiigner in such a way that they became accessible to the masses and congenial to the character of the Czechoslovak people. By these democratic principles Fiigner assured a favourable reception for the Sokols wherever they appeared. He was also responsible for initiating a number of national movements and for founding valuable cultural organisations. Although he did not live long with his Sokol brothers, he left his stamp on the whole movement and is rightly considered as one of its founders. The first Czech Sokol gymnasium, which was distinguished by its purposefulness as well as its beauty, was built out of the funds provided by Fiigner, who devoted his large fortune to the great Sokol idea.
