EDITORIAL REFLECTIONS ON AUGUST 21, 1980 — Thoughts on our situation Last week the twelfth anniversary of the invasion of Czechoslovakia was commemorated by a few people. The details you have heard about elsewhere - they are not important to this article. What is important is the fact that the Czechoslovaks in attendance were outnumbered 3 to 1 by the Poles and other nationalities who came to show support for us. The question begs to be asked — why? When world attention is once more focused on East Central Europe why do our people refuse to take a stand in support of our homeland? Fear, apathy, - it can be blamed on many things - but this passive surrender to the turn of events only serves to promote avoidance of the issues at hand. Unity among the East Central European nations is their only hope. However, if we who are in the free world cannot unite - what then? We have become complacent. We think and strive only after our own comforts. We want wealth, luxuries, security. Simply thinking about the Czechoslovak situation, simply acknowledging its existence makes us uncomfortable. Many of us claim not to be interested in politics. That is our way out. This, to me, is the most absurd thinking of all. The political sphere always has and always will affect our day-to- day lives. To deny this is truly a grave mistake. For how long can one ignore that which touches one’s life in such a constant fashion? Other peoples of other countries may be forgiven such muddled reasoning, but we of Czech descent, - for us I am afraid there is no easy excuse. Our nation has suffered much. It is our responsibility to bear witness to that suffering even though it may be a personal burden. It is all well and good to keep repeating Masaryk's motto that •'Truth Will Prevail", but unless we ourselves keep that truth alive, no number of verbal repetitions will actualize his motto. I do not seek to recriminate, I simply wish to pose questions which will hopefully make us all think about and discuss those ideals we hold to be true. Z.B.B. [pageend1]Strana-Page 2. Věstník Sokola San Francisco Zari-September ig^ Commemorative eveningi On Saturday August 23r there was held a commemorative evening of the occupation of Czechoslovakia in 1968 in our Sokol hall. At the same occasion there was a tribute paid to the memory of two Czech martyrs who chose death rather than abandon their democratic views. They are Dr. Miladě Horáková and General Heliodor Pika. Political events leading to the August 1968 invasion were dealt with in English by the first speaker who as an army officer of the Czechoslovak Army in Exile also personally knew Gen. Heliodor Pika, about whom he also delivered a speech in Czech at closing stage of the first half of program. Sister Z.B.B. delivered a beautiful address about Dr Milada Horáková in English. In Czech this subject was covered by br. Jan Turek. In the second half of the program the musical and entertainment medley was skillfully prepared by our devoted countryman from Lake Tahoe, world-wide known Eduard Ingris with his charming wife and son Eduard Jr., now fourteen years of age. He is at this tender age a veritable virtuoso on the piano and we know that there is a great career in store for him. The delivery and enthusiasm with which he played he won over all of us. It was a great pleasure to hear again Hasler’s songs beautifully rendered on tape by our countryman Jarda Zvolanek which were synchronized with projected slides of old Prague. The pictures were truly artistic and unforgetable. Those of us who know Prague refreshed our memories by viewing these pictures. The accompanying music and commentary was superbly handled by Eduard Ingris Sr. As a finale the Ingris family prepared a medley of about forty folk songs which encouraged the audience to join in a hearty sing-along. It was really a beautiful and interesting evening. It is a pity, that the attendance was rather low. You sisters, brothers and countrymen who have chosen to spend that Saturday evening otherwise and somewhere else you have a reason to be sorry. The executed heroes and our national cata- strophy, all this deserved more attention from you. Signed: J.T. Bro.
