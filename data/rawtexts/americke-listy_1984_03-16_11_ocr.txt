Cekoty “Z dalekých zahrad" kapitola “Existence” Continuing problems in the procedures by which social security disability cases are reviewed has prompted a great deal of concern in the Congress where legislation is now pending to restore uniformity, stability and fairness to this vital program of support to people who cannot work because of serious physical or mental disability. Shortly after taking office in 1981, the President ordered that benefits of social security disability beneficiaries be terminated while their cases were under review. This action resulted in the termination of benefits for 470,000 individuals, many of whom were truly disabled; nearly two-thirds of the beneficiaries who have appealed the administration-ordered terminations have had their benefits restored. In response to this crisis, the Congress enacted temporary reform legislation which provided for face-to-face hearings during the first appeals level and that benefit payments would continue to be paid through to the hearings by the administrative law judge. This law expired, however, December 7, 1983, and the Congress is prepared to enact a more comprehensive disability package, the “Social Security Disability Amendments,” which has been approved by the House Ways and Means Committee and is ready for floor action. The administration, however, opposes this legislation, believing that legislative remedies are not necessary and on February 2 of this year, instructed states to resume terminating benefits for beneficiaries whose cases are under review. The need for legislative solution to the crisis in the social disability insurance program is clear, and I strongly support the legislation now ready for action in the Congress to t eform the present review procedure. The House bill would also continue benefits for individuals whose terminations are under appeal and insure immediate face-to-face interviews with individuals whose benefits are to be terminated. Congress has a responsibility to act quickly on this legislation. The President should rescind his order that benefits under review be terminated while Congress is addressing this issue. We all have a responsibility to see that a program as essential as disability is run properly, and, most important, fairly and compassionately for those Americans who desperately need it.
