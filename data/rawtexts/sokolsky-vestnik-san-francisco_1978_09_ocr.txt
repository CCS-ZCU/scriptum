September-Září 1978 Číslo-No 9 FROM THE EDITOR: It is very simple to produce a bulletin such as this. All you have to do is get some money together (which is easy) and promise Sokol that your bulletin is self-supporting. Then you have to get some articles together which you simply do by begging, blackmailing and if all else fails, you write it yourself. Then you edit the stuff and try to find some good soul to help translate what you have written from Czech to English or you can attempt to do it yourself and this is where your trouble starts. You see, when you have lived outside of Czechoslovakia for over 50 years, you have a tendency to forget some of these terrible declinations of Czech words. When you have never really adapted to the spelling of the English language, after living in America for a while, it shows when you go to write it. After you have set up your titles, you take what you have accumulated to the printing shop. When you get home, you try to relax and as you begin to fall into a nice sleep, you start to have nightmares about dispelling one of the words in the IBulletiu. For example, instead of spelling "weekend” you typed "weeking.” So you jump out of your chair in a panic and call the girl at the printing shop to see if she can correct your mistake before they start running 500 copies of that page. After that episode, all that is left to do is correlating the pages, folding them all up and mailing them. . . Then, as you attempt to relax for the SECOND time, subconsciously you are waiting for the critics to start complaining. Of course, the good critics with something nice to say are welcome but the members who decide to start criticizing all your hard work are the ones who don't even contribute a word in the bulletin and start ridiculing your Czech language. They will notice an "I" mistakenly typed for a ”Y' but you would hope they would notice that the theme of the article was that the hall needed painting and that next Saturday we are commemorating the Invasion of Czechoslovakia by the Soviet army.
