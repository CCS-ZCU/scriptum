One such man was Jan Pavel of Branch L99. the Holy Trinity Society, tn Hostetter. On May 19.1928 an explosion ripped through a mine in Mather. Green County. Pa., killing 195 miners. It was the worst mine explosion of the decade. Jan Pavel belonged to the H.C. Frick Mine Rescue Team stationed In Mt. Pleasant, several miles from Mather. Pavels rescue team received the emergency call at 8:00 p.m. and went immediately to the scene. By the time they arrived, the area had been completely sealed off by police. Pavel’s team was supposed to enter the mine at 11:45 that evening to begin rescue operations, but a fire was still raging out of control, it was not until several hours later that they were able to enter. Their hopes were high of finding many miners alive, but they were soon disappointed. For tire rest of the night they waited outside, listening to the crying and to trucks bringing blankets for the injured Later in the rooming of May 20. Pavel and the team reentered the shaft They found several miners but all were heyond hope. They worked all day. and finally in the afternoon they found one miner, a Slovak, still alive. According to the Parents Resources Institute for Drug Education (PRJDE): "in 1960 less than one percent of American teenagers had ever tried marijuana or any other illicit drug.
