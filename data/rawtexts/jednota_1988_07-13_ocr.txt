Illusions and Realities PRESIDENT REAGAN & TOTALITARIANISM Michael Novak No doubt President Reagan is seeing something In the Soviet Union he never thought he'd live to see; The open question whether a totalitarian so- ■'ftety can change its stripes. .' Can the totalitarian USSR actually become a plain, ordinary "authoritarian society." a garden-variety tyranny, as General Secretary Gorbachev . seems to desire? Benito Mussolini, in 1925. was the first leader to use the word “totalitarian" In proclaiming his own alms. He envisaged a new type of society, unprecedented In history, committed to the power of a single will — "ferocious will." he called it. He contrasted this with "decadent." hesitant liberal societies and the timid, narrow dictatorships of the past. He counted on the cheering "mobilization of the masses” and the highly disciplined "organization” of the "party elite." especially its young He had studied Lenin's practices, of course, as Hitler was later to study Mussolini's. In the 1930s. the first Western analysts of this new type of power rested their hypotheses on empirical fact: what Lenin, Stalin and Mussolini had done, and what Adolf Hitler was promising to do. They observed Stalin's deliberate starvation of 8-12 million peasants ("kulaks”) In the man-made famine ofUkrainiain the early thirties. They watched Stalin. Mussolini. and the rise of the Third Reich. One of the new facts they noted In the new totalitarianism was its total abrogation of traditional ("bourgeois") morality. Religions such as Judaism and Christianity were held in explicit contempt, and tradi- tional virtues and moral principles were explicitly spumed.
