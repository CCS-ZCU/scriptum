The Slovak Archbishop Jozef Tomko, who is the General Secretary of the World Synod of Bishops in Rome, was Invited to the United States to take part in the 50th Convention of the Slovak Catholic Federation. He wrote to the Editor of his planned trip and that it was his Intention to atop for a day at the Jednota Press in Middletown, Pa. in tribute to the BOtii Anniversary of the JEDNOTA. He wanted to show witness that the Vatican supports the mission of the Slovak Catholic press and recognizes the 90th Anniversary of the JEDNOTA. Joining Archbishop Tomko on April 21, 1981 in the editorial office of the Jednota Press were two bishops of the Harrisburg Diocese — the Most Reverend Joseph T. Daley and the Most Reverend William II. Keeler; the Right Reverend Abbot Jerome Koval, O.S.B., Protector of the First Catholic Slovak Union from Cleveland, Ohio; and Mother M. Hiey paid tribute to the Society's contribution to the lay and religious apoštoláte in the diocese since 1911. Would that it be so easy 1 Sins are not just so many things to pick up and pitch out. Sin is a matter of rejected relationship, broken threads of love, and a damaged, if not. dead self. "God shows his love for us In that while we were yet sinners Christ died for us." (Rom. 6:8, 10). Lent can be more profitable , provided we begin by acknowledging that God's love reaches, out for us and Its first gift is forgiveness. "Only God can forgive sins." (Mk. 2:6). To recognize God's willingness to forgive our sins is Itself part of the gift of faith in forgiveness of sins. In his masterful and delightful conversations with the Samaritan woman at Jacob’s well, Jesus himself poses the central point of( God’s self-disclosure: “If you linew the gift of God, and who It is that is saying to you, . . " (John 4:10), Carefully Jesus raises the consciousness of the Samaritan woman, from the matter of quenching one's thirst with water, to acknowledging her sinful life and lastly affirming Jesus's own disclosure that he is "the Christ." The discussion comes to a close with the Samaritans — the woman and her village neighbors — calling Jesus the "Savior" of the world. This gospel text from John, one of the many texts read In the liturgical celebration of Lent, is a fine preparation for baptism as well as continuing one’s conversion from sin. The core of the Lenten preparation Is not sin and its repentance, but God’s love äs it is "gift-given" to us in Jesus's death and resurrection by baptism. It is true that the saving gift of God begins with a person being cleansed from his-her sins.
