The number of single-parent families in the United States has nearly doubled in the last 15 years. While many parents meet their financial obligations, millions fail to provide courtordered support. In 1981. only 47', of the 4 million single parents (primarily women, entitled to child support payments were receiving the full amount due. Congress has moved to help the children of single parents by strengthening the government program which is designed to help collect support money from the absent parent. The new Child Support Amendments, which take effect October 1st. provide incentives to stage agencies to do a -better job by using proven enforcement techniques and providing equal service for welfare and non-welfare families. The new law also improves interstate enforcement. To obtain an order for child support, the child support enforcement agency or lawyer — with the assistance of the custodial parent — must locate the absent parent, ascertain his or her ability to pay and determine the financial needs of the child. The new law requires states to use certain proven enforcement techniques, such as mandatory wage withholding when support payments arc delinquent ; expedited judicial or administrative procedures for establishing and enforcing child suppoit obligations; interceptions of state income tax refunds; liens against property; posting of bonds, and reporting to consumer credit agencies. The law also redesigns the financial incentive program to encourage States to operate more effectively and efficiently. The Child Support Enforcement Amendments are designed to help any person caring for a child who cannot get needed child support assistance from the natural parent. The service is free to any family receiving public assistance pyaments. For other families, the state may charge a small application fee — $20 or less — and ask for payment for administrative costs.
