That document contained significant human rights provisions calling for the free flow of ideas and information between East and West. As subsequent events have shown, the communist countries, and Czechoslovakia in particular, never intended to carry out the provisions of the so-called Third Basket which would protect human rights from arbitrary government action. A year after putting its signature on the Final Act, the Czechoslovak government continues to persecute those who refuse to be ‘normalized’ or ‘consolidated.’ The Czechoslovak government has granted permits for reunification of families in only a few cases. Bureaucratic red tape impedes visits of relatives to and from, Czechoslovakia. And the situation in other countries of the communist bloc is not too different. Most politicians in the West are very slow to speak up against these violations of human rights by the totalitarian countries. But some do and one man who deserves our praise in this respect is Daniel Patrick Moynihan, former U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations. He became a hero and a household, word during his brief but exciting tenure at Turtle Bay. While others may believe that the only proper way to deal with the enemies of democracy is caution and “diplomacy,” Moynihan did not hesitate to call a spade a spade. While our administration at times seems to hesitate to do anything that might upset the Kremlin leaders - - as, for example, the unfortunate decision to snub Solzhenitsyn during his visit here - - Moynihan spoke up bluntly. While others are reluctant to answer those totalitarian governments and movements which attack democratic countries and feign to give them lectures on democracy, Moynihan did not hesitate to tell the truth about what is going on in the United Nations where, for some delegates, “war is peace, slavery is liberty and injustice is justice.” And the American people listened. Moynihan has shown that the people are ready to hear what many self-seeking politicians and diplomats arc not ready to say. Because of his courage, knowledge and vision, Daniel Patrick Moynihan has emerged as one of the central figures in the current debate over the future of American fort ign policy.
