I am more than delighted that the 75th anniversary celebration for San Francisco Sokols was such an outstanding success. Your excellent leadership and hard work has brought great results. I wish to add how much I appreciate you sending the monthly Vestnik so I am enclosing my small contribution for the coming year. Our small group of The American-Czech senior club who in the past years, were the Sokols of Portland, Oregon, extend their best wishes to the Sokols of San Francisco. I showed the members the souvenir program you sent. It was of great interest to them. It was a sad day when they sold the Sokol Hall in the late 40's but the members were determined to carry on the meeting once a month. After 50 years or so, many of the members passed on. We still are eager to meet, to get together in our small way. We are not able to be outstanding gymnasts but deep in their heart the Czech spirit is there. We have a large community room which holds about 75 people. We have our buffet dinners and a wide range of programs with movies, slides of Czechoslavakia and any Czech events that come along are observed. I am the art director and plus the fact I was voted in as the new president for the coming year. If you have anything of interest to send to our members sometime, it would be appreciated. At The Czechoslovak National Council of America, October event, I was greatly surprised with a plaque award for the many years I have contributed as secretary and art director and working with the Czech refugee families. I felt quite honored but think the president, Giny Jackson, should share this honor with me as we worked together for many years. I was just talking to John and Mary Martin. John has been very poorly . He can hardly get around now. They too, are sending you their best wishes. < To wish you the best Christmas and Happy New Year.' Always - Mrs. Camile Bain LETTER FROM SOKOL FRESNO Well, here it is already 1980! Fresno wishes all the Sokols and friends a Happy and busy New Year! We did not have our traditional Christmas party but joined the Croatian Group which had a Santa and dinner party. So it turned out great for us. More fun and less fuss. Our New Year's Eve party was held way out in the boondocks, next to nowhere. I am just kidding. As it turned out to be a great party! Tt was some miles out in the country to the east of town but when we got there we had a very pleasant surprise. The hall was very unusual. Beside the smooth dance floor, we had a huge fireplace to sit around and a lovely mountain scene in one corner. The waterfall was not turned on but the Christmas trees, the squirrels, chipmunks, birds and other wild life were beautifully illuminated. There was also a big fancy chandelier and all the Christmas decorations were still up. Truly it was worth going the extra distance to find such an unusual hall. Everyone got home safely but the fog rolled in towards the morning. In the Spring, we hope to join the City Valley Hall Tournaments. Our young people are in college and getting married so we older members are hanging together with a love that binds us as Sokols. Roku 1912 tak jako mnoho mladých Moraváku v tehdejší době šel na řemeslo kožešnické do Vídně. V roce 1916 byl odveden na vojnu a prodělal válečná taženi na několika rakouských frontách. - V roce 1916 byl zraněn a léčil se v nemocnici Baden u Vídně.
