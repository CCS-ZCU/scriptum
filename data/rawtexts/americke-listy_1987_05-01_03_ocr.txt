Rakouská veřejnost, rakouské sdělovací prostředky a i rakouská úřední místa hodnotí návštěvu šéfa sovětské komunistické strany v Československu s neobvyklou pozorností. Ale přesto si jsou zdejší reakce zajedno, že úspěch Gorbačovových kontaktů s obyvatelstvem je jednak důsledkem sympatií pro jím ohlašované reformy, a jednak důsledkem zvědavosti, vidět nejvyššího sovětského funkcionáře, který, alespoň slovy vyhlašuje něco, co se podobá pokusu reformátorů z roku 1968 v Československu. PŘÍLEŽITOST K DOSAŽENÍ DOHODY Congresmann Bernard J. Dwyer reports . . . The U.S. merchandise trade deficit rose sharply to $15.1 billion in February, according to figures recently released by the Commerce Department. This is the third largest monthly increase in the trade deficit on record, and is almost $3 billion higher than the figure reported for January. The continued deterioration of the U.S. position in international trade must be addressed. The Congress will act soon on a major trade reform bill, designed to increase this country's competitiveness in the world market. I am cosponsoring this important legislation. The merchandise trade deficit measures the excess in value of total imports over the value of total exports. In January 1981, the American merchandise trade 'deficit for the year just ended (1980) stood at $36.3 billion. Last year the deficit soared to an all-time record of $169.8 billion. Earlier this yaer, in testimony before my House Appropriations Subcommittee, the U.S. Trade Representative, Ambassador Clayton Yeutter stated that we are beginning to see the fruits of this Administration's economic and trade efforts. He spoke of "moving swiftly and aggressively to open foreign markets to U.S. products and services". Unfortunately, this has not happened. The United States is the world's largest economy, the largest market, the leading exporter and importer. With 5 percent of the world's population, the U.S. produces 22 percent of the world's industrial goods. However, we cannot compete with subsidized foreign industries, low wage scales, and overburdening import restrictions on our goods. The United States wants to compete fairly. One of the first bills introduced in this new session of Congress was H.R. 3, the ":Trade and International Economic Policy Reform Act of 1987." This bill, which has strong support in the House, seeks to improve this country's competitiveness in world markets and to address the ever-increasing treade deficit. As introduced, this legislation was almost identical to legislation approved by the House last year. H.R. 3 has eight titles dealing with trade remedy reform, negotiating authority, telecommunications trade, export enhancement, international financial matters, education and training programs and agricultural trade.
