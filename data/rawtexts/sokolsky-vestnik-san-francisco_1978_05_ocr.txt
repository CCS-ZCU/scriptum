In every monthly meeting, some of our officers are asking for the help that is necessary for the function of our organization. But the appeal is directed to the few members attending the meeting. They are members that are already fully involved in work for the unit and they hardly can be asked for additional duties. Our SOKOL BULLETIN is also frequently challenging the members to step forward and offer a helping hand to their unit which they belong to. The response is usually negative and so those few faithful and responsible members are carrying their load over and over again. We can see the same faces working in the same places every time we have some activity going on. Others rather enjoy being entertained and served by members of the same organization and of course everyone likes to feel free of duties and responsibilities while enjoying the dance or other programs. This includes even the dedicated and hard working members and this could be easily achieved if others offered to help just once in a while. Membership participation is the essential ingredient in the activities of any organization. It is the members that decide the success or failure. Everyone should help sometime. Membership duties should just not be filled by paying dues. There is so much to do-so many different jobs and there are many different committees where your skills and knowledge would be welcomed and put to good use. Don't always let somebody else do the job. Just step forward and let yourself be counted into the working group of the Sokol Organization. I hope that someday you will find it in your heart to become aware of the fact that your Sokol needs you and that the life and activity depends on you as well as the other members. Or, if you don't care at all, and you think that this national and ethical organization is not even worth lifting a finger over, then you can follow the following 10 Commandments. 1. Do not work at all if you can help it. 2. Stay away from all the meetings. 3. Find fault with any suggestion and never give your own alternative or solution. 4. Consider all meetings, dances, shows and programs as a waste of money. 5. Decline office or appointments. 6. After you're named, don't attend board or committee meetings. 7. Get upset and resign if your idea is not accepted. 8. If you do attend a meeting, remain silent throughout it. 9. Complain that the meetings are dull and the officers are old fashioned. 10. The most important thing is not to pay your dues. If you obey these,you'll succeéd at what you were trying to do - then what? Jan Kučera [pageend3]Page 4. Věstník Sokola San Francisco May 197$ COLLECTORS1 CORNER Collectibles that don't cost you a penny, and why you should collect them Cancelled stamps: Any stamp you can lick is acceptable. Also, machine stamps and foreign stamps. They must be cut with approximately 1/4" of paper Left around the stamp, he stamps are sterilized, removed, repacked and sold. They must have the paper around them to work with and protect the stamp, WHY: During World War II there was a serious outbreak of TB among the children of Norway. They found a way of taking cancelled stamps and turning them into $$$$. A hospital was opened for the children with TB and respiratory ailments. With the decrease of sick youngsters and the increase of this project, they now include adults. This project is coordinated through the Sons of Norway. Computer Prints: When clipping these, be sure to include any number or letter under, around, over the computer print. WHY: 100 computer prints buys one minute of time on a kidney machine or respirator for a child located somehwere in the U.S, Currently, we have two children in California benefiting from this program. Coupons: ( including out-dated ones) WHY: These are utilized for various projects of the Mentally Retarded and the DAV, At present, they are striving for the purchase of a Braille Printing Machine. Some newspapers are willing to print news in braille, but cannot afford to purchase a machine. There is one newspaper printing one page of news in braille. They are trying to make it more. Two projects made possible by coupon collections have been (1) Bakery (2) Refinishing and upholstery business. Both presently operating at a profit and independently. Training of personnel and all the equipment necessary for these projects came through the collection of coupons. Added note: The senior citizens of Tulare County spend one day a week sorting coupons. They have a pot luck lunch and the bakery personnel bring goodies for dessert and eat with them. I can't describe the warmth felt in the area of the heart as you quickly see your collections growing and realize the good results from such a small effort on your part. Don't you want to be a collector also?
