Thomas Garrigue Masaryk President Liberator of Czechoslovakia The principal figure in the creation of the Czechoslovak Republic after World War I was Thomas Garrigue Masaryk. Of humble origin, Masaryk, by virtue of his own diligence and perseverance, was a Professor of Philosophy, a humanitarian, a politician and finally, at the age of 68, the first President of a free and independent Czechoslovakia. He was an uncompromising foe in the fight for truth and a champion of the realistic dream which he saw fulfilled during his lifetime: The rebirth of an independent and free nation. The first President of Czechoslovakia is already known as the "President Liberator" and Champion of Liberty" and as such was honored when the United States Postal Service issued a commemorative stamp in 1960. Masaryk believed in God and Justice and, although not a churchgoer, throughout his life advocated religious tolerance and the right to worship according to the beliefs of the individual. He deplored Communist philosophy and was quick to condemn Marxism-Leninism. He defended the rights of every individual within the law, the equality of all men of all races, of all religious beliefs and all social classes. He valued each person solely by human qualities: Sincerity, unselfishness and the desire to follow the path of truth, no matter the final consequence. The twenty years of a truly democratic Czechoslovakia were the result of his unceasing efforts. He died shortly before World War II, before Czechoslovakia fell victim to political appeasements; before the tragic Munich Treaty. He was, and remains still, the spokesman for free Czechoslovakia and for all people who believe in an intensely human and moral philosophy, in the insistence of strictest integrity, and in championing self-government and democracy based on respect for the dignity of each human being and national self-determination. Let Thomas Masaryk forever be remembered as a symbol of Humanity, Democracy and Freedom with his adopted motto "Truth Prevails." [pageend1]SOKOL VĚSTNÍK February 197^.
