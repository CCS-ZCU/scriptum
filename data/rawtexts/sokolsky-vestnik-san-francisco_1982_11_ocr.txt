Večer se sestrou Marii Provazníkovou Dne 27. listopadu v sedm hodin večer pořádá Sokol San Francisco večer se sestrou Marií Provazníkovou, poslední náčelnicí Čs. Sokola ve svobodném Československu. Přijďte si poslechnout a podívat na stále svěží a čilou sokolku a sami se přesvědčíte, co zdravý duch ve zdravém těle dokáže. Ačkoliv je to veřejným tajemstvím, neprozradím její věk poněvadž nemám její svolení. Sestra Provazníkové nám poví mnoho zajímavostí o sokolském hnutí a odpoví na všechny otázky buďto v naší mateřštině nebo v angličtině. Roztelefonujte tuto zprávu všem vašim přátelům, aby nezmeškali tuto jedinečnou příležitost seznámit se se žijící kronikou nejen Sokola, ale i pamětnicí a blízkou spolupracovnicí našich velikánů, o nichž dovede poutavě vyprávět. Tabulka zápasů: [pageend2]To him the history of mankind is an eternal struggle for survival to which must succumb and perish all that is unfit to live and detrimental to the whole. Every nation perished only through its own fault. The ultimate fate of nations is not decided on the battlefields, but before the battle by the inner quality of the nations. "Not one single nation of all that once were upon this earth, whether numerous or not, did ever perish in its prime, but succumbed to its fate when depraved, indifferent and rejected." In support of this theory Tyrs pointed to the ancient Greece that preserved its independence against tremendous odds, only by its physical fitness, spiritual maturity and moral qualities, closely associated with boundless love of one's country and liberty. This is what Tyrs wanted his country to become. He wanted to give it spiritual and moral maturity, which would secure its future, 1116 nation's renaissance was already secure in all fields of human endeavor, But it lacked physical excellence, the necessary basis for fighting power, together with organizational ability, respect for discipline and cooperation and the ability to set aside individual interests to the interests of the entire nation. In this Tyrs found his place in the realm of national endeavors. This was a position he could fill better than anyone else. Through his experience in the various gymnastic institutes he realized that physical exercise does not affect merely one's physical well being, but in the hands of a good instructor it can affect the development of one's character and his social attitudes. He saw how the working together in the various teams, their mutual efforts for progress, their various contests, was helping to develop unity out of individuals and how these common interests help to surmount all social, educational and other differences. For these reasons Tyrs selected gymnastics as the means for trai ning the nation in the very qualities necessary for its preservation and complete fulfillment. In this line of thinking Tyrs found exceptional understanding in a truly rare man he had met in the Bartelmus family circle. This was Jindřich Fugner, a Prague merchant, who used to spend his summer vacation in a game keeper's lodge not far from Jáchymov. This noble, broadminded man and convinced democrat, in spite of his German upbringing, was well aware of the excellence of the Czech nation and he felt the injustice it suffered under the Austrian yoke. He alligned himself wholeheartedly to the country where he lived, and also gave much thought to the problem of how he could help this nation. Thus the efforts of the two men met on a common ground. Fugner agreed readily with plans advanced by Tyrs. They both returned to Prague in the fall of 1861 just at the time when plans were being considered for the formation of a Czech Gymnastic Organization. The two men reached an agreement with Dr. Edward Gregr and Professor Emanuel Tonner, and with their well-thought out program they quickly assumed the leadership of the organization then being formed.
