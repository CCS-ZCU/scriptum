The federal government last year also proposed a formula for taxing the fraternals. The NFCA. with its Law Committee, plus fraternal leaders, scored a shoutout. In a most recent development, the monumental efforts of the NFCA member-societies licensed in the State of Florida were successful In deleting a provision In Florida House Bill 363 and Senate Bill 1054 that would have Imposed a 2% premium tax on fraternal benefit societies. It clarifies the difference between fraternal benefit societies and commercial Insurers. This clarification aids fraternal societies and insurance regulators in determining which activities are appropriate or inappropriate for fraternal organizations to engage in, without threatening their tax-exempt status." stated Senator O'Pake. "This legislation further provides that agents orfraternal benefit socities must have the same qualifications, education, and testing as commercial insurance agents.
