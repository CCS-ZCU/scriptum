I think now it is an appropriate time to present here a synopsis of a carreer of a lady who came to our Sokol hall whenever she found some time in her overcrowded schedule. oona Červena, the Czechoslovak mezzo-soprano who made her American debut with the san Prancisco Opera as Carmen in 1962, returned to the local Opera forthe tenth season to sing grandmother Buryja in Jenufa, Flora in La Traviata, Adelaide in Arabella and Mamma Lucia in Cavalleria nusticana. she has performed more than twenty-five roles here, including Azucena in 11 Trovatore, Countess Geschwitz in Lulu, Louise’s mother in Louise and Herodias in Salome along with such comic parts as the Countess Berkenfeld in La Figlia del reggimento, Mother Goose in The Rake’s Progress, Mistress quickly in Falstaff, Berta in Die Fledermaus and Martha in Faust. Miss Červená spent three years with the Prague Voskovec and Werich "Osvobozené divadlo", where she created among others the very popular song "Jakpak je dnes u nás doma?" with Jan Werich and bandleader Karel Vlach, before beginning her career as a singer at the Janáček Opera in Brno. Guest appearances followed at the National Theater in Prague in such roles as Carmen and Cherubino. Subsequently, she became a member of the State Opera in Berlin and was granted the title of Kammersangerin by the company following her portrayal of the title role in Gluck’s Orfeo ed Euridice. She has been heard as guest artist in London, Vienna, Milan, Zfirich, Geneva, Lisbon, Moscow and Warsaw and throughout Germany. Miss Červená has performed at the festivals of Bayreuth, Salzburg, Glyndebourne, Edinburgh, Wexford, Ghent, Amsterdam and Schwetzingen. Currently she is a member of the Frankfurt Opera. IN MEMORIAL Gustave BOSÁK— Gustave Bosák was born Augustin František Bosák in Uherské Hradiště, Moravia, Czechoslovakia on February 21, 1898. He spent three years in Vienna, Austria, learning the fur trade, graduating in 1915. After the W.W.I. years, he pursued this profession in Belgium. In 1923, he arrived in the U.S.A,, city of New York to work for á large French fur establishment, kt that time, he met Emily who was also from Uherské Hradiště and they enjoyed many happy times at the New York Sokol Hall. In 1925, Gustave came to San Francisco to work for a Czech furrier, Mr. Buben. Emily arrived soon after and they were married on September 12, 1925. Dpon the passing of Mr. Buben, Gustave joined Io Magnin Co. as head of the fur department for 32 years, retiring in 1968.
