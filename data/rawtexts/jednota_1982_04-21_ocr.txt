Stories by the scholarly writer were drawn from all walks of life in Slovakia and his historical research in Trenčín and Trnava brought to life many interesting occurences from the past. That was the question posed recently to Cardinal Anastasio Ballestreo of Turin by the Italian Association for Geliacic Children, an organization dealing with the problem of youngsters who cannot digest glutens, the plant proteins found In many cereal grains. especially wheat. The answer, said the cardinal through an archdiocesan spokes man, Ues not in hosts made from com or rice, but In a sip of consecrated wine. "Our children cannot tolerate glutens,” said Marlo Taddqi, president of the.association, In a. letter to Cardinal BaUestrero. "This Illness requires the absolute suspension of all foods with glutens (pasta, bread, etc.), including the hosts normally used in churches,” the letter added. The archdiocesan reply came In a letter from Father Aldo Marengo, director of the liturgy office. "In such cases, instead of the host a little consecrated wine can bo provided and offered to the communicants while pronouncing the words, body and blood of Christ,'" Father Marengo said. "Bread of corn or rlco, however, is not permitted.” Celiac disease, which can cause nevere Intestinal problems and nutritional disorders, is not unusual among children one to two years old. Usually it disappears within a few months to a year, but in some cases it persists for years, up to and past the age for first Communion.
