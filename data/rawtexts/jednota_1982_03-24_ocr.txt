LI Christopher Motto Feeding the Hungry - A Million Times Over Suppose you received an Inspiration from on high to feed the hungry? More specifically, a voice from heaven urged you to provide a million meals to needy people in five years. How would you do it? Could it be done? Yes! In fact It has been done, many times over, by the St. Vincent de Paul Society. -, Founded 160 years ago to provide the pooY with food, clothing and other basic needs, the society keeps a low profile. There are about 700,000 members worldwide, 36,000 of them In the United States: executives, blue collar workers, retirees, people in all walks of life. In Oakland, Cal., alone, the St. Vincent de Paul Society served more than a million meals in the last five years. The 300 members constructed and now support a dining facility*where 800 full nutritious meals are served seven days a week free to anyone in need. A crew of about 200 dedicated volunteers contribute their time, assisting Brother Victor Nolan of the Little Brothers of the Good Shepherd who has been administering the program since 1975. Although most of the food Is donated, the cost still runs about $12.000 a month. The Society raises this amount and more. What's special about the Oakland dining facility is that it 1b In a suburban area, and most of the people who come are senior citizens and the unemployed. I discovered this operation on a «rent trip to the coast, and was so impressed I had to share my admiration with you For years I had seen how the St. Vincent de Paul Society does its charitable work, quietly and unceremoniously, how members reach out in friendship to the poor and needy without regard for race, creed or religion. Now having travelled a bit I have a better grasp of their presence as. a national and international force for good. They help singles, family members, those who can’t afford a meal, the unemployed seeking work, the broken families without a wage earner, the elderly who can't make It on low fixed incomes, the discouraged new arrivals to a city without family or friends. It's all done as a volunteer service by generous human beings who love God very much. The Christophers salute the St. Vincent de Paul Society. On arms control, the wisdom now conventional among a majority of Catholic theologians tends to reject the "just war" theory, certainly with respect to nuclear weapons and, In some cases, even with respect to other contemporary wars (except "wars of national liberation," which tend to be justified). Because a point of view becomes conventional among academic and literary scholars without military or political experience or responsibilities, It does not therefore become correct, even theologically. But It does become potent. What are the dangers in these new, largely uncriticized, tendencies dominant In Cathólic thinking? The danger of divi- siveness js obvious. So also Is the danger of politicizing the Church. So also Is the excessive clericalization of the Church. The Church does not consist only of bishops. But if the bishops make all the key political Judgments — rather than, say, the one-fourth of the U.S. Congress which Is made up of Catholics, or, even better, an effective national council of lay Catholics — how far have we really advanced beyond the Middle Ages? There la, again, the danger of ridicule, which (privately at least) fa well advanced. Finally, pressures to elect our bishops for their political views, rather than for their religious qualities, are bound to grow. The road that Vatican n political theology Is uncritically traveling may well end in disaster. Some think that the medieval error consisted In the btah- ops' habitually siding with their government. Will matters be improved if they habitually are adversaries of their government? By no means ought bishops — or any other Catholics — to be confined solely to the sanctuary or to "religious" subjects, narrowly defined. Yet on particular matters of social and political life, on matters of strategy and tactics, practical wisdom is the, appropriate Intellectual habit, and laymen and laywomen have the appropriate vocation.
