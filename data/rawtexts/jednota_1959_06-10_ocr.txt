The very existence of a refugee "class" or "status." can be attributed to the fact that present day society treats a person who flees his homeland to escape unjust persecution as a legal nonentity, said Msgr. Harnett The refugee is gi- ven. only the minimum in the way of international assistance, is al the mercy of local authorities, and more often than not is reduced to anonymity, ho continued. Msgr, Harnett said that while a refugee aspires to become a citizen of whatever country will grant ilm the fundamental rights of man and principally the right to work, it has now become easier to ship a ton of steel or a bag of rice from one country of the world to another than it is to send a- man from one place to another. This, he said, is due primarily to the fact that countries with protected economics fear that they will be invaded by peoples from less la vored areas of the world if freedom of movement were to become an established principle. He observed that those with experience in this matter know that all men love their native soil, the place of their birth, and the sights with which they have been familiar since childhood. They are driven to leave only by the pressure of religious, political, or economic persecution, and there is no need io fear| the invasion of stronger countries, economically speaking, by uncontrollable masses of refugees. Msgr. Harnett said six fundamental principles of human conduct must be reaffirmed by the free nations if the World Refugee Year is to solve what he called the greatest human problem of the 20th century. He stated: 1. Freedom of movement must bo guaranteed by a document that Is acceptable in all of the free1 nations of the world and that' Identifies a man without stigmatizing him. To be effective such a document must entail automatic recognition of his right to live and work where his own best interests dictate. 2 . The right of asylum must be recognized to the extent that It permits the law-abiding to gain the protection of the laws of civilized nations without restrictions. 3 - The right to work is little different from the right to live: and only the Creator who gives life can take it easy. 4 A refugee should not be so classed because he fits a definition Ho should be recognized as such if and when he renounces his allegiance to. and the protection of. the country of his birth. It follows immediately that the rest of the world owes him the right of asylum and the right to work. 5 Man is well on the way to conquering the last of his enemies in nature. Ther remains only for him to conquer his fear of other law abiding men. 6.
