Today, fust as in Tyrs's day, vigilant attention to and active support of our Czechoslovak culture is necessary if we intend to preserve our heritage. Since that meeting, many people sharing my desire to preserve those Sokol Ideals, as described by Tyrs, have offered their assistance and contributions to Sokol. By doing this, these concerned individuals have provided an impetus which has resulted in dramatic improvements to Sokol San Francisco during the past 2% months. The more visible changes range from the new "American Sokol Organization' sign on the Sokol structure, to a new VCR machine and monitor. The new sign, installed to increase the visibility and prominence of the Hall to visitors and potential hall renters, is a most welcome addition to the otherwise nondescript building front. The VCR equipment purchased will be an invaluable training and educational tool which will help the Furiant! Dance Ensemble and the Prostny (Calisthenic Drills) participants in their preparation for upcoming exhibitions. In addition to the sign and VCR equipment, a call has been put out requesting a donation of an answering machine which would be used to publicize upcoming Sokol events and to record messages or inquiries from callers. Beyond the physical changes within Sokol, changes in attitude of Sokol members and guests have been witnessed. As an example, during the past two months, an unprecidented number of Sokol visitors have requested Vestnik subscriptions; not to mention membership applications. These people apparently sense the changing environment within Sokol San Francisco and wish to experience it. Sokols, as well as Sokol visitors, have been expressing their growing support of our Organization. This is exemplified by increased donations to Furiant! and the Vestnik. Increased activity by Sokol members is another sign of exciting changes occurring in Sokol San Francisco. Prostny practice sessions are taking place Friday evenings. These take place in addition to the Furiant! Continued Next Column PAGE - 7 dance practice held on Thursday evenings. As an example of upcoming events, popular singer and composer, Karel Kryl, will perform at Sokol on April 22, 1988. In addition to this evening of music, one other special event is scheduled for Fall, 1988. It is also for the "elders" who have been with Sokol for years and years, but who will appreciate a "refresher course". Watch for additional articles in future issues. WHAT IS SOKOL? The Sokol ideology is based on the belief that mankind should strive for physical and mental perfection; healthy mind in a healthy body. The founder of Sokol was Miroslav Tyrs. He formulated the basic principles and led the movement for a number of years. From his early youth and into his mature years as professor of philosophy at the Prague University, Tyrs was interested in the Greek and Roman civilizations, and particularly in the relationship of physical culture to the mental excellence of the ancient Greeks. He formed an opinion that their magnificent achievements in literature, arts, and philosophy were the results of sensible up-bringing, which stressed the unity of mind and body, as well as certain basic values and rules which are necessary for the sound development of both. Like Darwin, Tyrs conceived the evolution of the species as a continual fight between the healthy and strong, against the weak and unfit. The rule applies to nations as well; a nation will perish once it has lost its vitality and stopped going forward. Neither its size nor traditions or past glories can stop the effects of this law. Apathy, weakness and decadence kill nations as well as individuals; the fight for survival requires strength — not only physical, but mental and moral as well. During Tyrs's lifetime, the Czech nation was struggling for its very existence, preserving only with the greatest difficulty its traditions and language. In order to help the Czechs to win this fight, Tyrs started the Sokol movement. The first unit - Sokol Prague - was founded in 1862. A period of rapid growth followed, during which Tyrs constantly strove to gwe Sokol its ideological direction and sound philosophical foundation. Most of his thoughts were embodied in a long article, titled - "Our Task, Directions, and Goals", whvch became a kind of gospel of the movement. It is difficult to condense the whole Sokol concept into a few lines. It stresses love of life and everything that is good and beautiful in it. The Sokol ideal is a person who loves goodness and adheres to truth and progress; A person who loves his nation and the whole humanity. A Person who willingly subordinates his ego to the needs of others and his group, knowing that from it stems much of his strength. He is a democratic person and believes in equality of all people. In the Sokol movement, a good human'being and selfless worker is valued above all others; judged by others not on the basis of finances or power, but on human values, on the deeds toward fellow man, for the group and for the nation. All Sokols are brothers and sisters to each other. Hate and contempt are unthinkable. The movement stands quite apart from politics; people, not programs are important. It tries to unite men and women of all progressive, political creeds and thus alleviate disagreements and frictions. This aim of complete objectivity and impartiality in regard to politics, has been rigidly and jealously maintained throughout Sokol history. In short, the movement — as conceived by Tyrs — is based on universal and eternally valid laws governing the behavior of sane human beings. It contains a blueprint for a better future for both individuals and the nation. It particularly tries to influence the youth, to form their characters, develop their self-esteem, and their sense of responsibility toward themselves and toward their natzonal group. It teaches them civic duties, decency, love of freedom, the principles of democracy. Sokol believes that by producing strong bodies and characters in its members, it does significantly add to the strength and vitality of the whole nation. Today, those ideas are as valid as they were 125 years ago. Since the movement was outlawed in the land in which it was born, it is all the more imperative for us here to keep vt alive, develop, enlarge and strengthen it. To carry on the Sokol tradition is a duty to our country and to ourselves. Jan Kucera Sokol S. F. [pageend15]SOKOL SAN FRANCISCO VESTNIK MARCH AND APRIL, 1988 PAGE The Precarious Journey of a New Idea FEW THINGS IN LIFE have a more uncertain longevity and a tenuous future than a new idea. In the 17th century Galileo was forced to recant on the theory that the earth revolves around the sun. Skip 300 years later to New York City where a new musical play, seeking financial backing, is rejected by professional investors because "whoever heard of a musical production in which a leading character dies or one with a real story and a ballet sequence?" But fortunes were made by the amateurs who backed "Oklahoma." Five hundred years went by before the ideas of Leonardo da Vinci for submarines, parachutes, multiple locks and airplanes were adopted. Alexander Graham Bell offered a fifty percent share for $500 in his proven invention that enabled people to talk to each other from miles apart. It has been estimated that an average of 20 years tianspires between the conception of a new idea and its ultimate acceptance by the public. A man may build a better mouse trap, but mice will probably be extinct by the time the trap reaches the marketplace. Human nature is responsible for the delay. First, there is the established resistance most people have toward any kind of change. Any new thought, new idea, new hypothesis represent a deviation from the status quo. Second, there are apathy and indifference, common characteristics toward anything not part of the present scheme of things. And third, there is the jealousy toward those who may gain fame and fortune from the new idea. Historically, the majority of people who have pre-conceived notions that a new idea is without merit are wrong. Recall those who scoffed at the idea of a Catholic in the White House. Or that compact autos would ever be accepted by the American public, especially of Japanese manufacture. Or that anyone would ever break the four- minute mile. Or fly around the world non-stop without refueling. Or that men would land on the moon. Because they permitted their own prejudices to come between themselves and history, people have been more often wrong than right in their predictions of failure. IS ANYONE HOME? As many of you may have already discovered, answering machines are increasingly becoming a fact.of life, expecially in today's business world. Much as I dislike dialing a number only to find a pre-recorded' message, or, worse yet, conducting a one-sided conversation with an answering machine, I have reluctantly concluded that these machines do serve a very useful function. This is especially true where a business or organization seeks to make itself accessible to its clients or members, but does not have sufficient personnel to maintain an office where telephone inquiries may be directed. This is the case with our own Sokol San Francisco. Because the Sokol Hall is occupied sporadically and usually only in the evenings, many telephone inquiries remain unanswered. The solution? Yes, you guessed it — an answering machine! An answering machine would allow us to provide callers with membership information and details of upcoming events, and would greatly improve communications between Sokol, its members, and the community at large. We are therefore asking members and friends of Sokol to search their attics and basements to see if they have an extra answering machine which they would be willing to donate to Sokol. Ideally, the machine that would best meet our needs will have a (1) double cassete system for the outgoing and incoming messages, and (2) a tone remote control. (If this "spring cleaning" is unsuccessful in unearthing any answering machines, we will gladly accept cash contributions to be applied toward the purchase of a new machine.) We sincerely thank you for all of your past support, and hope that you can once again give generously in order that Sokol San Francisco may better serve you. Sis.
