ISSN This year’s SVU General Assembly meeting was held in Brno, on the occasion of the Society’s 18th World Congress. The meeting which was called for 4 o’clock had to be reconvened one hour later because of the insufficient number of members present. At 5 o’clock, more than one hundred persons attended, probably making it the largest General Assembly meeting in SVU history. The meeting was opened by the Society’s President Dr. Míla Rechcígl who expressed appreciation to the Brno organizers and the representatives of Masaryk University for allowing the Society to hold its General Assembly in Brno. He noted that the meeting was a historical first, considering that this was the first time such meeting was held on the territory of the Czech Republic. After allowing a few moments of silence in memory of recently deceased Rafael Kubelik and Jiří Nehněvajsa and other SVU members during the last two years, Dr. Rechcígl designated Ing. Nevrlý as minutes taker and then named a three member Resolution Committee, consisting of Prof. Jiří Fukáč, Docent Rabušič and Libuše Paukert. Then the approval of the minutes from last year’s General Assembly meeting in Košice followed. A major part of the program was devoted to the Report by the SVU President Mila Rechcigl on the status of the Society covering the period 1994-1996. He noted financial difficulties the Society had found itself in at the time when he and other members of the current Executive Board began their term in office. Then enumerated a series of steps the Executive Board took in an effort to remedy the situation, including the preparation of a detailed analysis of specific problems and preparation of the future Agenda, published in the Zprávy SVU. Zahájení kongresu v městském divadle bylo velice impozantní. Slavnostní nástup akademických funkcionářů za doprovodu fanfár, oděných v historických talárech se zlatými řetězy a uváděnými pedely nesoucí žezla různých univerzit a fakult zanechalo nezapomenutelný dojem. Ambassador MUDr. Branislav Lichardus, DrSc. a Dr. Miloslav Rechcigl. 23 [pageend23]Dear Fellow Members: On looking over the list of contributors to the Dvorak Statue Fund, I notice a surprising absence of donations from many SVU members. We have been raising the money over the past three and a half years in much the same way, that the National Theatre in Prague was built in the 19th century, by public subscription, one crown at a time. Most donations have been small, in the $25.00 to $100.00 range. (We have been happy to often receive even less!) I’ve no doubt that these are amounts almost all SVU members can afford, so I can't help but be puzzled by the lack of general participation from a constituency that should naturally wish to see the successful completion of this very important project, one that is highly symbolic and tangible at the same time. Surely everyone understands the enormous significance of a permanent monument in bronze and granite, of the most important Czech composer in the premier metropolis of North America. (To most people New York is America!) Just think, the bronze countenance of Antonin Dvorak will outlive us all... Your grandchildren and theirs will be able to visit and recall that you had something to do with placing it there! It is time now to bring this project to a close. We still need to raise the balance required by the Parks Department of the City of New York for the maintenance of the statue in perpetuity. Your contribution could get us to the unveiling of Dvorak in Stuyvesant Square Park this year. And we will have our monument even before Prague does, where you can now visit Dvorak only in Slavin cemetery. The effort to place Dvorak in the park across the street from the house where he lived and wrote some of his most famous music, e.g., the New World Symphony, has been ongoing since the first check was received on 1/93. We couldn't save the house, so we owe him! Further details are available from Mr. Jack Taylor, co-ordinator of the Dvorak American Heritage Assoc.. P.O. Box 332, New York, NY 10276. Checks should be payable to the fiscal agent: Stuyvesant Park Neighborhood Association. Please be as generous as you are able and know that we will be greatly appreciative of any amount you care to contribute.
