And, we do so without editorial comment. DIRECT BILLING POLICY OF OUR SOCIETY By S tephcn F. Ungvara ky Executive Secretary In the past several years I have read numerous articles and heard a number of diasertations concerning our Society’s direct billing system. These articles and comments were at times prepared by Individuals who were not fully Informed nor involved in the day-by-day activity of our direct billing system. For this reason, I feel that it is up to me to set the record straight so that our Branch Officers and membership can be aware of our Society's policy in respect to direct billing. Let me begin with a brief history of our direct billing system. It was started back in the year 1068, as adopted by the 3Bth Convention, which established our Home Office Branch No. 999 under tlie following By-Laws previal on: "The Board of Directors are hereby authorized and instructed to forrrt and organize a Home Office Branch and to do all things necessary to operate and carry on its affairs and business. The membership shall be composed of such members who will not or cannot send dues to a regular branch; members who do not fall within the regular jurisdiction of an already established branch; and other members, who In the opinion of the Officers, should be placed in the Home Office Branch. The management of thia Branch shall be under the jurisdiction of the Executive Scretary.” In 1WB, the System was mechanized with the bills being printed off our IBM System 3 Computer. During the year of 1076, the Executive Committee, chaired by Brother John A. Sabol, President, developed the following guidelines to be followed In expanding our direct billing system: A. Direct billing was to be expanded on a voluntary baste. Voluntary from the standpoint that it would be the branch membership's decision to either bill their members directly from the Home Office or continue the list billing (ROZPOČET) branch collection system. . B. Priority to convert^-to the direct billing system was to be given to those branches who were unable to find someone to perform the administrative duties and the collection of premium function. C. The annual mode of payment was to be recommended and encouraged so as to keep the cost to a minimum for both the member and the Society. Because of cost,, the monthly mode of payment was to be discouraged. D. For cost reasons, the cash dividend option was disallowed and replaced by either accu- mulation at Interest or applied to reduce premium due. E. The branch Integrity was to be maintained with all branch functions, such as approval of death claims, cash surrenders, certificate loans, new and Increased contract applications, delivery of contracts and checks to all members as well as all fra- temai activities to remain the responsibility of the branch officers.
