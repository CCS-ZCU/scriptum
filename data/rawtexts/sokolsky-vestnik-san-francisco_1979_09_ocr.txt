Once again, the Labor Day Weekend is approaching fast and generally it means that it is the end of the summer. The vacation is over and the children will be going back to school. Gym classes will open and the life in our Sokol units will resume. We all try to start the new season with firm resolutions in many areas but the simplest resolution is to take the weeks and months as they come and make each day count to the fullest. As we enter another season, this may be the time for each of us to take inventory of the past months. There has been a widespread feeling that the acceptance of responsibility from one to another as well as to the community that the Sokol unit has been lately declining and degenerating. Not one thing could be blamed for diluting our strength as it is an accumulation of many things such as inflation, energy shortages, unemployment, higher and higher wages, taxes and prices on everything and crimes and problems of all kinds to solve. In the news media today, there is too much emphasis reported on the "BAD" side of things and there is never enough printed on the good things that are happening and done by the millions of human beings living in this world. We still have control of our own actions and we can meet challenges as individuals and in communities if each of us will do something just a little bit extra by giving of ourselves, of our time, energies and talents. We would like to see a new life and activity in our community and in our unit as it will bring much satisfaction for everyone concerned. As far as our Sokol unit activities for the new season (1979), this is a -very significant time because we will be celebrating 75 years of the foundation of Sokol San Francisco. To make this a really successful occasion, we need more cooperation from our members. The 75 years celebration becomes a milestone in the history of the unit and it should serve as a stimulus for new enthusiasm for its activities. Our main problem that we should solve is to increase the activities in the gym classes. The most important should be the start of the children's classes. Without life in the gymnasium, we'll become "a club"; not the Sokol. Perhaps a few guidelines to increase membership and attendance (listed below) may help. Remember, there are too many other things competing with our organization for attendance at meetings and social activities. (con* t.) If an issue is discussed, try diplomacy instead of harsh words. Fighting turns other members away and no one wants to feel uncomfortable during the dispute that could be handled calmly and tactfully. Many members loose interest in attending meetings where there are constant battles among a few. 6) Do give a pat on the back to those members who deserve recognition. Don't knock members down and do not spread gossip about them! 7) Read and use your monthly Vestnik, which gives you varied information and keeps you informed as well as in touch with the organization. Write to us about the happenings around you. Don't just gripe to a few friends but let US know your problems. 8) Try planning programs for a year ahead and use the Calendar of Events to list activities. Good programming with membership cooperation, can do only one thing: increase attendance. Let's all try to make the 75th anniversary of Sokol San Francisco an outstanding and rewarding occasion for increased activities for our organization. NAZDAR! Jan Kučera Educat. Dir. [pageend4]September 1^79 Věstník Sokola San Francisco Page 5 1879-RUDOLF FRIML-1979 The Czechoslovak rtmerican press forgot to coinmemorate the hundred anniversary of the Czechoslovakian composer RUDOLF FRIML. Born in Czechoslovakia in 1879, Friml is remembered as the famous composer of Rose Mary, The Vagabond King, Bird of Paradise, The Firefly. Although he died in Los Angeles in 1972, he had lived for many years in San Francisco. It is gratifying that the anniversary will be remembered in the Menlo lark Civic Center / Ca / with the production of The Vagabond King. ‘Sing-Alongs’ Help Singers Learn Friml’s Music “Sing-Alongs” to help interested singers learn the music of THE VAGABOND KING will be held at 7:30 p.m. August 13, 20, and 27 (three consecutive Mondays) at the Recreation Center in the Menlo Park Civic Center. The sing-alongs will be conducted by Ward Johnson, music director for the Menlo Players Guild production of this Rudolph Friml operetta, which opens the Guild’s 1979-80 season October 19. THE VAGABOND KING, which tells the story of poet and vagabond Francois Villon, will play through November 10, and is presented in celebration of Friml’s centennial. Open auditions for solo parts, speaking roles, dancers and singers will be held September 4, 5, and 6 at the Burgess Theater. The show has roles for 43 people and will be directed by Gene Coy.
