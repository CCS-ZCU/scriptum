Dr. Miroslav Tyrš The founder and the creator of the Sokols was Dr. Miroslav Tyrš. He was a man of excellent education, fully enlightened and of a noble character, who gave to his nation and ideed to the whole world the most valuable system of physical culture, coupled harmoniously with mental training, moral development and national understanding. The physical education of the old Greece, which he had studied intensively, was his model. He learned from personal experience the merits and demerits of the German Turner system originated by Jahn as well as of the Swedish plan oi Peter Ling. He created a bigger work—built on íirin, unshakable foundations. His goal was to bring the nation to a physical and mental efficiency through voluntary discipline. With the Sokol idea he coupled the love m the country, of national freedom and education for clbcuvc defense. Tyrš was born September 17,” 1832, in Děčín, Bohemia. In the University of Prague he first took up the law and, later, philosophy. He studied the natural sciences, physics, chemistry, anatomy. His favorite study waá esthetics. Through the study of the ancient classical authors he became a passionate lover of the old Greeks, particularly of their gymnastics, whose beneficial results he saw in the beautiful personalities of- the Greeks in whom there was in evidence their love of their country. He made a practical use of physical training for himself, and excelled in all branches of gymnastics as well as in the sports of his times. He was a teacher in the physical education institute of Jan Malypetr in Prague. He convinced the Czech patriots and enthusiasts of his day of the feasibility of founding a physical training organization in which, besides physical culture, there could be cultivated the spiritual and moral values of man on a national basis. Thus on February 16, 1862, through the formation of a physical culture society, there was laid the foundation of the Sokol, which grew into a world organization in comparatively a short time. Tyrš became its first chief and vice president. He created for the practical training an original terminology, to which no other terminology can be compared. In his book, The Essentials of Physical Training," he arranged the principles of physical culture science into an original physical culture system, which is called Tyrs’s system or Sokol system. This system is most remarkable in precision and effectiveness. He founded a monthly periodical, “Sokol." In his leading article, "Our Task, Our Aim and Goal," he expressed in a most brilliant form his idea as to the mission of the sublime Sokol Idea in the nation. Tyrš wrote many physical culture and ideological works. He devoted his whole life—full twenty years—until his untimely death—to his people and to Sokol. Besides these activities he was a professor in the university and the university technical institution, a writer and critic in art and esthetics and a deputy in the Czech diet. He was the leader of the first Sokol Slet in Prague, in 1882. He raised a healthy generation for his nation, as well as brilliant leaders—teachers—to carry on the work. After his fatiguing work he went to Oetz, in the Tyrolian Alps, to rest and to renew his strength. There he found his untimely grave in the wild waves of the river Acha, August 8, 1 884. His tragic end is veiled by mystery of the Tyrol Alps. He is buried in Olšany Cemetery, in Prague. [pageend1]Page 2. Historie je velkou učitelkou a kdo si nedá pozor, může snadno vyvodit poučení velmi a velmi pochybné. To se stalo československým komunistům v roce 1968, kdy se pokusili dát národu víc svobody a lidskou důstojnost. Na přelomu noci, na den 21. srpna 1968 těžká létadla a tanky varšavského paktu provedly hanebnou okupaci na Československo aby se ho zmocnily a opanovaly. Doma národ ví, že z východu nikdy nic dobrého na střední Evropu nepřišlo at to byli Tataři, Mongole!. Jeden Džigistán tehdy prohlásil : „Jako, že jedno slunce svítí - tak může být jen jeden Džigistán ! Dnes jsme v situaci, v níž se národ octnul v dobách po třicetileté válce, kdy svět se k nám obrátil zády. To byla doba, kdy se zdálo, že národ zanikne a že po něm nezůstane jiné památky, než záznam v knihách dějin a přece se zvedl z ponížení a dospěl k síle. V dnešní době je třeba buditelů, kteří uchovají odkaz, jenž ač zastíněn, spočívá hluboko v podvědomí národa. 0 tom svědčí události z roku 1968.
