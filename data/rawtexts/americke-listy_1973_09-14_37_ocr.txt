June of this year in order to contact certain members of the suppressed reform 'movement. Alexander Dubček and Dr. Frantisek Kriegel, two of the most prominent personalities of the Prague Spring, were not on the list of the visitors, but a dozen of ex- com munica ted. Communists were so honored by the e- missaries from Moscow. In al! instances, the same script was followed, to the letter. The agents tried, to obtain their unwilling hosts’ opinion about the Husák régime, the mood of the population and the events that would follow the withdrawal of Soviet armed forces from Czechoslovakia. The Czechoslovak non-per- sons mostly deemed to commit themselves on any of such questions and the Russians indicated that the "-new” Moscow policy disagreed with the political trials in Czechoslovakia and the attitude toward culture and arts. It was up to the hosts to draw up their own conclusions as to the future of Husák and particularly of the Party hardliners. In a few days it became clear that the Czechoslovak government was not informed beforehand about the visit by Moscow. This caused considerable paranoia among the neo-Stali- nists and several of the “political corpses” whom the Kremlin emissaries had visited, received summons for a “comradely talk’ from the Ministry of the Interior, i. e., from the secret police, who wanted to know everything that had transpired. Some prominent Party representatives exhorted the comrades to be particularly vigilant against d™.machinations of the rightwing opportunists’ and "deviationists”. However, the government is unable to prevent Moscow from following its own devices in Czechoslovakia, particularly if directed by the Moscow Central Committee which is now solidly in Brezhnev’s hands. It is possible that the purpose of the visit was to gather material against the deposed Ukrainian Party leader Shelest who is knoivn to be one of the most persistent advocates of the hard line against Dubček in 1968. Another feasible assumption is that the emissaries were gathering information that could be helpful to Brezhnev, in case the Czechoslovak question would have popped up during the negotiations with Nixon in the U. S. Another possibility is Moscow’s preparation for a compromise at the Helsinki conference, leading to a substantial pull-out from Central Europe. In order to retain tight control over Czechoslovakia, the Soviets are looking for some folk heroes who replace the present unpopular government in Czechoslovakia, as was the case of Gomulka in Poland two decades ago. It is extremely important to select the right man who would be malleable and subservient to Moscow at a price — the price being the present leadership of the Parly in Czechoslovakia. The Soviets visitors allegedly used the term “internal political crisis” when describing the situation. In view of the current political purges and which hunts in the USSR, one cannot expect that the Soviets would relinguish their stranglehold on Czechoslovakia or any other satellite state. Their economic and political survival would be unthinkable without the exploitation of other nations. Přesvědčil jsem se také, jak je poslouchán a jakou významnou roli hraje zahraniční rozhlas. A od té doby' se věci jistě dále progresivně vyvinuly. Nejen Rusko mocenské a imperiálni, s mnoha prvky' asijského despotismu, ale i Rusko humanistické a liberální, hlásící se k tradicím evropské kultury, hledající a žádající návrat do Evropy'. Rusko, které zrodilo Puškina a Lermontova, Ger- cena a černyševského. Rusko, které obdivoval Masaryk, a kterého si i my' vážime. Toto Rusko žije dál. Je blízké našim tradicím a nadějím. Není dnes ještě silné natolik, aby' mohlo způsobit převratné změny, ale je možno věřit, že mu patří budoucnost.
