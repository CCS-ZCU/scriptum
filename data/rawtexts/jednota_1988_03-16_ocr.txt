Supreme Chaplain, FCSU Holy Week is the culmination of Lent What we have heard in Mass homilies, sermons during Stations □t the Cross and retreats, all center upon the events of this week Once agam we hear the Passion of Jesus Christ read to us. the his- $ lorica! happenings leading up to his suffering and death. The very same crowds that cry out in today's Gospel, "Hosannah! Blessed be he who comes m the name of the Lord." will cry out five days later, "crucify him!" Knowing what we know today about the life of Jesus, it would be unimaginable for us to think that anyone today would cry out to crucify the Lord. Yet we do this when we mistreat people, neglect the poor, turn our backs on others, be- л come callous. For indeed, when we * are guilty of these sins against our neighbor, we have done these things against our Lord. We have "crucified" him again by these offenses. We cannot expect to become close to Christ when we ignore our fellow human beings. This is a contradiction. Jesus taught us the two great commandements are to love God above all things and our neighbor as ourself. The second is equal to the first. When we are concerned about our neighbor we are concerned about God. When we ignore the starving, the destitute, and downtrodden, we choose to ignore God. Jesus is quite specific about this in the description of the Last Judgment in Matthew 25: 31-46 Those who feed the hungry, give drink to the thirsty, clothe the naked, etc. also do it lor the Lord These righteous will go off to eternal life. But those who neglect these works of mercy. Jesus has something to say: ' Depart from me. you accursed, into the eternal fire prepared for the devil and his angels 'Amen. I say to you. what you did not do for one of these least ones, you did not do for me.' " If we are conscientious about our faith and wish to make progress in it. we will sacrifice our lime this week to attend the sacred liturgies of Holy Thursday. Good Friday and Holy Saturday. As we identify with the suffering and crucified Lord, we also identify with our fellow human beings who are suffering in a similar manner brought about by the evil in the world. Jesus was born Messiah for all and Jesus died tor all. Bethlehem speaks of the humility of love; Calvary shows that there is no limit to that love. May this Holy Week strengthen us to be more fervent in our benevolence and service Io God and one another so that the goodness brought about by the death of Jesus may be realized in a world that is desperate for his peace and love. Independence lor the Slovak nation became a reality at noon on March 14th. 1939. when 56 members ol parliament in Bratislava unanimously declared national independence This year marks the 49th Anniversary of the histone event that brought freedom to the Slovak nation and the emergence of the Slovak Republic as a free and independent state in peacetime before the outbreak of the Second World War The Slovak Republic received international recognition by more than 35 countries, including Great Britain. France. Sweden, Norway. Denmark. Italy, Spain, Portugal, Germany, and the Soviet Union. Under the presidency of Dr. Joseph Tiso. the Slovak Republic survived six years until the end of the war when the major powers arbitrarily withdrew their recognition and declared the Slovak state non-existent. This act was unquestionably a violation or human rights and the principle that every nation, no matter how small, has a natural right to be a self-governing state. Today the Slovak nation is governed by the Slovak Socialist Republic, subject to the Czech Socialist Republic in a common Czechoslovak state which is a satellite of the Soviet Union that has had its military garrisons and airfields in the country since 1968. Slovaks all over the world are keeping the spirit of freedom alive by commemorating Independence Day each year in the undying hope that the Slovak nation will be liberated to re-establish its own free and independent self-governing state. In 1924 the majority of the members of the St. Stephen the King Society. Branch 15 in Passaic. N J left the Catholic Church and founded an independent church. This was a clear violation of the Society's bylaws, since by belonging to a schismatic church one could not perform the required Eiaster duties necessary for membership.
