NEWS AND VIEWS A STEP IN THE RIGHT DIRECTION We hail the proposal submitted in the Senate by Senator Henry Jackson, democrat, introducing an a- mendment that would stipulate certain conditions on the trade with the USSR. We believe that it will not cut off the negotiations, because the Soviets cannot afford such a break. On the contrary, the trade with the US is a matter of life and death for them because of the dire facts of their economic situation. For that same reason, they wi’l be forced to accept the American conditions, by which the would-be emigrants from the Soviet Union would certainly profit. The treatment of Soviet Jeivs is frequently discussed and condemned by the free world, however, for them at least a possibility of emigration exists, while other citizens of the USSR and of the entire Soviet bloc do not have even such a hope. We do believe therefore that the Jackson amendment should demand unrestricted emigration from all the Iron Curtain countries, where stringent laws make it almost totally im- posivble for any citizen to leave the country legally. This drives people to desperate escape attempts — such as the recent case of three men and one woman from Czechoslovakia who had strapped themselves to the axle of a bus going from Bratislava to Vienna. . One cannot even imagine all the dangers and perils they had to live through, yet they were lucky. They reached freedom, but thousands of others who were unsuccessful in their attempt are languishing in prisons behind the Iron Curtain. Nobody should be forced to take such risks. Everybody should have a free choice of domicile. Another matter to consider in this connection is the reunification of forcibly separated f a m i l i e s. We know of numerous cases rohere the parents left Czechoslovakia after the Soviet invasion in 10G8 and ever since, they are trying in vain to gain the release of their minor children from. Czechoslovakia. Great as the plight of Soviet Jews is, the plight of these little pawns of ' the Communist regime in Czechoslovakia and of their parents abroad may be even greater, because even outrageous a m ounts of hard cash cannot solve their problems. The Communist regime is playing a cat-and-mouse game with the refugees and will not stop this hideous practice unless forced to do so. The lime to stop this immoral activity is now. once and for all. The Soviet Union has enough power over Czechoslovakia to bring about the liberation of such children, as well as to implement human and civil rights, in its entire bloc. The Soviets will not do this willingly, but under economic pressures applied by the Senate or by other organizations, such as the Longshoremen’s Union — they will buckle down. This would, lead to a real improvement of international relations between the two worlds. We call on the Senate to extend the demands on the Soviet Union along these.
