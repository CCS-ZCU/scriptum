[pageend1]Page 2 Věstník Sokola San Francisco October I978 OCTOBER 28th The month of October is a traditional one because we celebrate the independance of Czechoslovakia. This month will be the 60th anniversary of the new state. On October 28, 1918, a new republic was born in a land that had been underthe rule of autocrats for 300 years. The name Czechoslovakia which appeared in newspapers and on maps, was an unfamiliar one. So were many of the other names of new states and regimes that were formed out of the empires that had out-lived their usefulness and stubbornly refused to change to the new ideas. The beginning of a historic period, bent on self determination, a revival of the spirit that had created the United States of America and in Europe, the French Revolution. The leader who organized and led the last great charge on the bastions of a decadent monarchy was a philosopher. He was a man of the people; Washington and Lincoln in one personality. By his own efforts, through a long, hard life of working at 1 earning,studying, thinking and teaching thousands of eager, young minds, he became the greatest human of his time. His army within and without the borders of his country was an army of volunteers, an army of citizens in whose hearts and souls burned the longing for freedom. They fought hard and died. Many of them served under the flags of the United States, Britain, France, Italy and Canada but a11 of them without exception, were serving voluntarily with the same spirit and for the same ideals that guided the legions of Masaryk. This same land, the same people, after 20 fruitful years of democratic rule, was invaded by the forces of Nazism, the flower of its entire history.'A few short years of recovery were given to it when the forces of another philosophy, as foreign to its national character as Nazism, again put it into bondage. What then do we have to look forward to? Will the peace, prosperity, humanity and decency of Masaryk's Republic never return? As dark as the picture may look, we believe in the future. Nothing lasts forever. Our problems are no greater than those that faced humanity in the past. Throughout recorded history, every setback has set the stage for a new advance of mankind. We have, in spite of everything that has happened, a just reason to be proud. Our people had shown the world the ability and national character and established the nation that was such a g1 aring contrast to anything, that Europe had ever seen in such a short period. Europe and its politicians and systems are not ready for what they saw in Czechoslovakia and what we have here - free democracy and freedom. San Francisco contingent was also well represented. Fresno was participated by delegates to the District meeting and in the Board of Instructors meeting. My estimation would be that well over 150 people were there of all ages and a very encouraging sign was that we could see alot of children enjoying the outing. The Czechoslovak Scouts were sharing the same camping grounds with the Sokol and even there we had four boy scouts from our unit. As usual, the main activities was the concentration of playing volleyball and the evening campfires. Singing around the campfire wa» accompanied by six guitars, one ukelele and an accordian. For both days, many of the marathon singers kept going into the wee hours of the morning. Sis.Nekuda, President of Pac. District, called the meeting of the delegates on Sunday, Sept. 3rd, at approximatly 11:15 a.m. The main subject of discussion was next years 70th anniversary of Sokol Los Angeles and also the same for the Pacific District. It was agreed that L.A. will work out the program and will invite some of the Sokol Districts from back East. The afternoon meeting of the Board of Instructors was discussing more of the details of this same event. Of course, the Labor Day Camping would not have been the same without the volleyball match between Los Angeles and San Francisco. The S.F. team was re- inforced by one of L.A.'s best players (Radek) and they put up a terrific fight and came out on top by a score of 3 sets to 2. The volleyball match had a very good standard and was really enjoyed by the fans from both units. Unfortunately, with the joyful times there always has to be a little bit of sad ones. Someone discovered the mushrooms growing and before you knew it, the word spread and everyone was out searching for them. There were some good ones and apparently some bad and as a result two people suffered mushroom poisoning and were taken to the hospital for treatment. Everybody should be warned next time that if you are not sure about these mushrooms, do not touch them. It started to rain on Monday which was the last day of the camp and as a result, the camp was abondoned faster than we planned. But the rain could never wash away al! the nice times that we had there and I am sure that next year, the crowd will be even larger.
