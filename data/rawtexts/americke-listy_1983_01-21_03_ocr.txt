WINTERIZING YOUR SKIN By Judy Anne Cohen Dealing with winter weather — rough winds and freezing temperature can wreak havoc upon our skin. And rob it of moisture, for cold air holds less moisture than warm air does. What to do? Read on. Dr. JIM BARAL, New York Dermatology Center, N. Y. Your skin is more than just a part of you. It is you. And you need to be a skin vigilante, says Dr. Jim Baral, of the New York Dermatology Center, in order to care for it properly. The first rule of proper skin care, says the doctor, is “moderation”. That is, be moderate in exposing your skin to the elements and to any kinds of treatments. Winter has been given a bad name by some individuals, although the majority of us may actually enjoy the nippy weather. With that first snowfall, we tend to shed bright clothing, eat heavier foods. And, who doesn’t love a hot bath, or glass of wine by a blazing fireplace? Coping with winter weather is important, however, when it comes to taking proper care of your skin. Winter’s rough winds and freezing temperature can rob your skin of moisture. If, for example, the relative humidityJs 100 percent and the temperature is 35 degrees, there is still less water in the air than when the relative humidity is 100 percent and the temperature is 85 degrees. As a result, when unprotected skin in winter radiates moisture into the drier atmosphere around it — it loses moisture to the environment. And don't expect ■refuge indoors. According to Dr. Jim Baral, who is also affiliated with the Mt. Sinai Hospital in New York, “Indoor heat is like the Sahara Desert. Heat dpaws moisture out .of the air . . . and out of your skin”, says the good doctor. What to do? Buy a good humidifier if you’re working in an overheated environment. It will stand you in good stead. Similarly, a humidifier for home use will help return necessary moisture to the air and keep your skin somewhat protected. Using a good moisturizing lotion more often can also have some positive effect. But most important, warns Dr. Baral, is moderation. “Excess of just about anything can cause wear and tear on your skin — even fresh outdoor air. That ruddy outdoor look of the skier may not be all that healthy. Analyse that look as a skin vigilante would, and you see irritation and aging.” WINTER “GOOSE BUMPS” Have you ever wondered what kind of changes your skin might be experiencing when it’s cold enough to cause you to shiver and have “goose bumps?” When you go from heated indoor climates to freezing outdoor climates, fluctuating temperatures and bitter winds can create problems, not only with the surface of your skin, but with facial capillaries and the deeper layers of skin as well, says Dr. Baral. Therefore, wintertime directs that you care for your skin in very specific ways. Following are some tips from the doctor: Dryness and chapping are the most common cold weather skin problems. When exposed to low humidity, heat and cold, you lose a certain amount of that moisture from surface layers and your, skin may feel dry and irritated. As the water loss extends into the deeper layers, you experience flaking. One way to help prevent this is to bathe or shower well in advance of going out, allowing skin and hair to dry thoroughly. Showers and baths should be warm, but avoid hot water, for it can rob skin of its natural moisture. Always use a body moisturizer after bathing. Remember to dress accordingly. Gloves and boots are natural winter garb, but don’t neglect hats and scarves, since your scalp, face and neck are also prime candidates for winter skin problems. Wearing a lip protectant and an extra rich facial moisturizer are also necessities that should become regular features of your beauty regimen all winter long. Protect yourself from winter sun as you would from summer sun. Snow reflects sun and you can do just as much harm and get a sun or windburn in outdoors activities if you don’t use a sun blocking agent. Protect your skin in winter as well as other seasons of the year. So, do enjoy winter — whether you’re a sun lover, a true winter lover, an outdoors enthusiast. But remember, protect your skin by winterizing rt with proper moisturizing and clothing so you may enjoy the winter without paying a price by premature aging. It’s all up to you.
